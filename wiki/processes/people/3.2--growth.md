# Recruitment & Hiring — Growth (~50–150 people)

> At growth stage you start hiring senior leaders and C-level roles. The budget → offer → contract chain from [early revenue](3.2--early-revenue.md) still applies. On top of that: one person's "not a fit" should be addressed as a broken process— split the interviews, remove solo vetoes, and let all three rounds inform the decision.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Senior hires go through three separate conversations — and no single interviewer outside the hiring manager has a veto.**
  The hiring manager checks fit and chemistry. Someone one level up checks potential only (live case, whiteboard — not a final hire/no-hire). A long-tenure culture carrier checks values over coffee. Each gives a signal (e.g. a 1–5 rating); none can kill the hire alone, and interviewers know that upfront.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) | flags: needs practitioner check -->

- **The hire happens based on all three conversations — not when one person says "not a fit."**
  A binary culture veto from one interviewer is too subjective — that is not the question at this stage.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) | flags: needs practitioner check -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Run the three conversations in order — with different jobs for each round.** (1) **Hiring manager** — role fit, hard criteria, chemistry: did this meeting give you energy? (2) **One level up** — **potential only**: unprepared problem, live case. Output is a potential estimate, not hire/no-hire — **no veto**. (3) **Long-tenure culture carrier** — life, work, values over coffee. Also **no solo veto**. All three signals together inform the decision.
   <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) | flags: needs practitioner check -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry

- **A culture or values interview ends in a binary "not a fit" with veto power.**
  One subjective no blocks senior hires that the hiring manager and executive team would otherwise make — and the process stops scaling with you.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

- **You expect a specialist hire (e.g. CRO, CFO, Head of CS) to be friends with every other department head.**
  The bar is manager chemistry and values alignment — not universal friendship across the leadership team.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

## How this evolves next

At **scaled** stage, the same three-conversation pattern typically extends to professional recruiting infrastructure (ATS, pipelines, employer branding) — see [3.2 at Scaled](3.2--scaled.md).

## Tools & resources

- **9-box thinking (performance × potential × values)** — optional shared language across the three conversations, not a bureaucracy layer.
  Gives hiring manager, senior reviewer, and culture carrier the same vocabulary without a single scorecard veto.
