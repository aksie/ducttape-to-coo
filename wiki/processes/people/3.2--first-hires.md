# Recruitment & Hiring — First Hires

> Your first structured hires still need lightweight gates: agree the role is in budget before you post, and do not let managers send their own contracts. Full approval chain detail at [Early Revenue](3.2--early-revenue.md); signing authority under [4.1 Governance & Corporate](../legal/4.1--first-hires.md).

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Basic job posts with 2–3 interview rounds, scorecards, and reference checks** — plus an emailed OK from CFO that the role fits budget and a rough salary band before the post goes live.
  Interview discipline and budget discipline start together; neither replaces the other.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

- **Offers and contracts do not leave the founder, CFO, or HR filing owner without explicit OK.**
  No self-edited contracts, no salaries that were never agreed.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Before posting on AngelList, LinkedIn, or your network, agree as founders (or with CFO) that this role is in budget** and note a rough salary band by level (junior, senior, manager).
   One line in a shared doc is enough at this stage.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

2. **Run 2–3 interview rounds with different people; use a simple scorecard; always check references.**
   <!-- sources: processes.json stageFocus | flags: unverified -->

3. **Before any offer goes out, founder or CFO confirms the amount fits what you agreed when you opened the role.**
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

4. **Send the labour contract from your lawyer-reviewed template** — founder, CFO, or designated HR owner — not an edited attachment from the hiring manager.
   See [3.1 HR Administration](3.1--first-hires.md) for templates and [4.1 Governance & Corporate](../legal/4.1--first-hires.md) for signing authority.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

5. **Get the signed contract to HR before day one** so payroll and insurance are live.
   A handshake hire without paperwork is how people end up working uninsured.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry

- **A job post went live because a manager was underwater — nobody checked budget first.**
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

### Output quality

- **A new hire signed a contract the manager customised, with a salary leadership never approved.**
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->
