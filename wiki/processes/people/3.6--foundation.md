# Employee Satisfaction & Feedback — Foundation

> At foundation there are no employees to survey. "Satisfaction and feedback" here means **staying close** — especially between co-founders — through informal check-ins and, if you are more than one founder, a **weekly founder-only sync** where relationship feedback is on the agenda. Not an engagement survey. Not a HR process.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **You stay close without formalising.** Coffee walks, end-of-week "how are we doing," quick debriefs after a hard week — enough rhythm that small irritations surface before they become trust problems. No tool, no form, no cadence deck required.
  <!-- sources: processes.json 3.6 stageFocus, editorial -->

- **Co-founder teams have a recurring founder-only slot — not just "we talk when something breaks."** Weekly, 60–90 minutes, founders only: feedback on the **relationship**, strategy alignment, and the things you would not say in a mixed room. Agenda items get added all week; money and roles belong here, not in the hallway.
  <!-- sources: who-decides proposal Track B (Buechler), https://scaleyourself.com/the-user-manual/the-founder-sync -->

- **Decision friction is handled in the same founder circle, not left to fester.** When you disagree on direction or who owns what, that is feedback too — the full decision-rights framework lives in **[1.4 Who Decides What](../strategic/1.4--first-hires.md)** (founder circle, approval list). This page is the **relationship and check-in** side of that same infrastructure.
  <!-- sources: who-decides proposal — founder circle vs team-wide layers -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **If you have co-founders, block a weekly founder sync and treat it as non-negotiable.** Use [Amy Buechler's founder sync template](https://scaleyourself.com/the-user-manual/the-founder-sync) as a starting agenda — feedback, undiscussables, and alignment, not status updates you could send in chat.
   <!-- sources: who-decides proposal Track B (B2), https://scaleyourself.com/the-user-manual/the-founder-sync -->

2. **Make relationship feedback explicit, not accidental.** At least once a month, ask directly: *Are we still aligned on why we're doing this? Is anything between us going unsaid?* Informal is fine; avoiding the question until a blow-up is not.
   <!-- sources: who-decides proposal — founder circle warning signs (vision drift, no founder-only time) -->

3. **When the same fork gets argued for weeks, switch from "feedback" to decision process.** Name who decides, when you revisit, and close — see [1.4 Who Decides What](../strategic/1.4--first-hires.md) and the [who-decides proposal](../../../docs/proposals/who-decides-what-summary-and-direction.md) (founder circle + ABC map). Maintenance beats repair while you still like each other.
   <!-- sources: who-decides proposal — maintenance vs repair, closure section -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Founder / key-person time

- **There is no founder-only time — everything happens in the same room, the same Slack, or only when something is on fire.** Hard feedback waits for a crisis; small irritations compound into "personality clash" later.
  <!-- sources: who-decides proposal — founder circle warning signs -->

- **Founders are operating in different daily realities** — one is in customers all week, the other in code, and neither has checked whether they still want the same company.
  <!-- sources: who-decides proposal Track B (Lockett / vision drift) -->

- **You keep talking about the same fork without ever closing it** — another meeting, another Slack thread, another "let's sleep on it." Irritation builds; you wonder whether your co-founder will ever let something go, or whether you're being overruled without a real decision. Trust rarely blows up in one fight — it erodes in loops where nothing closes, and later you blame "personality" when it was really process ambiguity.
  <!-- sources: blog draft — Who Decides When Founders Disagree -->

- **The same decision gets argued for weeks** while a hire, a customer, or the runway clock waits. Roles were assumed from titles; you said you'd "figure out the process when it happens."
  <!-- sources: blog draft — Who Decides When Founders Disagree (Maintenance, not repair) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At First Hires, feedback becomes a team habit.** Add a simple **start / stop / change** question to your weekly team sync — three questions, five minutes: what should we start doing, stop doing, or change? Builds the habit of giving feedback before things fester. See [3.6 — First Hires](3.6--first-hires.md).
