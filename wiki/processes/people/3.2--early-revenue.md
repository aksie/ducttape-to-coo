# Recruitment & Hiring — Early Revenue

> Hiring at scale needs an approval chain, not just interview scorecards. Budget → approved role → post → offer → contract → payroll. Signing authority and templates live under [4.1 Governance & Corporate](../legal/4.1--early-revenue.md) and [3.1 HR Administration](3.1--early-revenue.md) — this page owns the hiring steps between them.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Every open role has a documented approval before it goes public: budget check, management agreement on title and level, and an indicative salary range** (junior, senior, manager, VP, executive).
  No job ad goes live on headcount hope — leadership has aligned on spend and level before candidates enter the funnel.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

- **Offer letters and labour contracts only leave the building after finance sign-off and from authorised senders.**
  CFO or COO confirms each offer amount fits the approved band. Head of HR or CFO/COO sends the contract from the lawyer-reviewed template — see [4.1](../legal/4.1--early-revenue.md) for signing authority and [4.5 Internal & Labour](../legal/4.5--early-revenue.md) for template discipline.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

- **HR receives the signed contract before the start date.**
  Payroll and insurance are active on day one — "hired" means finance has the paperwork, not that a manager shook hands.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Before posting a job opening, confirm budget and get management agreement on the role.**
   Record title, level, and indicative salary range on a headcount sheet, hiring tracker, or approval email. No LinkedIn or job-board post without that row filled in.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

2. **Run your interview process and scorecards** — but treat an approved role as a gate: if the profile changes materially, go back for a new approval before continuing.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

3. **Before sending an offer letter, get CFO or COO OK on the specific salary** within the band agreed at step 1.
   The offer is not a manager-side formality — it is the finance checkpoint. This prevents salary conflicts later; at scale, hiring managers often bias toward their preferred candidate without weighing budget impact.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->
   <!-- sources: human:aksie | flags: unverified -->

4. **Issue the labour contract only from the standard template, sent by head of HR or CFO/COO** — not a hiring manager's edited Word file.
   The signer must have documented authority per [4.1 Governance & Corporate](../legal/4.1--early-revenue.md).
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

5. **Hand the signed contract to HR before the start date** so payroll and insurance are set up.
   File in `/06-hr/employment-contracts/[name]/` per [3.1 HR Administration](3.1--early-revenue.md). A job opening is not closed until the signed contract is received. Keep meeting other candidates until the offer letter gets a positive reaction — candidates may still choose another company or only grasp net salary after seeing the offer.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->
   <!-- sources: human:aksie | flags: unverified -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry

- **Job openings are live but nobody can name who approved the budget or salary band for that role.**
  You are recruiting on manager urgency, not on a headcount plan. Huge red flag.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

### Output quality

- **Someone is "hired" on a self-edited contract with a salary that was never approved, signed by a person without signing authority.**
  Payroll, insurance, and diligence will all flag it — often weeks later.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

### Founder / key-person time

- **CFO or CEO discovers a new hire is working but not on payroll or insured** because the contract never reached HR.
  Retroactive fixes burn finance and leadership time under pressure.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Growth, formalise the chain in an ATS or hiring tracker** — approval fields, offer band, contract template version, and HR handoff status visible to finance and leadership without chasing email threads.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

## Tools & resources

- **Hiring tracker columns (minimum):** role, level, approved salary band, budget OK (Y/N + approver), offer amount, offer approver (CFO/COO), contract sent by, contract filed in HR (date).
