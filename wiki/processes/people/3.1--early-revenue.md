# HR Administration — Early Revenue

> At early revenue, HR administration must run without tribal knowledge. A shared structure, naming convention, and one owner beat ad hoc folders and inbox contracts.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **One person owns HR admin; every contract and amendment is findable within two minutes.** Headcount by role is current. A simple attrition log and disputes list exist. Freelance contracts live alongside employment contracts with the same IP standards.
  <!-- sources: src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Maintain a simple HR metrics sheet: headcount by role/department, attrition log (who left, voluntary or not), sick leave % from payroll, disputes with one-line summary and link to signed PDFs.**
   <!-- sources: src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry

- **Contracts and amendments still live in managers' email.** At 15–25 people, informal storage fails due diligence and creates compliance risk. The fix is a named owner and a single `/hr/` structure, not more reminders.
  <!-- sources: src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) | flags: unverified -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Growth, an HRIS (Personio, AFAS, or similar) often replaces folder-based filing — migrate cleanly with one designated source of truth.** Buyers at this stage expect org chart with compensation, dispute history, and works council documentation if applicable.
