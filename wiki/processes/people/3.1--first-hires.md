# HR Administration — First Hires

> From the first hire, HR administration is a filing discipline: every contract signed, centrally stored, with IP assignment and NL compliance obligations active from day one.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Every employee has a complete signed contract in a known folder; freelancers who touch IP have explicit assignment agreements.** Org chart is current. Standard templates are lawyer-reviewed and used consistently.
  <!-- sources: src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **File every employment contract as a signed PDF in `/hr/employment-contracts/[name]/` from day one.** Include non-compete, non-solicitation, confidentiality, and IP assignment as standard clauses — not per-hire negotiations. Add a short addendum every time you agree on a new salary, and save these signed with the person's contract as well. Payroll needs to match contracts in due diligence — verbal salary agreements lead to errors, internal conflict, and DD issues.
   <!-- sources: src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) -->

2. **Use lawyer-reviewed templates for offer letters and freelance agreements; store them in `/hr/templates/` with a review date.** In the Netherlands, [Penrose employment templates](https://penrose.law/downloads/) are a sensible starting point before counsel reviews. Do not draft one-off contracts for each hire.
   <!-- sources: src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) -->

3. **Track working hours for every employee from day one — keep it simple.** Dutch law (Arbeidstijdenwet) requires objective, reliable, accessible time records from the first employment contract, regardless of headcount. Keep records at least one year.
   <!-- sources: src-034 (Working Hours and Rest Times — Dutch Government) -->

4. **Designate a vertrouwenspersoon (confidential counsellor) and tell the team who it is.** Required under the Arbowet from the first employee — not only from 10 or 50 FTE. External providers are fine for small companies; file who you appointed in `/hr/` and `07-compliance/labour-relations/`.
   <!-- sources: src-035 (Vertrouwenspersoon — Arboportaal.nl), src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **Freelancers on Fiverr, Upwork, or similar platforms do not automatically transfer IP to your company.** Anyone touching code, content, or creative output needs a signed agreement with explicit IP assignment language.
  <!-- sources: src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) -->

- **Missing IP assignments from early contractors are among the most common HR due diligence findings — and among the hardest to fix retrospectively.**
  <!-- sources: src-040 (Practitioner contribution — HR Filing Structure) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Early Revenue (~15–25 people), informal HR filing breaks down — you need a system and a named owner.** Volume makes email-based storage fail. Light HR metrics (headcount, attrition log, disputes list) become worth maintaining.
