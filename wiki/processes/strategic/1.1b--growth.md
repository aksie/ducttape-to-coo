# Team Goals & Cascading Goals — Growth (~50–150 people)

> At growth stage, company goals cascade up reliably before they cascade down. Team goals should mirror the company split between running metrics and locally owned change goals — with slack for dependencies baked in.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Team-level planning mirrors the company split: running metrics from C-level, change goals owned locally, with ~70% of departmental capacity pre-booked on local objectives.**
  Cascade goals fail when departments inherit top-line metrics without local problem ownership. The ~70% figure is a practitioner rule of thumb.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) | flags: conditional, needs practitioner check -->

- **Cross-team dependency slack is built into departmental plans — not added as an afterthought.**
  Dependencies surface at scale; slack reserved upfront prevents gridlock when marketing, engineering, and legal need each other mid-quarter.

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **Information cascades up but teams execute on goals from a stale or partial picture** — upward articulation without downward transmission leaves teams on old plans even when leadership is aligned in the management team.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

- **Tech runs heavyweight goal machinery while commercial runs on heroics** — no shared planning language across functions.
  Misaligned planning maturity becomes visible friction around roughly eighty people — headcount will vary.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) | flags: conditional -->

## How this evolves next

At **scaled** stage, full goal cascade from company → department → team → individual with systematic dependency management. See [1.1b at Scaled](1.1b--scaled.md).
