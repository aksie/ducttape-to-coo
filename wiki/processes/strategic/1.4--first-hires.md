# Who Decides What — First Hires

> At first hires this is not RACI or SPADE — it is **founders aligned first**, then one visible list of what still needs founder approval. Everything else defaults to the person doing the work, with a named decider and a duty to consult before you close.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Founders have aligned on default domain ownership before imposing rules on the team.** An Areas-of-Responsibility pass (skill + passion per domain, not job title) produces "Founder A defaults on X / Founder B defaults on Y." Personal runway and founder sync belong in the same founder circle — see [3.6 — Foundation](../people/3.6--foundation.md) for the relationship rhythm.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), Track B (Ada Lin AOR, Buechler founder sync) -->

- **One visible founder approval list exists.** Hiring (see [3.2 Recruitment & Hiring](../people/3.2--first-hires.md)), spend above an agreed €X, and material pivots or strategy changes need founder OK. Everything else, the owner of the work decides — and escalates only when impact is unclear.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), processes.json 1.4 stageFocus -->

- **Meetings name a decider and a deadline — and the decider consults the room before calling it.** Light DRI, not a matrix: "Alex decides by Friday." The decider actively gathers input from affected people and relevant expertise — not a vacuum decision.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), Advice process, Coinbase DRI, Grove -->

- **Disagreements use consult → decide → commit, not endless re-debate.** For day-to-day calls (Bucket A in your ABC map), the domain owner consults, states the decision clearly, and the non-owner commits aloud with a reopen trigger — *"I've said my piece. Your call. I back it until [date]."*
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), blog draft who-decides-when-founders-disagree -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Run an Areas-of-Responsibility exercise among founders.** List domains (product, engineering, sales/GTM, ops/finance, hiring). Each founder scores skill (1–10) and passion; cluster ownership — if skill ties, passion wins. Output: default owners, not a CEO negotiation.
   <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), First Round Founder Dating Playbook (Ada Lin) -->

2. **Capture a personal runway snapshot per founder (founders only).** Months without salary, minimum income to stay full-time, full-time trigger — not company runway ([2.1 Cash Flow](../financial/2.1--foundation.md)). Shortest personal runway sets a company constraint on hiring pace, founder salary, and bootstrap vs raise.
   <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), YC Q7, Wasserman, Mercury cofounder prenup -->

3. **Write one page: approval list + ABC decision map.** Bucket A — domain owner decides after consult. Bucket B — discuss, then CEO tie-break at two founders. Bucket C — unanimous (equity, fundraising, hire approval, spend above €X, pivot). Show the team the Bucket C subset; keep the full map among founders. Template in the [who-decides proposal](../../../docs/proposals/who-decides-what-summary-and-direction.md#optional-team-template-first-hires).
   <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), Ascent ABC, Ramp founders agreement -->

4. **Delegate projects, not tasks.** Leadership sets boundaries; the team owns execution inside them — do not become the approver on every step. Practise letting go on reversible calls; keep founder approval on irreversible or high-stakes items only.
   <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), Shape Up Ch. 10, Jason Fried -->

5. **Before a fork, answer Grove's six questions out loud.** What decision? By when? Who decides? Who must be consulted? Who can veto? Who needs to know? Then: free discussion → clear decision → full support.
   <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), Grove High Output Management -->

6. **Codify decisions that matter within ~48 hours** per [1.3 Communication Norms](../strategic/1.3--first-hires.md) — chat announces, wiki/doc/ticket is authoritative. Decision rights and communication norms fail together when everything stays oral.
   <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), 1.3 first-hires -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Founder / key-person time

- **Founders become bottlenecks on trivial decisions** — every vendor, design tweak, or hire still waits for founder OK. The team learns to wait instead of act.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), HN Founder Mode Tradeoff (Kent Beck) -->

- **The same fork gets argued for weeks** while a hire, a customer, or the runway clock waits. Trust erodes; later you blame "personality" when it was process ambiguity.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), blog draft who-decides-when-founders-disagree -->

### Process entry

- **The team waits for founder OK instead of acting** on work inside their mandate — or hiring and spend happen **outside** the approval list you never wrote down.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), HN CEO micromanager thread -->

- **Roles were assumed from titles and never discussed** — or personal runway was never spoken aloud, so one founder stops or freelances after a few months and the other reads it as lack of commitment.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), Track B warning signs -->

### Output quality

- **The decider calls it without consulting** — the partner feels overruled, not committed. "Disagree and commit" used as cover for skipping input.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), Advice process, Wistia counter-school -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Early Revenue, name a decision owner per area and add escalation thresholds.** Founder gives input in meetings; the area owner decides — decisions should not re-open because a founder wasn't in the room. SPADE memos and formal RACI wait until growth/scaled or repeated cross-team conflict at ~15–25 people.
  <!-- sources: src-063 (synthesis), HSG seed article, reStruggle -->
