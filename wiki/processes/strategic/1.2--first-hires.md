# Internal Communication about Strategy — First Hires (2–10 people)

> At first hires the strategy story still travels by repetition in a small room — not by cascade machinery. The failure mode is assuming that scales without naming the shift.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Founders still tell the strategy story often enough to each employee, so that the early team shares one mental model.**
  Repeated narrative at this scale replaces formal cascade machinery — osmosis works when everyone is still in the same conversation often enough.

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **Multi-founder teams explain direction slightly differently to their functions** — creating unspoken frustration and uncertainty before anyone names the misalignment.
  Inconsistent strategy stories compound as functions grow.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

### Process entry

- **Internal comms is treated as separate from strategy and nobody owns transmission.**
  A practitioner view at this stage: internal comms is a part of the strategy process, not a separate parallel workstream — if nobody owns telling the story, cascade will fail when headcount grows.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) | flags: needs practitioner check -->

## How this evolves next

At **early revenue** and **growth**, the story must flow down on a planning rhythm — not just up through reporting. See [1.2 at Growth](1.2--growth.md).
