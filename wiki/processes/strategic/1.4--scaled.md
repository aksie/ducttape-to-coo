# Who Decides What — Scaled (150+ people)

> At scaled stage, founder intuition still drives the company — suppressing it loses the role. Channel it with process steps instead of smoothing it away.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Process steps wrap founder intuition rather than replacing it** — a shower insight becomes a staged test, not a company-wide pivot by Monday.
  Founder energy drives the company; the operator's job is to channel it, not remove it.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

- **When conflict rises, use mediator-style neutral questions to surface what is unsaid.**
  Practitioner-trained mediation helps; it does not replace willingness to reflect. Your team may use other structured conflict tools — the point is surfacing what's unsaid before it explodes.

## Warning signs you're behind

### Founder / key-person time

- **An operator tries to smooth all founder erratic energy away** — and loses founder trust or the role.
  The energy that built the company needs somewhere to go.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

### Output quality

- **Task-driven, low-empathy founders who believe the best facts win and will not move without full conviction stall a growing org** — fine for product decisions, brutal for organisational disagreement.
  Mediation helps but does not replace willingness to reflect. Patterns vary — treat this as one failure mode to watch for, not a personality diagnosis.
