# Company-Level Planning & Goals — Scaled (150+ people)

At scaled stage, planning is a maintained system. Each layer has a review cadence, and the risk is stale artifacts, not missing goals.

## What good looks like at this phase

Planning at this stage is less about setting goals and more about maintaining a hierarchy of artifacts that reinforce each other. The system should run on its calendar with minimal intervention.

## What you actually need to do

- **Assign review rhythms to each planning layer: strategy reviewed annually, annual goals reviewed quarterly, and quarterly OKRs reviewed monthly.** Planning artifacts become stale unless they have a maintenance rhythm. Review cadence keeps priorities current without waiting for a full planning reset.
  <!-- sources: src-025-gitlab (gitlab_handbook) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **Planning artifacts from last quarter or last year still look exactly the same, but nobody has checked whether they are still true.** Stale artifacts are worse than no artifacts — they give a false sense of alignment.
  <!-- sources: src-025-gitlab (gitlab_handbook) -->

## How this evolves next

- **The scaled-company evolution follows a pattern: calendar to hierarchy to maintenance.** First make planning rhythms visible, then show how goals ladder up, then review each layer so the system stays alive. This pattern emerged across GitLab's 2021–2024 planning evolution. At scale, the risk is not just lack of goals; it is stale goals, disconnected goals, and planning events that do not reinforce each other.
