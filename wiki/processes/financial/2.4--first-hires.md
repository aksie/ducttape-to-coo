# Accounts Payable & Expense Management — First Hires

> At First Hires, expense management is barely a process — but the habits you build now determine whether your books are clean or chaotic when it starts to matter.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Clear payment approval threshold from day one.**
  Everyone knows what they can spend without asking, and anything above that amount gets a second pair of eyes before it's paid.
  <!-- sources: src-008 (founderpath, sells_fractional_cfo_services) -->

- **An accountant engaged — not full-time, but on call.**
  Someone you can ask "Can our cash flow support a new hire?" or "How much should we set aside for taxes?" They should work with businesses similar to yours and walk you through the reporting so you can answer these questions yourself — they are an experienced extra set of eyes, but you need to understand it yourself to run the business.
  <!-- sources: src-017 (ycombinator) -->
  <!-- sources: human:aksie | flags: unverified -->

## What you actually need to do

- **Move all business expenses onto dedicated cards — no personal mixing.**
  Even smoother: founders and employees pay expenses (anything except SaaS subscriptions) on their personal card and only get reimbursed with a receipt. The cost of admin from a $20 coffee receipt with a potential customer is much higher than $7. At founder marginal tax rates (50%+), every missed deductible costs roughly half its value.
  <!-- sources: src-017 (ycombinator) -->
  <!-- sources: human:aksie | flags: practitioner_rewrite -->

- **Track every expense from day one.**
  When someone in the founding team spends more than an hour a month briefing the finance admin on cost categories, structure your expense categories properly — retroactive recategorisation is brutal and you will hate yourself later if you don't.
  <!-- sources: src-019 (zeni, sells_finance_concierge_service), src-005 (ycombinator) -->
  <!-- sources: human:aksie | flags: practitioner_rewrite -->

- **Get a bookkeeper earlier than you think you need one.**
  They'll pay for themselves within 3 months in founder time saved. Your job is to build the company, not categorise transactions.
  <!-- sources: src-006 (reddit) -->

- **Document your expense policy in writing.**
  At a certain size, add it to a handbook that people sign off on — but keep the tone in line with your size. "Just follow common sense and spend like it's your own money" works at first, but people are different and join at different stages, so won't automatically make the decisions you expect. A verbal policy doesn't survive growth past 15 people and fails diligence every time.
  <!-- sources: src-008 (founderpath, sells_fractional_cfo_services) -->
  <!-- sources: human:aksie | flags: unverified -->

- **Have your external accountant reconcile every card transaction monthly.**
  Match every transaction to a receipt and a category before closing the month. With proper tooling this takes less than 2 hours; if you skip it, uncategorised transactions compound into a mess.
  <!-- sources: src-009 (bench, accounting_firm_interest), src-008 (founderpath, sells_fractional_cfo_services) -->
  <!-- sources: human:aksie | flags: unverified -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **You can't produce a receipt for any business expense on demand.**
  The tax authority (IRS in the US) can audit specific transactions years later. If you can't find a receipt, you lose the deduction plus pay penalties. The remediation cost is far higher than capturing receipts from day one.
  <!-- sources: src-017 (ycombinator) -->

### Founder / key-person time

- **Multiple people charging on a shared card with no per-person visibility.**
  You find out what was spent at the end of the month, after the money is already gone. By the time the statement arrives, corrective conversations are awkward and the spend is unrecoverable.
  <!-- sources: src-018 (spendesk, sells_expense_management_software) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Early Scale (~15-25 people), expense management becomes a real process.**
  You'll need defined approval workflows (who approves what amount). The volume will be too high to deal with finding receipts in hindsight — either your quality drops and creates tax risk, or you spend too much founder time fixing things after the fact.
  <!-- sources: human:aksie | flags: practitioner_rewrite -->

---

## Tools & resources

- A dedicated business credit card (or small set of cards) — no personal expense mixing
- A simple expense log or spreadsheet if you're pre-tooling; Pleo, Spendesk, or Ramp once the team grows
- A bookkeeper (part-time or outsourced) engaged before you think you need one
- A one-page written expense policy: thresholds, categories, receipt requirements
