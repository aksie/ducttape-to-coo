# Privacy & IP — Growth

> At growth, IP and privacy move from "know your exposure" to active protection: register the brand, close the back door, and train the people in front of it.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **The first-hires baselines still hold: you know your IP exposure and your data, with reviewed policies and DPAs.**
  What changes at growth is acting on them — registering, protecting, and controlling access. See [Privacy & IP — First Hires](4.6--first-hires.md) for the baseline.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Spend on protection where your edge actually is — for most companies that's trademarks, not patents.**
   Patents are usually low value unless your USP truly depends on something specific; trademarks matter because copycats appear, so register your mark and keep all registrations current. An unprotected, easily-copied brand is a red flag in due diligence.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

2. **Close the back door (CISO work) and train the people at the front.**
   Most big leaks are human error, so combine technical access controls with awareness training. Cyber insurance almost always requires awareness training anyway — together they give the lowest chance of real trouble.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

3. **Set rules for AI use.**
   At a bare minimum, don't upload customer or employee databases into AI tools — treat sharing with an external AI as a potential data leak, even on a paid enterprise account — and ideally have an AI governance policy stating how and when AI may be used.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

4. **If you develop or deploy high-risk AI, consult a legal expert in this field.**
   The EU AI Act sets strict rules; for example, a recruitment tool that auto-screens CVs before a human is involved can be high-risk (discrimination risk). Building one yourself should trigger expert advice — a general scan can't resolve it.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **Privacy is copy-pasted terms with no internal access controls.**
  Borrowed policies are not enough — without measures so not everyone can access all data, inadequate protection risks an Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (AP) fine plus reputational damage, weighted by how sensitive your data is.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At scaled, privacy and compliance become a standalone function.**
  GDPR documentation, a current processing register, and an audit trail for an exit; the IP portfolio and licences are actively maintained.
