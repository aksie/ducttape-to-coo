# Legal (above certain size also Compliance) — First Hires

> The moment you hire your first employee, Dutch employment law activates — fully, with no startup exemption and no headcount threshold. Most of the obligations are easy to meet if you know about them. Almost none of them are obvious.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **One person owns the filing structure, and all employment documents have a known home.** Contracts don't end up in a manager's email. Every new employee's signed contract, with IP assignment and confidentiality clauses, is filed the day they sign. The founding documents from the previous stage are still current and findable.
  <!-- sources: src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure) -->

- **NL compliance obligations are active from day one of employment.** Hour registration runs. A vertrouwenspersoon is designated. There are no retroactive gaps to fix.
  <!-- sources: src-034 (Working Hours and Rest Times — Dutch Government), src-035 (Vertrouwenspersoon — Arboportaal.nl) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Designate one person as the explicit owner of the HR admin — including the filing of contracts — when you make your first hire.** This person is responsible for ensuring every signed contract ends up in the right place. Without a named owner, documents default to email inboxes and are effectively lost. The owner doesn't need to be a lawyer — they just need to know what goes where and do it consistently.
   <!-- sources: src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure) -->

2. **Start tracking working hours for every employee from their first day — but keep it extremely simple and pragmatic.** Dutch law (Arbeidstijdenwet) requires objective, reliable, and accessible time records for all employees, regardless of role or arrangement. Records must be kept for at least one year. This is not optional and not staged by headcount — it applies from the moment the first employment contract is signed. A basic spreadsheet or a lightweight time-tracking tool is enough.
   <!-- sources: src-034 (Working Hours and Rest Times — Dutch Government) -->

3. **Designate a vertrouwenspersoon (confidential counsellor) before you reach 10 employees.** Dutch law requires this from 10 employees onwards, but putting it in place earlier prevents a compliance gap and signals a healthy culture. If you don't have someone appropriate internally, an external provider is available and costs relatively little per year.
   <!-- sources: src-035 (Vertrouwenspersoon — Arboportaal.nl), src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure) -->

4. **File every employment contract as a signed PDF the day the employee signs.** Include non-compete, non-solicitation, confidentiality, and IP assignment as standard clauses — not optional add-ons negotiated per hire. Keep a standard offer letter template reviewed by a lawyer once, and use it consistently. The same applies to freelance and contractor agreements.
   <!-- sources: src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Early Revenue, the filing structure you've built becomes the foundation for transaction readiness.** The compliance calendar (tax deadlines, insurance renewals, trademark renewals) matters more as obligations multiply. The question shifts from "do we have a structure?" to "is it complete enough that an external party could navigate it in a week?"

## Context variants

### If you are bootstrapped

The NL compliance obligations apply regardless of funding status — they activate with the first employment contract, not with external investment. Hour registration and the vertrouwenspersoon requirement are legal minimums, not investor-driven best practices.

### If you have angel or friends & family investors

You are likely approaching your first structured due diligence. The employment contract filing discipline built now will directly reduce prep time for any future round. Retroactively collecting and correcting contracts is slow; doing it right at hire time is not.

### If you are venture-backed (seed / Series A)

Investors will review your employment contracts as part of formal due diligence — IP assignment clauses in particular. Inconsistency across contracts (missing clauses, non-standard terms, unsigned copies) is a material finding. Standardise your contract template with counsel before you're in a live process.

## Tools & resources

- **External vertrouwenspersoon providers:** Several providers offer this as a dedicated low-cost service for small Dutch companies. Contracting externally costs relatively little per year and satisfies the legal requirement immediately.
