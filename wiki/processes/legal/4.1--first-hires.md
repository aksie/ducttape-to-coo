# Governance & Corporate — First Hires

> Employment templates, NL labour obligations, and privacy/IP have their own wiki cells — **[3.1 HR Administration](../people/3.1--first-hires.md)** (labour filing, vertrouwenspersoon, hours), **4.5 Internal & Labour**, **4.6 Privacy & IP**. This page: corporate filing, signing authority, and commercial discipline.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **One person owns the filing structure, and all employment documents have a known home.** Contracts don't end up in a manager's email. Every new employee's signed contract, with IP assignment and confidentiality clauses, is filed the day they sign. The founding documents from the previous stage are still current and findable.
  <!-- sources: src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure) -->

- **NL labour obligations are owned under [3.1 HR Administration](../people/3.1--first-hires.md)** — hour registration, vertrouwenspersoon, contract filing — with no retroactive gaps.
  <!-- sources: src-034 (Working Hours and Rest Times — Dutch Government), src-035 (Vertrouwenspersoon — Arboportaal.nl) -->

- **Everyone who joins — employee or contractor — receives something in writing immediately.** Not "we are all friends, it will never go wrong." For digital companies, contracts include IP, confidentiality, and NL employment basics from day one.
  <!-- sources: src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk) -->

- **Anyone who signs employment or commercial contracts on the company's behalf has documented authority and clear boundaries.** Signing is not assumed from job title alone. The delegation covers which contract types each lead may sign, and the legal instrument (power of attorney or equivalent) is on file before the first delegated signature.
  <!-- sources: src-048 (contract signing authority, human:stefanverkerk) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Name an owner for HR contract filing when you make your first hire — see [3.1 HR Administration](../people/3.1--first-hires.md).** Corporate and commercial documents still need a home under `/01-corporate/` and `/03-contracts/`; the compliance calendar owner can be the same person or a co-founder.
   <!-- sources: src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure) -->

2. **Before delegating contract signing from the CEO to an Ops or HR lead, agree in writing which contract types they may sign and set up formal signing authority.** As contract work moves off the founder's desk, the lead needs legal power to bind the company (typically a power of attorney from the formal director) and clear boundaries — not blanket authority to sign whatever they want. Keep it lightweight: a one-page matrix of authorised contract types plus the delegation document, filed in `/corporate/` or `/contracts/`.
   <!-- sources: src-048 (contract signing authority, human:stefanverkerk) -->

3. **Employment contracts, freelance IP, hour registration, and vertrouwenspersoon → [3.1 HR Administration](../people/3.1--first-hires.md) and 4.5 Internal & Labour.** Hiring approval chain (budget → offer → contract) → **[3.2 Recruitment & Hiring](../people/3.2--first-hires.md)**. Use [Penrose employment templates](https://penrose.law/downloads/) or counsel-reviewed equivalents before the first hire.
   <!-- sources: src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure), src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk), src-060 (Hiring approval chain) -->

4. **Privacy and GDPR from day one if you collect personal data → 4.6 Privacy & IP.** Do not collect user data for a year and only then write a privacy policy.
   <!-- sources: src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry

- **You hired someone with no employment or contractor contract because you trust the relationship.** When relationships sour or diligence arrives, missing contracts create liability and IP uncertainty that are expensive to fix retroactively.
  <!-- sources: src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk) -->

- **You have been collecting user data for months without a coherent privacy policy, lawful basis, or technical controls.** Late privacy fixes often require product and architecture changes under time pressure.
  <!-- sources: src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk) -->

- **Team leads are signing employment or vendor contracts without documented signing authority.** Contracts signed by someone without legal power to bind the company may be invalid or unenforceable — and diligence will flag improperly authorised agreements alongside missing contracts.
  <!-- sources: src-048 (contract signing authority, human:stefanverkerk) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Early Revenue, the filing structure you've built becomes the foundation for transaction readiness.** The compliance calendar (tax deadlines, insurance renewals, trademark renewals) matters more as obligations multiply. The question shifts from "do we have a structure?" to "is it complete enough that an external party could navigate it in a week?"
  <!-- sources: src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure) -->

- **At Early Revenue, commercial contract signing authority extends beyond HR.** Customer MSAs, vendor agreements, and partnership deals need the same delegation discipline — extend the contract-type matrix before commercial volume grows, not after someone signs outside their authority.
  <!-- sources: src-048 (contract signing authority, human:stefanverkerk) -->

## Context variants

### If you are bootstrapped

The NL compliance obligations apply regardless of funding status — they activate with the first employment contract, not with external investment. Hour registration and the vertrouwenspersoon requirement are legal minimums, not investor-driven best practices.

### If you have angel or friends & family investors

You are likely approaching your first structured due diligence. The employment contract filing discipline built now will directly reduce prep time for any future round. Retroactively collecting and correcting contracts is slow; doing it right at hire time is not.

### If you are venture-backed (seed / Series A)

Investors will review your employment contracts as part of formal due diligence — IP assignment clauses in particular. Inconsistency across contracts (missing clauses, non-standard terms, unsigned copies) is a material finding. Standardise your contract template with counsel before you're in a live process.
