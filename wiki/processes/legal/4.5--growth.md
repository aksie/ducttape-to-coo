# Internal & Labour — Growth

> At scale the risk flips from "no contract" to "the contract no longer matches reality." Move employee records into an HRIS and keep documented terms true to what's actually paid.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **The first-hires baseline still holds: standardised, lawyer-reviewed contracts, signed and filed.**
  What changes at growth is consistency across many hires and keeping every record true to what's actually paid. See [Internal & Labour — First Hires](4.5--first-hires.md) for the baseline.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Keep documented terms true to actual pay, and move to an HRIS with a file per employee.**
   When the template is updated correctly but the salary on file drifts from what's paid, payroll, disputes, and due diligence all run against the wrong numbers. At scale, an HRIS keeps each employee's record accurate.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **The template was updated correctly, but the salary on file no longer matches what's actually paid.**
  A term gets fixed (e.g. 7 months → a year) while pay drifts away from the record. When documented terms diverge from reality, payroll, disputes, and DD all break against the wrong numbers.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At scaled, employment agreements run at volume with a structured disputes-and-settlements register.**
  HRIS migration should preserve contract history; works council obligations apply once headcount triggers are met (see 3.1 HR Administration).
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## Tools & resources

- **Employer of record (e.g. Remote.com) for people working remotely or abroad.**
  Solid, including Dutch payroll, and it removes the need for a local entity for a few people — but the per-head cost can get expensive quickly, so it is not the right tool for a whole Dutch workforce.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->
