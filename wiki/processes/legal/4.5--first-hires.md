# Internal & Labour — First Hires

> Get one employment contract checked by a lawyer before you scale hiring — your earliest hires are locked into that first template, and employee IP assignment is the clause most often left unsafe.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **You use standardised employment contracts, reviewed by a lawyer at least once.**
  Revisit them periodically — yearly is enough. Law and your own terms drift, so a periodic review keeps the standard template enforceable instead of quietly going stale.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Have one employment contract checked by a lawyer early.**
   Your key early hires all sit on that first, often flawed, contract and are hard to move to stricter terms later because of the personal relationship. Fixing the template early is cheap; renegotiating with people you now depend on is not.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

2. **For freelancers, make IP transfer explicit and add an indemnity.**
   NL context: under the *wet DBA*, IP does not transfer by default — state it. Include an indemnity so liability for false self-employment (*schijnzelfstandigheid*) can pass to the freelancer, and draft so the arrangement genuinely reads as freelance, not employment.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

3. **Store any changes to the labour agreement terms as addenda filed alongside the contracts.**
   So a due diligence reconciles — otherwise the salary report shows €90k while the contracts only sum to €75k. Unfiled side deals read as a controls gap.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

4. **Keep a good, logical folder structure for legal documents.**
   Most acute for employment, but it applies to every domain including commercial contracts. When you cannot find the current signed version fast, every later process (DD, disputes, renewals) slows down. See the [Company Ops Registry](../../../templates/rendered/company-ops-registry.html) for a folder layout.
   <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **Employee IP assignment is not actually safeguarded, or you rely on clauses that aren't enforceable for the contract type.**
  IP assignment is the clause most often left unsafe; if employee IP isn't assigned to the company, ownership of your product is unclear in due diligence. A non-compete, for example, is not enforceable everywhere.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At growth, the risk flips from "no contract" to "the contract no longer matches reality."**
  Templates get updated while actual pay drifts away from the record. Keep documented terms true to what's paid and, at scale, move to an HRIS with a file per employee.
