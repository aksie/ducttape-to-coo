# Privacy & IP — Early Revenue

> By early revenue, privacy stops being theoretical: the question is whether your data setup would survive a professional investor's scrutiny, or trigger a "rebuild first" before they invest.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **Your data architecture and privacy setup would survive scrutiny from a professional investor.**
  A clear lawful basis, data that isn't scattered, DPAs with your processors, and a versioned privacy policy — the opposite of duct-tape. Trademarks and domains sit on the renewal calendar. The first-hires baseline (know your data, reviewed policies) is now diligence-ready — see [4.6 — First Hires](4.6--first-hires.md).
  <!-- sources: src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **Your data architecture and privacy setup would not survive scrutiny from a professional investor — data scattered, no clear lawful basis, duct-tape everywhere.**
  Outcome is often "rebuild or we are not investing."
  <!-- sources: src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At growth, privacy moves from "diligence-ready" to active protection.**
  Internal access controls plus security-awareness training, a current processing register, registered trademarks, and explicit AI-use rules. See [4.6 — Growth](4.6--growth.md).
