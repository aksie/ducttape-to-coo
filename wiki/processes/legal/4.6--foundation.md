# Privacy & IP — Foundation

> At foundation, IP is the thing you can never redo cleanly: get all of it into the company at incorporation, and register the brand and domains before they lapse silently.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **A clean entity exists and all IP — code, brand, product, content — sits in the company, including work from the pre-legal-entity period.**
  Execute an IP assignment from founders as part of incorporation; not "mostly," not "except the first commits."
  <!-- sources: src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk) -->

## What you actually need to do

1. **Have every founder assign their IP to the company in writing as soon as possible.**
   This is a standard clause in a co-founder agreement or a separate IP assignment document. Without it, the IP legally belongs to the individual founder, not the company — which surfaces as a material finding in every future due diligence.
   <!-- sources: editorial | flags: unverified -->

2. **Register trademarks and domain names early, and record their renewal dates immediately.**
   Don't wait until you're bigger. Trademark and domain registrations lapse silently — there are no urgent reminders. Note renewal dates in the folder or a simple spreadsheet at the moment you register. A lapsed trademark or domain name is recoverable only slowly and sometimes not at all.
   <!-- sources: src-038 (Practitioner contribution — Legal & Corporate Filing Structure) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

- **After incorporation, meaningful IP, code, or brand assets still sit with individual founders.**
  Do not assume "IP never killed a deal" — this drives unclean cap tables and due-diligence pain.
  <!-- sources: src-041 (startup legal interview, human:stefanverkerk) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At first hires, the focus widens from owning IP to protecting data.**
  If you collect personal data, design privacy from day one — lawful basis, storage, and a DPA with every processor. See [4.6 — First Hires](4.6--first-hires.md).
