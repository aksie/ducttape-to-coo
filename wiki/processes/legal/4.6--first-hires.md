# Privacy & IP — First Hires

> Two early baselines: know whether your value rests on proprietary IP, and know what personal data you process — with reviewed policies and processor agreements in place.

## What good looks like at this stage

- **You know whether your value and competitive position rest on proprietary IP.**
  Brand, design, software, invention, trade secret — that answer determines how much of the rest of the IP work matters for you, so IP effort stays proportional to how much your edge actually comes from it.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

- **You know what personal data you process, your policies are reviewed, and processors are under agreement.**
  You can say whether you handle sensitive data (health, financial, biometric); your privacy and cookie policies are up to date, public and legally reviewed; and you have a data processing agreement with every third party that processes personal data on your behalf.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (Trailhead legal scan, human:trailhead-legal) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At growth, this moves from "know your exposure" to active protection.**
  Register trademarks, decide where your software IP lives, add internal access controls plus awareness training, and set explicit rules for AI use. See [Privacy & IP — Growth](4.6--growth.md).
