## What this process is (and isn't)

**1.4 — Who Decides What** is about **decision rights**: who can decide without asking, what must escalate, and when founders stop being the default approver.

At 2–10 people this is **not** RACI, SPADE, or a GitLab-scale DRI handbook. It is:

1. **Founder circle (soft)** — align among founders on domains, energy, and constraints *before* imposing rules on the wider team.
2. **Team-wide (harder)** — one visible list of what still needs founder approval; everything else defaults to the person doing the work.

Practitioners disagree on how early to delegate, how much to write down, and whether founders should stay hands-on (“founder mode”) or name single deciders early. **Schools** below capture those camps; the wiki default is a **middle ground**, not a single right answer.

## Two layers @ first hires (recommended wiki structure)

| Layer | What | When |
|-------|------|------|
| **1. Founder circle** | Three axes: (a) skill + passion, (b) personal runway & life context, (c) recurring founder sync → default domain ownership among founders | Before or alongside the team-facing list; stays relevant while the deciding core is mostly founders |
| **2. Team-wide** | Written approval list: hiring, spend above €X, pivots/strategy → founder OK; rest → hire with mandate | Once you have non-founder hires or repeated “who do I ask?” friction |

**Order matters:** founders align **with each other** first; then publish what the **team** must escalate. Not the reverse.

---

## Why this matters (the fork, stall, and trust)

Founders will hit moments where one thinks: *I'm not convinced this is the best route.* That's normal.

What's expensive is **talking without taking the fork** — another meeting, another Slack thread, another "let's sleep on it." Sometimes that's wisdom. Often it's stall; stall is often worse than a reversible wrong turn.

**Relationship cost:** irritation builds; you wonder if your co-founder will ever let something go, or if you're being overruled without a real decision. Trust rarely blows up in one fight — it erodes in loops where nothing closes. Later you blame "personality" when it was **process ambiguity**.

**Maintenance beats repair:** agree rules while you still like each other, not three weeks into the same argument.

## What to decide at each stage (and the schools)

For each decision: **School A** · **School B** · **Middle ground**.

### Foundation (0–1) — founders only; conversation before framework

`processes.json` today: *“Founders decide everything together. No framework needed.”* Discovery suggests **soft alignment still belongs here** — especially multi-founder teams — even when there is no team to delegate to.

| Decide | School A | School B | Middle |
|--------|----------|----------|--------|
| **Do we need written decision rules?** | No — oral, trust, small | Yes — one page from day one | **Oral until first non-founder hire or repeated re-litigation; then write the approval list** |
| **How founders split domains** | By title (CEO/CTO) | By negotiation after conflict | **AOR exercise:** skill 1–10 + passion per domain; cluster ownership; passion breaks ties ([Ada Lin](https://review.firstround.com/the-founder-dating-playbook-heres-the-process-i-used-to-find-my-co-founder/)) |
| **Personal financial context** | Taboo — “we’re all in” | Full disclosure before partnering | **Per founder: months without salary, minimum income, full-time trigger** ([YC Q7](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/10-questions-to-discuss-with-a-potential-co-founder/)); shortest personal runway = company constraint |
| **Company path (bootstrap vs raise)** | Bootstrap as long as possible | Raise early for speed | **Explicit constraint from personal runway + company cash ([2.1](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#2.1--foundation))** — not assumed from titles |
| **Founder overlap** | Clean silos (tech vs business) | Full overlap on everything | **Complementarity with overlap** — founders stay literate in each other’s domains ([HN 43818415](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=43818415)) |
| **Recurring founder ritual** | Ad hoc | Weekly founder sync + monthly partnership check-in | **Weekly 60–90 min founder-only sync** ([Amy Buechler](https://scaleyourself.com/the-user-manual/the-founder-sync)); money/roles on the agenda, not in the hallway |

**Open design question:** Should foundation get a **light wiki page** (currently `future` in `processes.json`) for founder-circle content, or should first-hires §1 subsume it with a “still just founders” note?

### First hires (2–10) — approval list + practise letting go

| Decide | School A | School B | Middle |
|--------|----------|----------|--------|
| **Default decision mode** | Founder still approves most things | Delegate aggressively from hire #1 | **Short written list of founder-only items; everything else → owner of the work** |
| **What’s on the founder approval list** | Long (tools, vendors, design) | Minimal (cap table, bank) | **Hiring ([3.2](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#3.2--first-hires)), spend above €X, pivots/strategy** — tune €X to your stage |
| **How non-founders get mandate** | Implicit (“you’re the hire, you decide”) | Explicit per project | **Delegate projects, not tasks** — leadership sets boundaries; team owns execution ([Shape Up Ch. 10](https://basecamp.com/shapeup/3.1-chapter-10)) |
| **Naming a decider in meetings** | Consensus until agreement | One DRI per decision, said aloud | **Light DRI call-out** — “Alex decides by Friday”; no matrix |
| **Founder mode** | Stay hands-on on product/engineering | Step back immediately | **Keep founder approval on irreversible/high-stakes; practise letting go on reversible calls** ([Kent Beck thread](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41464524)) |
| **CEO role** | CEO decides everything | Co-CEOs / no hierarchy | **CEO owns external face + tie-breaks**; domains otherwise by AOR, not title ([YC Second Job CEO](https://blog.ycombinator.com/the-second-job-of-a-startup-ceo/)) |
| **Personal runway (ongoing)** | Set once at founding | Revisit when hiring/spend changes | **Revisit when adding payroll or first hire** — asymmetry causes “why are we raising now?” fights ([Wasserman](https://hbr.org/2008/02/the-founders-dilemma)) |
| **Written artefact** | Slack pin | Notion handbook | **One page in wiki/shared doc** — approval list + founder default owners; link to [1.3](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#1.3--first-hires) for codifying decisions |

**Triggers for more than oral rules:** first non-founder hire, remote team, repeated “waiting for founder OK,” hiring without [3.2](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#3.2--first-hires) chain.

**Defer to early-revenue:** budget thresholds by area, SPADE-style memos, formal RACI.

| Decide | School A | School B | Middle |

## Founder circle — three axes (detail for Phase 2)

### Axis 1 — Skill + passion (not job title)

**Mechanism:** Areas of Responsibility table — domains e.g. product, engineering, sales, ops, finance, hiring. Each founder scores **skill (1–10)** and **passion (none → high)**. Cluster ownership; if skill ties, passion wins. Output: “Founder A defaults on X / Founder B defaults on Y” — rough roles, not a CEO negotiation.

**Schools:**

| School | Position |
|--------|----------|
| **Title-based** | CEO/CTO split — fast, often wrong after PMF |
| **Passion-based** | Energy drives ownership — risk of ignoring skill gaps |
| **Company-needs-first** | Build role map from what the company needs; plug founders in ([Buechler / Seibel](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/amy-buechler-and-michael-seibel-on-founder-coaching-and-having-hard-conversations)) |
| **Middle (wiki)** | AOR exercise + company-needs check — revisit after first non-founder hires |

### Axis 2 — Personal runway & life context

**Not company runway.** Per founder:

- Months without salary (savings ÷ fixed costs)
- Partner income / family support / debt
- Minimum salary for full-time commitment
- Full-time trigger (revenue €X, funding €Y, or date)

**Why it belongs in 1.4:** Unequal pressure distorts *who pushes for which decisions* — premature monetisation, bad funding terms, freelance on the side read as “lack of commitment,” fights over small shared expenses ([Wasserman asymmetry](https://www.noamwasserman.com/founding-with-friends-founding-with-strangers/), [Mercury cofounder prenup](https://mercury.com/blog/cofounder-prenup-money-questions)).

**Archetypes (context, not prescription):** frugal / low fixed costs; partner income; moonlighter (day job); personal debt — [FutureSight](https://futuresight.ventures/7-ways-founders-fund-their-personal-runway/).

**Rule:** Shortest personal runway sets a **company constraint** (hiring pace, founder salary, sales pressure, bootstrap vs raise) — name it, don’t treat it as weakness.

**Cross-ref:** Written baseline for money in and scenarios → **4.1 foundation**; company cash → **2.1**.

### Axis 3 — Founder sync (conversation infrastructure)

| Ritual | Cadence | Purpose |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Founder sync** | Weekly 60–90 min, founders only | Feedback, strategy alignment, relationship — agenda filled all week ([Buechler template](https://scaleyourself.com/the-user-manual/the-founder-sync)) |
| **Partnership check-in** | Monthly 30 min | Domain ownership, “undiscussables,” decision rules — separate from sprint/board ([Munson](https://www.mattmunson.me/how_to_resolve_cofounder_conflict/)) |
| **Hard conversation format** | As needed | Speaker–listener (5 min uninterrupted, reformulate); I-statements; scheduled not hallway ([Riescher](https://sixtineriescher.substack.com/p/the-anatomy-of-founder-conflict-and)) |

## Getting to closure when founders disagree

*Practitioner default (wiki + blog): **right to decide → duty to consult → then close.** “Disagree and commit” is the closing step — not the whole model. If consultation was skipped, commit feels like being overruled.*

### The pattern (middle-ground wiki default)

**Phase 1 — Right + duty (decider)**

1. **Name the decider upfront** — domain owner, or CEO on cross-domain / tie-break ([Elad Gil](https://blog.eladgil.com/p/unequal-cofounders), ABC Bucket A/B).
2. **Decider actively gathers input** — affected founders, relevant expertise; not a vacuum decision ([Advice process](https://reinventingorganizationswiki.com/en/theory/decision-making/), [Coinbase on DRIs](https://medium.com/the-coinbase-blog/coinbase-is-a-product-led-company-d8f03549f498): *“does not mean one person decides in a vacuum”* — responsible for **collecting input** then final call).
3. **Grove six questions before the fork** ([High Output Management summary](https://tyastunggal.com/p/high-output-management-by-andy-grove)): What decision? By when? Who decides? **Who must be consulted?** Who can veto? Who needs to know?

**Phase 2 — Discussion**

4. **Free discussion** — dissent required, not polite silence (Grove: junior speaks first to avoid pile-on).
5. **Understand, not just disagree** — until “reasonable people could disagree” ([Frances Frei](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/francesfrei_conflict-officehours-activity-7363228447105511424-zVNx): Disagree → **Understand** → Commit).

**Phase 3 — Close**

6. **Clear decision** — decider states it explicitly (Grove: free discussion → **clear decision** → full support).
7. **Commit aloud** — non-owner pledges support (SPADE **commitment meeting** — [Rajaram / First Round](https://review.firstround.com/square-defangs-difficult-decisions-with-this-system-heres-how/)); Bezos *“gamble with me on it?”* assumes debate already happened.
8. **Freeze + reopen trigger** — date, metric, or milestone ([Clarityframe PACT](https://clarityframe.io/briefs/when-founders-disagree-resolution-framework/)).

**Founder-circle script (informal — after consult, not instead of it):**  
*“Your call. I've said my piece. I'm not convinced, but I don't need to be. We go with your view until [date / milestone]. I won't relitigate in Slack.”*

### Literature map (consult → decide → commit)

| Model | Core idea | Link |
|-------|-----------|------|
| **Advice process** | Decide after advice from affected + experts; voice not vote | [RO wiki](https://reinventingorganizationswiki.com/en/theory/decision-making/), [Laloux summary PDF](https://www.dynamisk-netvaerk.dk/wp-content/uploads/2020/08/Reinventing-Organizations-complete-summary-v6-copy.pdf) |
| **DRI** (Apple → Coinbase) | One person collects all relevant input, then decides | [Coinbase blog](https://medium.com/the-coinbase-blog/coinbase-is-a-product-led-company-d8f03549f498) |
| **Grove** | Consult → clear decision → full support; six pre-questions | [Summary](https://tyastunggal.com/p/high-output-management-by-andy-grove) |
| **Bain RAPID** | **Input** (consulted, no veto) + **Decide** (single accountable) | [Bain](https://www.bain.com/insights/rapid-decision-making/), [Bridgespan PDF](https://www.bridgespan.org/getmedia/34fd3f75-3067-4162-ba86-5707a365aee0/RAPIDDecisionMaking.pdf) |
| **SPADE** | Consult → decide → commitment meeting | [First Round](https://review.firstround.com/square-defangs-difficult-decisions-with-this-system-heres-how/) |

Rajaram: *“What is important isn't that everyone agrees, it's that everyone is listened to. And then the right person makes a decision.”*

### Sources agree / differ (summary)

**Agree:**

- One owner; owner must **actively gather input** (Advice process, DRI, Grove, RAPID).
- Consensus everywhere = no ownership ([Rajaram](https://review.firstround.com/square-defangs-difficult-decisions-with-this-system-heres-how/)).
- Decide process before the fight ([YC Q10](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/10-questions-to-discuss-with-a-potential-co-founder/), [Munson](https://www.mattmunson.me/10-questions-every-co-founder-team-should-discuss/)).
- Domain owners day-to-day; unanimity existential (Ascent, Ramp, Mercury — see ABC below).
- Personal runway constrains which forks are affordable ([YC Q7](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/10-questions-to-discuss-with-a-potential-co-founder/), [Wasserman](https://hbr.org/2008/02/the-founders-dilemma)).
- Quarterly ABC map review ([Ascent](https://ascentincubator.com/legal-administrative/founders-agreements-the-founder-prenup-that-prevents-cofounder-conflict-and-speeds-up-mvp-execution/)).

**Differ:**

| Camp | Position |
|------|----------|
| **Consult → decide → commit** (Advice process, DRI, Grove, RAPID, SPADE) | Input duty first; commit is the close |
| **Disagree and commit** (Bezos soundbite) | Closing ask after debate — harsh if consultation skipped |
| **Passion partnership** ([Wistia](https://wistia.com/learn/culture/founder-partnerships-that-last)) | Find real buy-in; don't commit to what you don't believe |
| **Unequal clarity** ([Elad Gil](https://blog.eladgil.com/p/unequal-cofounders)) | CEO can decide across overlapping domains |
| **Lightweight buckets** (Ascent, Ramp) | One-page A/B/C; don't over-lawyer @ MVP |

**Wiki default:** buckets + consult/decide/commit in A and B; unanimity in C; quarterly refresh.

### Schools (preserve in wiki, like 1.3)

| School | Position | Risk |
|--------|----------|------|
| **Consensus required** | No move until both fully agree | Gridlock; silent “no”; relationship erosion ([Clarityframe](https://clarityframe.io/briefs/when-founders-disagree-resolution-framework/)) |
| **Consult → decide → commit** | Named decider + input duty → close with support | Skipped consult → feels authoritarian |
| **Disagree and commit (label only)** | Bezos closing ask after debate | Used as excuse to skip consultation |
| **Domain owner decides** | AOR cluster owner has final call in Bucket A | Breaks on cross-domain without Bucket B |
| **CEO tie-break** | CEO on Bucket B deadlock ([Munson](https://www.mattmunson.me/10-questions-every-co-founder-team-should-discuss/)) | CEO overuse → resentment |
| **Passion partnership (anti-school)** | Don't commit without belief ([Wistia](https://wistia.com/learn/culture/founder-partnerships-that-last)) | Slower; some 2-founder creative pairs |
| **Middle (wiki)** | ABC map + consult duty + understand/commit close + reopen trigger |

### Pre-agree the process (before you need it)

| Question | Answer now, not mid-fight |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Who decides when we disagree? | Domain owner → CEO tie-break → advisor ([YC Q10](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/10-questions-to-discuss-with-a-potential-co-founder/), [Munson](https://www.mattmunson.me/10-questions-every-co-founder-team-should-discuss/)) |
| Is this principle or preference? | Principles need unanimity or exit; preferences defer to domain owner ([Clarityframe](https://clarityframe.io/briefs/when-founders-disagree-resolution-framework/)) |
| Is this reversible? | Two-way door → disagree-and-commit OK for 4–12 weeks; one-way door → take longer, maybe unanimous ([HSG seed](https://www.hsgcap.com/article/how-startups-build-decision-systems/)) |
| What’s the reopen trigger? | Date, metric, or customer milestone — not “when I still feel right” |

### ABC bucket model — who decides what (emphasis)

*The most portable artefact from founder-agreement literature for 1.4: not a legal contract, but a **one-page decision map** founders fill in together. Sources: [Ascent Incubator](https://ascentincubator.com/legal-administrative/founders-agreements-the-founder-prenup-that-prevents-cofounder-conflict-and-speeds-up-mvp-execution/) (explicit A/B/C), [Ramp founders agreement](https://ramp.com/blog/what-is-in-a-startup-founder-agreement), [Mercury cofounder prenup](https://mercury.com/blog/cofounder-prenup-money-questions), [Equity Matrix template](https://equitymatrix.io/blog/co-founder-agreement-template), HBS founders-agreements framing (roles, rights, commitments, contingencies — cited via Ascent).*

**Core idea:** Every decision type is pre-sorted into one of three buckets. In a disagreement, you don’t renegotiate the process — you look up the bucket and apply the rule. **Bucket A is the advice-process zone:** owner decides, owner consults, partner commits once heard — not “decide alone,” not “consensus forever.”

| Bucket | Rule | What it’s for |
|--------|------|---------------|
| **A — Domain owner decides** | One named founder: **duty to consult** + final call; partner commits after heard (see script above) | Speed; daily execution; reversible calls |
| **B — Discuss, then decide** | Founders align in conversation; if still split → **majority** (3+ founders) or **CEO tie-break** (2 founders) | Cross-domain or shared bets; not existential |
| **C — Unanimous** | All founders must agree; no override | Company identity, cap table, survival |

#### Bucket A — examples (one founder decides)

*Ascent: “day-to-day execution choices in their area.” Ramp: “day-to-day operational calls sit with the relevant domain lead, without requiring group approval.”*

| Domain (from AOR) | Owner decides (examples @ first hires) | Partner’s job |
|-------------------|------------------------------------------|---------------|
| Product / roadmap | Feature order for this cycle; what’s in/out of scope for a bet | Input in founder sync; commit once owner calls it |
| Engineering | Stack/tool choices under €X; how to ship; who reviews what | Same |
| Sales / GTM | Which segment to pursue first; pricing *test* design (not company-wide price lock) | Same |
| Ops / finance | Vendor under €X; expense policy tweaks; invoice follow-up | Same |
| Hiring execution | **Not** “hire anyone” — posting/offer still hits [3.2](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#3.2--first-hires) + Bucket C/B for approval | |

**Closure in Bucket A:** owner consults affected founder(s) + expertise → states decision clearly → non-owner commits aloud → optional reopen trigger for two-way doors (4–12 weeks). If owner skipped consult, partner is not obliged to “commit” — that's overrule.

**Not Bucket A:** anything that touches equity, bank signing authority, fundraising, adding a founder, selling the company, or spend above your agreed threshold ([Mercury](https://mercury.com/blog/cofounder-prenup-money-questions): “major financial decisions — unanimous or final say?” must be chosen explicitly).

#### Bucket B — examples (discuss; majority or CEO tie-break)

*Ascent puts here: “pricing tests, feature prioritization, and small spend limits.” These are **shared** bets — both founders care, but deadlock is unacceptable.*

| Topic | Why B not A | Default tie-break @ 2 founders |
|-------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| **Pricing / packaging test** | Affects product + GTM | CEO, or whoever owns GTM if pre-agreed |
| **Feature prioritization** (cross-team) | Eng + product + customer impact | Product owner, or CEO if cross-domain |
| **Spend between €Y and €X** | “Small spend limits” (Ascent) — above pocket money, below Bucket C | CEO, or CFO-designate founder |
| **First non-founder hire role profile** | Shapes company; execution still via 3.2 | Often unanimous in practice → consider **B→C** for first hire |
| **Brand / positioning shift** (not full pivot) | Marketing + product | GTM owner or CEO |
| **Tooling / stack** (if both founders are technical) | Cross-domain | CTO / eng owner |

**2-founder nuance:** “Majority vote” is meaningless at 50/50 — sources agree you need a **named tie-break** ([Ramp](https://ramp.com/blog/what-is-in-a-startup-founder-agreement): advisor, board member, or designated tiebreaker; [Equity Matrix](https://equitymatrix.io/blog/co-founder-agreement-template): “[Founder 1 / mechanism] shall have final authority” on deadlock; [Munson](https://www.mattmunson.me/10-questions-every-co-founder-team-should-discuss/): CEO breaks ties or domain owner). **Wiki default:** CEO tie-break on Bucket B unless the issue clearly sits in one founder’s AOR cluster.

**Bucket B closure:** full founder sync debate → if no alignment, tie-break owner decides → both commit → **one narrative** to team ([Clarityframe](https://clarityframe.io/briefs/when-founders-disagree-resolution-framework/)).

#### Bucket C — examples (unanimous)

*Ascent: “equity changes, fundraising, adding/removing a founder, large spending, and selling the company.” Ramp: “fundraising, bringing on new co-founders, and selling the company.” Mercury: large expenses, debt, fundraising. Equity Matrix: new equity, large indebtedness above $[THRESHOLD], sale/merger.*

| Topic | Notes |
|-------|-------|
| Equity split / changes, vesting exceptions | Written in [4.1 foundation](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#4.1--foundation) — legal home |
| Fundraising (any external capital) | Terms, dilution, investor choice |
| Adding or removing a founder | Cap table + culture |
| Selling the company / material M&A | Existential |
| **Large spend** above €X | Team agrees €X in founder sync; tie to [2.1](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#2.1--first-hires) runway |
| **Pivot** (material strategy change) | Overlaps our team-wide approval list |
| **Hiring** (approval to hire / offer band) | Bucket C for *approval*; [3.2](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#3.2--first-hires) for *process* |
| Bank signatories, guarantees, personal debt for the company | [Mercury](https://mercury.com/blog/cofounder-prenup-money-questions) |

**If Bucket C deadlocks:** you cannot disagree-and-commit past a true principle conflict ([Clarityframe](https://clarityframe.io/briefs/when-founders-disagree-resolution-framework/): non-negotiables listed upfront). Options: mediator ([CGL](https://cgl-llp.com/insights/key-strategies-for-resolving-founder-deadlocks/)), advisor tie-break (only if pre-agreed for *specific* classes — rare for C), or graceful separation conversation ([YC Q10](https://www.ycombinator.com/blog/10-questions-to-discuss-with-a-potential-co-founder/)).

| Wiki layer | Bucket mapping |
|------------|----------------|
| **Founder circle** | Fill ABC map + AOR owners; Bucket A is default posture between founders |

#### Schools (bucket strictness)

| School | Position |
|--------|----------|
| **Strict ABC** | Every decision type listed once; no “we’ll figure it out in the moment” ([Ascent](https://ascentincubator.com/legal-administrative/founders-agreements-the-founder-prenup-that-prevents-cofounder-conflict-and-speeds-up-mvp-execution/)) |
| **A-only + oral C** | Domain owners decide; only fundraise/sell/equity are explicit unanimous |
| **CEO as B and C tie-break** | [Elad Gil](https://blog.eladgil.com/p/unequal-cofounders): one person can decide across areas when domains overlap |
| **Middle (wiki)** | Written ABC one-pager; quarterly 30-min review ([Ascent Step 7](https://ascentincubator.com/legal-administrative/founders-agreements-the-founder-prenup-that-prevents-cofounder-conflict-and-speeds-up-mvp-execution/) / HBS: revise as company becomes real) |

#### Grey areas — decide once, write down

| Situation | Source guidance | Suggested default |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Cross-domain (product ∩ sales) | [Elad Gil](https://blog.eladgil.com/p/unequal-cofounders): “equal but own different areas” fails when issues overlap | Bucket B → CEO or pre-named “tie domain” owner |

### Warning signs (closure-specific)

- Debate without naming who decides
- Decider calls it without consulting — partner feels overruled, not committed
- “We’ll revisit” with no trigger — decision reopens every week; irritation compounds
- Passive agreement (silence) then sabotage or “I told you so”
- Same fork argued for weeks — trust erodes; later blamed on “personality”
- One founder always “disagree and commits” — cover for no authority map or no consult duty
- Cross-domain issue with no tie-break owner ([Elad Gil](https://blog.eladgil.com/p/unequal-cofounders) red flag: “equal but own different areas”)

## Team-wide layer — approval list (detail for Phase 2)

**Default middle-ground list @ first hires:**

1. **Hiring** — including offer band and contract ([3.2](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#3.2--first-hires))
2. **Spend above €X** — tune to your burn (founders agree X in founder sync)
3. **Pivots / strategy** — material change to product, market, or business model
4. **Everything else** — owner of the work decides; escalate if reversible impact is unclear

**Delegation principle:** Projects with boundaries, not tasks with approvers ([Shape Up](https://basecamp.com/shapeup/3.1-chapter-10), [Jason Fried](https://world.hey.com/jason/delegating-projects-not-tasks-f36cb8bc)).

**Light DRI:** In meetings, name one decider and a deadline — no RACI grid. Decider owns **consulting** the room before calling it ([DRI / Advice process](https://medium.com/the-coinbase-blog/coinbase-is-a-product-led-company-d8f03549f498)).

## Warning signs (candidates for wiki)

**Founder circle**

- Endless debate on same fork — stall; relationship irritation; trust erosion
- Roles assumed from titles; never discussed
- High skill / low passion — still “owns” that domain
- Personal runway never discussed — founder stops or freelances after ~4 months
- Financial asymmetry unspoken — resentment on expenses, salary, or fundraising pace
- No founder-only time — everything in team meetings
- Vision drift — founders have different daily realities ([Lockett](https://review.firstround.com/five-practices-to-strengthen-your-co-founder-relationship/))

**Team-wide**

- Founder bottleneck on trivial decisions ([HN 41464524](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41464524))
- Team waits for OK instead of acting
- Hiring or spend happens outside the approval list
- Strict silos — founders not literate in each other’s domains

## Maintenance vs repair

| Teams that stall | Teams that move |
|------------------|-----------------|
| Roles from titles; process “when it happens” | One evening on the ABC template |
| Same fork for weeks | Weekly founder slot; disagreement allowed |
| Irritation → “personality clash” | Close with: who owns it, when we revisit |
| Decider skips consult | Decider chases input, then calls fork |

You don't need conviction on every route. You need a rule for the fork, consult duty on the decider, and discipline to close.
