# 1.2b — Shareholder & Investor Reporting: proposed variants

This is a review draft. Edit inline (Apple Notes / on mobile is fine), then paste the edited markdown back into chat so it can be applied to `data/processes.json`.

## Context

`1.2b — Shareholder & Investor Reporting` is hidden for **bootstrapped** companies entirely (via `conditions.fundingStage`). So variants only need to cover the 4 funded segments: `angel-fff` (default), `venture-early` (seed / Series A), `venture-growth` (Series B+ / PE), `public`.

The current `stageFocus` text reads like it was written assuming the most *typical* funding stage at each headcount stage (angel at first-hires, venture-early at early-revenue / growth, mature governance at scaled). The variants below override the off-diagonal cases — e.g. a small team that's already venture-backed, or a growth-stage company that's gone public.

`foundation` is intentionally not overridden because the process is `future` at that stage anyway and the "not relevant yet" text holds for all funding levels.

---

## Proposed variants

### Default (`angel-fff` / friends & family)

Keep current `stageFocus`, no changes needed.

---

### Variant: `venture-early` (seed / Series A backed)

**first-hires:** Monthly investor update by email — KPIs, cash, hiring plan, key wins/losses, what you need help with. Quarterly board meeting with a proper deck (5-10 slides, pre-read 48h ahead). Keep written board minutes — your shareholder agreement requires them. Cap table managed in a real tool (Carta, Pulley, Ledgy) from day one of the round.

**early-revenue:** Monthly investor email with a KPI dashboard, cash position, plan vs actuals, and asks. Quarterly board meeting with a structured deck: financials, KPIs, plan vs actuals, hiring, strategic initiatives. Annual budget formally approved by the board. Information rights tracked: most term sheets require monthly metrics + audited annual financials. CEO or CFO owns the cadence — don't let it slip.

---

### Variant: `venture-growth` (Series B+ / PE)

**early-revenue:** Board operates like a real board: chair, formal agenda, written minutes circulated, action items tracked. Quarterly board pack (10-20 slides) covers financials, KPIs, strategic initiatives, risk register. Auditor likely appointed by now. CFO owns investor relations as a defined responsibility. Monthly investor updates are standardised and consistent month-over-month.

**growth:** Formal board cadence (quarterly or 6 meetings/year). Comprehensive board pack circulated 5 working days in advance. Audit committee active and meeting separately. Annual audited financials. Investor relations is a defined function (usually CFO + finance lead). Compliance with information rights, drag-along, ROFR, and other shareholder agreement obligations actively tracked by legal.

**scaled:** Full board governance: audit committee, compensation committee, sometimes nomination committee. Monthly investor reporting plus a quarterly board cycle. Audited financials. If preparing for IPO or exit, data room hygiene is a year-round discipline, not a fire drill. IR function staffed (head of IR or CFO + analyst). Compliance team partners with legal and finance on shareholder agreement obligations and side letters.

---

### Variant: `public`

**growth:** Full quarterly reporting cadence (10-Q or local equivalent). Earnings release and call every quarter, even at this size. IR function in place (often a head of IR reporting to CFO). Audit committee with independent directors. Material non-public information protocols enforced across the company. SOX 404 readiness program (US) or local equivalent. Trading window communications to staff every quarter.

**scaled:** Full IR operation: earnings calls, investor day, sell-side analyst coverage management, AGM logistics. Regulator filings on cadence: 10-K, 10-Q, 8-K, proxy statement (or local equivalents). Three standing board committees (audit, compensation, nominations) with majority independent directors. SOX 404 controls audited annually. Material non-public information and trading window protocols enforced. Crisis communications playbook for adverse disclosures (earnings miss, security incident, leadership change).

## Review prompts

- For each variant, ask: "is this what I'd tell a founder in that situation?"
- Easiest edits: tighten language, swap examples, remove anything that feels off (especially the `public` variant — small public companies are rare and may be over-formalised here).
- Hardest call: the `venture-early` variant at `first-hires`. Is "cap table in a real tool from day one of the round" too prescriptive? Some VC-backed seed companies still run on a spreadsheet for months. Open to softening.
