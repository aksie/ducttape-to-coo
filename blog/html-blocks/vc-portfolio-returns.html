Interactive explainer: when most startups in a fund return zero, the surviving few must each return a much higher multiple for the fund to hit its 4× target.

A fund promising ~20% a year is basically a savings account paying 20% to its own investors. Compounded over ~10 years that means handing back roughly 4× everything that went in. But most bets return nothing — so the survivors have to cover the corpses too.
