## Claim

A lawyer left alone often makes things worse — trained for years to avoid risk and say yes to no one, they flag everything, which doesn't scale; boundary-setting in-house is hard, personal, and needs coaching.

## Source quote or paraphrase

A lawyer alone often makes it worse — trained 5–6 years to avoid risk and solve everyone's problems, they flag everything; in-house, clients are at your desk, so boundary-setting is hard and needs coaching.

## Why

Flagging everything erodes trust and overloads the lawyer; without coaching on boundaries, the legal function becomes a bottleneck instead of an enabler.
