## Claim

Dutch employment law places compliance obligations at the first hire — not at 10, 25, or 50 people. The default assumption that "this doesn't apply yet" is wrong and expensive.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Hour registration, sick leave procedure, non-discrimination policy, and working hours documentation are all legally required from the moment an employment contract exists. There is no startup exemption or headcount threshold for these obligations.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

The widespread founder assumption that HR compliance is a later-stage problem is a significant source of legal risk for early Dutch startups. The obligations exist from day one; the consequences of non-compliance compound with every hire and every month that passes without documentation. Fixing this retroactively is possible but requires reconstructing records that should have existed from the start.
