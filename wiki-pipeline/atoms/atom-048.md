## Claim
Design something into the first few days that makes the new hire easy to approach — don't leave the burden of building connections entirely on them.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Gilboa describes Warby Parker's "Nice to meat you" balloons on new hires' desks as beacons that condition existing employees to introduce themselves. New hires also introduce themselves at all-hands with a memorable fun fact. "People don't do this for themselves as proactively as you think they will. You need to build it into their first days on the job to make them as approachable as they'd like to be."

## Why (if explicit in source)
Being new in an established team is socially uncomfortable. Everyone around you has shared history. Without a designed nudge, both the new person and existing employees tend to avoid the slightly awkward first introduction — and connections that don't form in week one often don't form at all.
