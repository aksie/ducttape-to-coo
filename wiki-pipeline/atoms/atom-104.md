## Claim

At First Hires stage, what good looks like for Office & Facilities is almost entirely about two things: an explicit decision on where people work, and one named person who owns the physical space (if you have one).

## Source quote or paraphrase

HN practitioners consistently describe the First Hires remote/office question as one the founding team must answer intentionally — "it doesn't have to be black and white, but it needs to be decided, not defaulted to." If you take office space, someone needs to be the person supplies, the lease, and basic safety compliance land on.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

At 2–10 people the team is small enough to operate without formal systems — but not without ownership. If the office question is not answered explicitly, each new hire will form a different assumption about where they should be and whether they can work remotely. Retroactively changing a work model after people have joined on certain expectations is expensive in trust and attrition. If office logistics are not explicitly owned, the failure mode is not catastrophic — it is death by a thousand small frictions: empty printer paper, an invoice nobody knows to pay, a lease renewal nobody noticed.
