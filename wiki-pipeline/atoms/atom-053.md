## Claim
If your onboarding process lives in one person's head, the quality of a new hire's first week depends entirely on whether that person is available — document it so it can run without them.

## Source quote or paraphrase
GitLab's onboarding guidance emphasises that the onboarding process should be documented in a checklist or runbook, not carried by the person who last ran it. If that person is on holiday when a new hire starts, the experience degrades.

## Why (if explicit in source)
Undocumented processes have single points of failure. At 2-10 people, one person often runs onboarding informally, but when they're not available, new hires get a significantly worse experience — and the inconsistency is noticed.
