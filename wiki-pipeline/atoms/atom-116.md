## Claim

Skipping hour registration in the Netherlands can result in fines of up to €10,000 per employee — and the risk applies from the moment you have one employee.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Non-compliance with the Dutch Working Hours Act (Arbeidstijdenwet) carries fines up to €10,000 per employee. The Dutch Labour Inspectorate (Nederlandse Arbeidsinspectie) enforces this actively.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

This is a strict liability rule — intent doesn't matter. Many early-stage founders assume hour tracking is only relevant once they scale, but the obligation begins with the first employment contract. Catching up retroactively is difficult and leaves the company exposed in any dispute about hours, overtime, or sick leave entitlements.
