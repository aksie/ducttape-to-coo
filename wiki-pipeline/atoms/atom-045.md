## Claim
Write down your culture — what the company is, where it came from, how you work, what you value — in a document that new hires can read and return to. Verbal transmission doesn't scale and is inconsistent.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Brier created Percolate's "Day One Document" — a living Google Doc covering company culture, origin, values, and working norms. "The key to designing a thorough onboarding program that gets new people acquainted quickly is over-documenting everything. We find that words scale well. Plus, documentation is a ready resource that new hires can refer to before asking other colleagues."

## Why (if explicit in source)
When culture is only transmitted verbally by founders, it degrades with each telling and disappears when founders are unavailable. A written document can be read asynchronously, referenced repeatedly, and updated as the company evolves.
