## Claim

Filing gaps found by an investor's or buyer's legal team will damage your negotiating position — the most likely outcomes are a lower price or a longer list of representations and warranties.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Had the investors' legal team found those gaps, it would have damaged our negotiating position — not because the underlying issues were serious, but because disorganisation signals risk. The likely outcome would have been either a lower price or more extensive reps and warranties.

## Why

Reps and warranties shift risk from buyer to seller. A buyer who finds organisational gaps uses them to extract either a price reduction or broader contractual protection. The issues themselves may be minor; the signal they send — that other things may also be poorly managed — is what drives the negotiation impact.
