## Claim

Once you reach 10 employees in the Netherlands, you are legally required to designate a vertrouwenspersoon (confidential advisor) — document this in your handbook before you hit that number.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Dutch law requires organisations with 10 or more employees to make a confidential advisor available for reports of undesirable behaviour including harassment, discrimination, intimidation, and aggression. The advisor must be reachable confidentially and without consequence for the reporter.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

The requirement exists because power dynamics make it unsafe for employees to report undesirable behaviour through the normal management chain. Without a designated person, employees have no confidential route — which means problems escalate or go unreported until they become serious. Small companies often discover this obligation only after a complaint surfaces.
