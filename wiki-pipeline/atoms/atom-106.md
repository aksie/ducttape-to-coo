## Claim

If you take office space, assign one named person as responsible for it — supplies restocked, lease terms known, basic health & safety covered, and a single point of contact for the landlord and any vendors.

## Source quote or paraphrase

The practitioner (alexrodba.com, running a 10-person remote company) describes hiring an "office manager" despite having no physical office — because the function is necessary regardless: someone must be the "operational glue" who handles everything that falls between defined roles. In a physical office this is even more acute: supplies don't reorder themselves, the lease renewal doesn't happen automatically, and the landlord calls someone.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

In a 2–10 person company, nobody has "facilities" in their job title. This means that unless ownership is explicitly assigned, facilities tasks become nobody's job — which means they happen late, badly, or not at all. A single named owner costs nothing to designate and prevents a class of small but persistent frictions. The owner doesn't need to do the work themselves; they just need to be the person it lands on.
