## Claim

Real governance kicks in once an investor comes aboard: even with a majority you effectively need their consent on things like the annual budget, because they can withhold approval until they like the plan.

## Source quote or paraphrase

While founders run things governance is a mishmash; it becomes real when an investor joins — e.g. they must approve next year's budget, so even a majority needs their consent.

## Why

Consent and budget-approval rights shift power away from raw share majority; founders who don't plan for this collide with investors at budget time.
