## Claim
When setting goals, founders should share their thinking — including what they don't know — before asking the team to propose plans. Goals set in a vacuum come back misaligned.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Rachitsky and Gilbreth: "Teams need context. They need to know what the company absolutely needs to nail over the next year." The W Framework starts with leadership sharing context, not leadership dictating goals.

## Why (if explicit in source)
Teams cannot propose good plans without understanding the strategic constraints. When leadership shares both what they know and what they're uncertain about, teams can fill in gaps rather than guess.
