## Claim

Watch for everything defaulting to the statutory directors: they are solely authorised by default, so signing piles onto them until they blind-sign — and a next round's due diligence will flag it if an officer who isn't a statutory director signs everything without a resolution authorising it.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Statutory directors are solely authorised by default; eventually everything piles on them and they blind-sign. DD asks "why is this signed by a non-statutory officer?" — the data room should show the authorising resolution/matrix.

## Why

Blind-signing means no real control, and an unauthorised signer surfaces in DD as a governance gap — both undermine the company exactly when an investor is looking.
