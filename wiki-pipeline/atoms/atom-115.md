## Claim

In the Netherlands, you must track working hours for every employee from the first hire — including remote workers and people on flexible arrangements.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Dutch Arbeidstijdenwet requires objective, reliable, and accessible time records for all employees. A 2022 Dutch high court ruling confirmed this applies universally, regardless of work arrangement. Records must be retained for at least one year and employees must have access to their own records.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

The 2019 CJEU ruling established that EU member states must require objective time tracking, and the Netherlands codified this strictly. The employer bears the burden of proof in any working hours dispute — without records, the employer loses by default.
