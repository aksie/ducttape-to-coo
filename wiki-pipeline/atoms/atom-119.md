## Claim

Missing a WVP milestone during an employee's illness can extend your legal re-integration obligation beyond two years — a mistake that is easily avoided with a checklist but expensive to fix after the fact.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Dutch WVP sets hard deadlines: problem analysis by week 6, plan of approach by week 8, first-year evaluation by week 52, UWV notification at week 42 if return seems unlikely. Missing any milestone risks a "loonsanctie" (wage penalty) — the employer must continue paying for an additional year.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

The Dutch system places the full re-integration burden on the employer. A loonsanctie — extension of the two-year obligation — is the most common and most avoidable financial penalty in Dutch employment law for small companies. It happens not because of bad intent but because nobody knew the timeline.
