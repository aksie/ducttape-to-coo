## Claim
Move all non-contractor business expenses onto one or a small set of corporate cards (personal name or LLC name is fine; the rule is no personal expenses mix in).

## Source quote or paraphrase
patio11 advises moving non-1099 expenses to a small set of cards dedicated to business use, with the explicit goal of simplifying bookkeeping by keeping personal and business spending separated.

## Why
Mixing personal and business expenses on the same card creates reconciliation work later and risks missing deductible business expenses. At a startup founder's marginal tax rate (50%+ when combining federal, state, and self-employment), every missed deductible expense costs roughly half its value out-of-pocket. A $2,000 hotel charge missed in bookkeeping is a $1,000 personal cost for the sake of one minute of data entry.
