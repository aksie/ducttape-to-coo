## Claim
Limit company priorities to 3-5 at most — anything more means you don't have priorities, you have a wishlist. Put real resources behind the few things that matter most.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Rachitsky and Gilbreth: "When you leave the planning process wondering if you put too many resources behind a single bet, that's the bet that ends up succeeding. Bold ideas need bold resourcing."

## Why (if explicit in source)
Spreading resources across too many priorities ensures none of them get enough to succeed. The discomfort of cutting good ideas is the price of giving the right ideas a real chance.
