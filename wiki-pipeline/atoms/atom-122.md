## Claim

Separate "time off" (vacation, sick days, public holidays) from "leave of absence" (parental, medical, calamity) in your documentation — conflating them creates confusion and missed escalation paths.

## Source quote or paraphrase

GitLab's handbook distinguishes time off (shorter absences that employees manage themselves) from leave (longer, formally approved absences for specific life circumstances). This distinction clarifies when approval is needed, what documentation is required, and what the re-entry process looks like.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

Without this distinction, both employees and managers apply inconsistent standards: some treat parental leave like a long vacation, others treat a sick day like a formal leave application. The confusion is especially costly in the Netherlands where parental and medical leave have specific legal procedures and deadlines attached.
