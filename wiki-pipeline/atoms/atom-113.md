## Claim

For Dutch startups, the KVK website and arborisico.nl/branche-RI&E tools provide free or low-cost RI&E instruments that qualify as the legally required risk assessment — no external safety consultant needed at ≤25 employees.

## Source quote or paraphrase

KVK guidance: companies using a "erkend RI&E-instrument" (recognised branch-specific RI&E tool) with ≤25 employees are exempt from the professional review requirement. Many Dutch industry associations publish recognised tools for their sector. For a generic tech/office environment, the standard Arbowet RI&E checklist for kantooromgevingen (office environments) is available via recognised instruments.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

The practical implication for a 2–10 person startup is that the RI&E obligation is real but the cost of fulfilling it is low: a few hours completing an online checklist, documenting the risks (typically: ergonomics, emergency exit, first aid kit, fire extinguisher), and writing a brief Plan van Aanpak. The cost of not doing it is a fine from the Arbeidsinspectie and potentially reduced insurance coverage — disproportionate to the effort of doing it.
