## Claim

At First Hires stage, the default-good workplace setup is coworking or flexible space — not a dedicated leased office. This preserves capital and optionality while still providing a shared physical context for the team if they are local.

## Source quote or paraphrase

HN practitioners discussing the pre-seed/seed revenue data note that the colocation benefit is about being in the same room for fast iteration, not about having a dedicated company office. A coworking space qualifies. The "radical colocation" effect that drives the data does not require a company-specific space — it requires physical proximity.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

Coworking at First Hires stage gives the team a shared context for collaboration and culture-building (the primary benefit of in-person work) without the capital commitment of a lease or the operational overhead of managing a space. At 2–10 people, you don't need to manage facilities — you need to use them. Coworking outsources that management to the space operator. The cost per desk is higher than a comparable leased desk at scale, but the total cost including lease risk, fit-out, and management time is lower until headcount is sufficiently predictable.
