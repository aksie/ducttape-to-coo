## Claim

For most companies, spend time building a great product over locking it down — patents are usually low value unless your USP truly depends on something specific; trademarks, by contrast, do matter (copycats appear), so register your mark and keep all registrations current. An unprotected, easily-copied brand is a red flag in due diligence.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Patents usually low value unless the USP depends on it; trademarks matter (copycats appear) and show well in DD; not protecting an easily-copied brand is a DD red flag; keep registrations valid.

## Why

Patents are costly and often protect little, while a registered trademark cheaply stops copycats and signals diligence to investors; lapsed registrations quietly lose that protection.
