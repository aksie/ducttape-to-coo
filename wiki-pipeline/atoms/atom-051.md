## Claim
Run a 30-day check-in with every new hire as a standing ritual — ask what's going well, what's confusing, and what they wish they'd known earlier. Use the answers to improve onboarding for the next hire.

## Source quote or paraphrase
GitLab's onboarding guide prescribes a 30-day check-in as a structured conversation. The questions focus on experience rather than performance — creating a feedback loop that improves the process over time.

## Why (if explicit in source)
New hires see the onboarding experience with fresh eyes that insiders can't have. Their confusion and surprises reveal gaps that existing team members are too acclimated to notice. A 30-day check-in captures this signal before it fades.
