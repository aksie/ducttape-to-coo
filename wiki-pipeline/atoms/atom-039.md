## Claim
At First Hires, your primary planning metric should be a growth rate you track weekly — not a quarterly planning document. If you hit it, you're on track; if you don't, that's your only problem to solve.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Paul Graham: "We usually advise startups to pick a growth rate they think they can hit, and then just try to hit it every week. The key word here is 'just.' If they decide to grow at 7% a week and they hit that number, they're successful for that week."

## Why (if explicit in source)
Elaborate planning systems are premature at 2-10 people. A single weekly growth target is both necessary and sufficient — it tells you whether to be satisfied or alarmed, and it makes prioritisation simple.
