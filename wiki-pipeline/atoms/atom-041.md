## Claim
For a team of 2-10, consider OGSM (Objectives, Goals, Strategies, Measures) over OKR. OKR is process-heavy and light on strategy — OGSM forces you to connect the objective to a strategy before jumping to metrics, which is more useful at a stage where strategic clarity matters more than goal-tracking rigour.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Direct experience: OKR works well at scale where teams need a shared language for goal alignment, but at 2-10 people the process overhead outweighs the benefit. OGSM — Objective (what we want to achieve), Goal (the quantified target), Strategy (how we will get there), Measure (how we know it's working) — is simpler to apply and forces the strategic question that OKR often skips.

## Why (if explicit in source)
At First Hires, the most valuable planning work is deciding *how* you will win, not tracking whether you hit a metric. OKR assumes the strategy is settled and focuses on execution. OGSM keeps strategy visible as part of the planning artifact, which is more appropriate when the strategy is still being figured out.
