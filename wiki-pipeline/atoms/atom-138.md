## Claim

Once you start hiring, designate one person as the explicit owner of the filing structure. Without a named owner, contracts and documents default to email inboxes and are effectively lost.

## Source quote or paraphrase

At first hires, HR documents start to accumulate. The risk is that contracts end up in a manager's email rather than a central location. Designating one person as "owner" of the filing structure prevents this.

## Why

Shared responsibility for filing is no responsibility. In the absence of a named owner, everyone assumes someone else has filed it. The person most likely to be the de facto owner — the hiring manager or founder — is also the person most likely to be too busy to do it consistently.
