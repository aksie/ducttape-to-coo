## Claim

Consistent, immediate access revocation protects the departing employee as much as the company — if something breaks later, they can point to having been locked out properly.

## Source quote or paraphrase

HN practitioners report ex-clients asking for access after departures; those who handed over credentials and deleted local copies were glad; consistent lockout avoids suspicion if a breach occurs post-departure.

## Why

Without documented revocation, former employees remain suspects in every incident. Fast lockout is reputational protection for both sides, not just security theater.
