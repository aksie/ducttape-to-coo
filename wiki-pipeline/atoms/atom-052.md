## Claim
If you're too busy to give new employees a good first week, that attitude will perpetuate every time you hire — sloppy onboarding becomes sloppy culture.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Guthrie: "A lot of companies hide behind 'we're a scrappy startup and moving so fast.' It might be something as simple as putting Chrome on a new hire's computer, but it sends the message that you can't be bothered, that you're about broad-strokes. What does this tell people about how to treat your company's product or users when they see a bug or something wrong?" Sloppy onboarding turns into sloppy culture.

## Why (if explicit in source)
The standards you demonstrate in onboarding become the standards people expect everywhere. If the company can't be bothered to set up a desk properly, employees infer that attention to detail is not actually a value here — whatever the values doc says.
