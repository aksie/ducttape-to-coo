## Claim
At Early Scale (~25+ people), goal-setting needs a structured process with clear roles — who sets context, who proposes plans, who integrates. Informal goal-setting breaks down when there are multiple teams.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Rachitsky and Gilbreth note the W Framework is most applicable at ~25 people once there are multiple teams. Before that, the informal version works: founders share context, team proposes, founders decide.

## Why (if explicit in source)
With one team, the founder can hold the planning process in their head. With multiple teams, you need explicit roles or you get either top-down dictation or bottom-up chaos.
