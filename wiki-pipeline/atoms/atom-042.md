## Claim
Everything the new hire needs is ready before they walk in on day one — laptop set up, accounts provisioned, desk prepared. Waiting until they arrive to sort logistics sends the message that you weren't expecting them.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Percolate's Noah Brier: the standard at Percolate was that new hires walk in to find a desk with everything laid out — computer, monitor, notebook, pens. One of the most common onboarding failures he sees is new hires having to wait until 2pm for their laptop.

## Why (if explicit in source)
The first impression is formed before any conversation happens. A desk that's ready signals that the company is organised and that they were genuinely anticipated. A missing laptop signals the opposite and is hard to walk back.
