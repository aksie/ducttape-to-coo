## Claim

Document all three address variants in your handbook: visiting address, registered address (KvK), and postal address — they are often different and the distinction matters for legal correspondence.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Early-stage companies often have a registered address (e.g. at an accountant or flex-office) that differs from where they actually work. Employees, clients, and government agencies may use any of these. Undocumented, this causes missed letters and legal notices.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

Government agencies and courts use the registered address for official correspondence. If that address is at a flex-office or accountant and mail isn't being forwarded, you can miss tax notices, UWV correspondence, or legal summons — with consequences that accrue before you know the document arrived.
