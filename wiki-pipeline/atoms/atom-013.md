## Claim
The first time you do a proper budget vs. actuals review, you will find at least one significant line item that has been wrong for months — this is normal and the point is to fix it, not to be embarrassed by it.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Reddit practitioner notes that the first budget vs. actuals review almost always surfaces a surprise — a vendor charge that went unchallenged, a subscription nobody cancelled, a cost coded to the wrong category.

## Why (if explicit in source)
Without a comparison ritual, errors persist invisibly. The first review is diagnostic; the value compounds over subsequent reviews as errors get caught earlier.
