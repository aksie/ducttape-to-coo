## Claim
If your team consistently hits 100% of their goals, the goals are too easy — you're optimising for comfort rather than progress.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Davis: "Most people wouldn't consider 70% to be a good grade, but for OKRs that's just about perfect." If everyone is hitting everything, nobody is stretching.

## Why (if explicit in source)
Goals that are always achievable at 100% don't force the hard questions: what would it take to do 50% more? Goals should create productive discomfort, not confirmatory comfort.
