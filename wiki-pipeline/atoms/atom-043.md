## Claim
Over-schedule the first day rather than under-schedule it. Don't leave new hires with blank time and no one to talk to — default to filling their calendar.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Brier: "I know there are different schools of thought about onboarding schedules. Generally, we try to over-schedule instead of under-schedule for new employees. In my own personal experience, it's a weird feeling to show up on the first day and not having any work or idea of what to do."

## Why (if explicit in source)
Blank time on day one creates anxiety, not autonomy. New hires can't fill time productively without context they don't yet have. A structured first day removes the uncertainty and signals that their arrival was anticipated.
