## Claim
If your team treats the bank balance as the P&L, you have a financial literacy problem that will cause real decisions to be made on wrong assumptions.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Bench describes treating the bank balance as a proxy for business health as the most common bookkeeping mistake they see. Cash balance doesn't reflect outstanding invoices, deferred revenue, or accrued liabilities.

## Why (if explicit in source)
Bank balance is a snapshot of cash; P&L is a measure of value created. They diverge in opposite directions depending on whether you invoice ahead (cash looks good, profit less so) or behind (cash looks bad, profit better than it appears).
