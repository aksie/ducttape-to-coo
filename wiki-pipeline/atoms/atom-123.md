## Claim

At the First Hires stage, offer at least 25 days of annual leave — the Dutch legal minimum is 20, but 25 is the market standard and affects your ability to hire.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Dutch law mandates a minimum of 20 days annual leave (4× contracted weekly hours). Market practice at startups and tech companies is 25 days. The gap matters in recruiting: candidates interpret fewer than 25 days as a red flag.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

The 5-day gap between legal minimum and market norm is a low-cost recruiting signal. Candidates know the Dutch market standard. Offering only 20 days signals either that you don't know the norm or that you've chosen to go below it — neither reflects well in the first hires stage where you're competing for talent without the brand advantage of larger companies.
