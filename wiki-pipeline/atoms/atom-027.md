## Claim
Don't tie company goals to individual compensation or performance reviews — this causes people to set safe, easy-to-hit goals rather than ambitious ones.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Davis is explicit: OKRs tied to compensation cause sandbagging. People set goals they know they'll hit rather than goals that push them. The result is a planning system that generates comfort, not progress.

## Why (if explicit in source)
If hitting 100% of goals gets you a bonus, you'll set goals you know you can hit at 100%. You want goals ambitious enough that 70% is a good result. These two incentives are incompatible.
