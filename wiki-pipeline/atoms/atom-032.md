## Claim
If your team built their goals without any context from you first, you will get back a list of five times as many priorities as you have people — all pulling in different directions.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Rachitsky and Gilbreth describe a planning cycle where leadership skipped the context step and received bottom-up plans: "There were five times as many priorities as there were teams, and individual team strategies often pulled in opposite directions."

## Why (if explicit in source)
Without shared context, each team optimises for their own area. The sum of individually rational plans is often a collectively irrational set of priorities.
