## Claim

HR due diligence in a transaction is a significant workload. Buyers expect: an org chart with compensation, a key person risk assessment, all employment contracts, full dispute history, collective agreement status, and works council documentation. If none of this has been maintained, preparing it from scratch during a live transaction is extremely costly.

## Source quote or paraphrase

At growth stage, HR due diligence in a transaction is a significant workload. Buyers want to see: org chart with compensation, key person risk assessment, all employment contracts, all dispute history, collective agreement status, works council documentation.

## Why

HR due diligence touches both legal risk (contract gaps, disputes, compliance) and commercial risk (key person dependency, retention). Buyers invest significant time in it. A company that has maintained its HR filing throughout has a clear advantage: it can respond quickly, projecting operational maturity. A company that scrambles is sending the opposite signal at the worst possible time.
