## Claim

From early revenue, maintain a one-page document (or a single spreadsheet tab) that bridges your management financials to your statutory annual accounts — explaining revenue recognition differences, EBITDA adjustments, and any non-recurring items.

## Source quote or paraphrase

A one-page (or one-tab) document that says "here's why these two numbers differ, and here's the reasoning behind each adjustment." Write it once a year when you prepare the annual accounts, update it as you go, and file it next to the annual accounts.

## Why

Every company has two versions of its financials: statutory accounts and management accounts. These legitimately differ. In a due diligence process, buyers expect the differences — what they don't expect is that you can't explain them. The bridge document removes this risk entirely and is far easier to write once a year than to reconstruct under deal-time pressure.
