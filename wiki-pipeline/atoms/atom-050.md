## Claim
Assign an onboarding buddy who is not the new hire's manager — a peer-level guide removes the power dynamic and makes asking "stupid questions" feel safe.

## Source quote or paraphrase
GitLab's onboarding guidance recommends a buddy system where the buddy is a peer, not a manager. The manager relationship carries performance implications; a peer buddy creates a safe space to ask questions that the new hire might hesitate to ask upward.

## Why (if explicit in source)
New hires have many questions they're embarrassed to ask their manager because it might signal incompetence. A peer buddy absorbs these questions without judgment and accelerates the new hire's time to productivity.
