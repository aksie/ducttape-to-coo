## Claim

Several handbook sections are legally required in the Netherlands — treat them as compliance obligations, not optional documentation.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Dutch employment law requires written documentation of: sick leave notification procedure, working hours and rest times, non-discrimination policy, and how the employee can access their own HR records. These are not discretionary; their absence creates legal exposure.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

Dutch courts and the Labour Inspectorate (Arbeidsinspectie) consider the absence of required written policies as evidence of non-compliance, not just administrative sloppiness. In any employment dispute, the employer who cannot produce the required documentation starts from a disadvantaged position.
