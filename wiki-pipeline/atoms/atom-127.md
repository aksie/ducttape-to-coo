## Claim

A short, readable handbook is more valuable than a comprehensive one — if nobody reads it, it doesn't exist.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Basecamp's handbook is roughly 14 chapters and fits in a single document. GitLab's is 2,200+ pages. Basecamp's approach: cover everything once, clearly, in plain language. GitLab's thoroughness is a product of remote-first at scale, not a model for early-stage companies.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

At the first-hires stage, the handbook's primary job is to set expectations and build trust — not to be a legal reference document. A handbook that employees actually read and refer to is worth more than one that covers every edge case but sits unread. Thoroughness becomes more valuable as you scale; at 2-10 people, clarity is more important.
