## Claim

At first hires, decision rights are **two layers**: founders align on default domain ownership and runway context first, then publish one visible approval list (hiring, major spend, pivots) while everything else defaults to the person doing the work — with a named decider, duty to consult, and close (consult → decide → commit), not RACI or SPADE bureaucracy.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Synthesis of who-decides proposal + source-discovery Tracks A–C: order is founder circle before team-facing rules; ABC buckets map day-to-day (A), cross-domain tie-breaks (B), and unanimous items (C); middle ground rejects consensus-everywhere and founder-mode bottleneck equally.

## Why

At 2–10 people the failure modes are process ambiguity (stall loops, trust erosion) and founder bottleneck — not missing a handbook. This framework's value is **what to write down** and **how to close a fork**, with schools preserved so teams can choose delegate-early vs stay-hands-on without pretending one size fits all.

## Embedded decisions (first hires)

**Founder circle:** AOR exercise; personal runway per founder (not company runway); weekly founder sync → see 3.6 foundation.

**Team-wide:** Written list — hiring ([3.2](../people/3.2--first-hires.md)), spend above €X, pivots/strategy → founder OK; delegate **projects** not tasks (Shape Up).

**Disagreement:** Bucket lookup → owner consults → clear decision → commit aloud with reopen trigger; Grove six questions before the fork.
