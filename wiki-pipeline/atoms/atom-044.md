## Claim
If your onboarding experience doesn't match the experience you sold during recruiting, your best candidates will notice immediately — and they have other options.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Carly Guthrie: "There's this dangerous mentality of simply getting butts in seats with little thought paid to the onboarding process. Everyone wants to feel cherished, especially after being so vigorously pursued. People who are this talented get calls from recruiters all the time, and if they suddenly realize that they're just a number or a cog in the wheel to you now, they have other options."

## Why (if explicit in source)
Recruiting creates an expectation. Onboarding is the first test of whether that expectation was real. A gap between the two is experienced as a broken promise, and unlike most early impressions, this one is very hard to repair.
