## Claim

The Office & Facilities process doesn't become a real function until around 25–50 people. At First Hires stage it is one person's side responsibility; at Early Revenue it acquires a named policy; from Growth onwards it starts to require dedicated management.

## Source quote or paraphrase

The alexrodba.com practitioner describes the progression: "office manager" at 10 people (handling all operational glue, not just facilities), a second ops hire (Project Manager) at 20 people, and a second People Ops hire at 30 people focused on recruiting. The facilities function per se only gets dedicated attention as the team and space grow. This is consistent with the Control Solutions / Cushman & Wakefield data point that a dedicated Facilities Manager becomes a key decision-maker around 100 employees.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

At 2–10 people, "facilities" is roughly: is there coffee, are there chairs, is the lease known to someone, and is the floor plan legal. This is manageable as a side responsibility. At 25–50 people the complexity of managing space, vendors, maintenance contracts, health & safety, visitor/contractor access, and potentially multiple locations starts to exceed what a side-responsibility can absorb. Building a formal facilities function before the team reaches that scale creates process overhead before the process has real work to do.
