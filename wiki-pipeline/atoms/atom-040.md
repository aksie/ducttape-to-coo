## Claim
Delegating goal-setting requires skills your first hires probably don't have yet. At 2-10 people you have likely hired for functional excellence — the best designer, the best engineer — not for the ability to translate strategy into tactics or cascade goals. Don't delegate goal-setting before those skills exist in the team.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Direct experience: at a scale of 10, team members are typically strong individual contributors selected for their domain skill, not for strategic thinking or OKR methodology. Asking them to set goals that ladder up to company strategy often produces goals that are either too narrow (just their own work) or disconnected from what actually matters.

## Why (if explicit in source)
Goal-setting delegation only works when the person delegating to has the capability to do it well. If they don't, you either get bad goals or you end up correcting everything anyway — which is more work than doing it yourself. The fix is either developing those skills in existing people or hiring for them as you grow.
