## Claim

If you build software in-house, protect it and register where possible; consider housing it in a separate software BV for a clean carve-out (this affects your legal structure and contracts). If you build for clients, license it — decide explicitly what IP you keep versus what transfers, and protect your IP in the client contracts.

## Source quote or paraphrase

In-house software: protect/register; consider a separate software BV for easy carve-out; building for clients → license it, decide what IP you keep vs transfers, protect your IP in client contracts. This is a flag → architecture/roadmap point.

## Why

Where the software lives and who owns it shapes future fundraising and M&A carve-outs; deciding IP retention up front avoids handing your core asset to clients by default.
