## Claim
Set a clear payment approval threshold from the start — define what amount any single person can spend without asking, and require a second pair of eyes above that amount.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Founderpath recommends dual-signature approval for payments above a threshold (e.g. $5K) should be in place before Series A, and separation of duties between the person approving and the person paying reduces fraud risk significantly.

## Why
Without a threshold, you end up with either chaos (everyone spends freely and you discover the problem at month-end) or micromanagement (the founder has to approve every $20 software subscription). A clear threshold gives employees autonomy for small purchases while protecting you from large unauthorised spend. At the first-hires stage, you should keep the threshold relatively low — the goal is to build the habit of controlled spending before the amounts get large enough to matter.
