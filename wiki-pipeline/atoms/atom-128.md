## Claim

Publishing your employee handbook publicly is a recruiting signal — it shows candidates what working at your company actually looks like before they apply.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Basecamp, GitLab, Remote.com, and Zapier all publish their handbooks publicly. This serves as a recruiting tool: candidates who read it and still want to work there self-select more reliably than those who discover the culture post-hire.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

At the first-hires stage, you can't compete on brand or salary with larger companies. A public handbook signals transparency and confidence in your culture — and filters for candidates who fit before a single conversation. It also forces you to actually write down what you believe, which has internal value regardless of whether it's public.
