## Claim
Once you're hiring multiple people per week, take advantage of new hire cohorts — help them bond like a freshman class. The cross-team connections they form become the tissue that holds departments together as you scale.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Loftesness: "At a certain scale, you're growing so fast that you might have five to 10 or more employees starting in a given week. Take advantage of these new hire cohorts and help them to bond like a freshman class at college. Those cohorts will likely end up in different departments, and they will form and facilitate connections and empathy."

## Why (if explicit in source)
People who start together share a common reference point. When they end up in different teams, those personal connections bridge team boundaries naturally — reducing the friction of cross-team collaboration without needing formal processes.
