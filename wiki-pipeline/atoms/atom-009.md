## Claim
If your books are more than 4 weeks behind, you are flying blind — you cannot make good hiring or spending decisions without knowing where you actually stand.

## Source quote or paraphrase
Multiple HN commenters describe not having a month-end close ritual as meaning their books were 2-3 months behind. One commenter: "we didn't know our burn rate to within 20% until Series A — that's terrifying in retrospect."

## Why (if explicit in source)
Spending and hiring decisions made without accurate financials tend to be based on bank balance, which is a lagging and misleading indicator of financial health.
