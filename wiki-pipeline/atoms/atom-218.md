## Claim

You should treat communication norms as **stage-scoped decisions** between practitioner schools — not a fixed channel manual — and write down only what your stage requires: one-liners at foundation, a one-page norm at first hires when remote or cross-role, then escalation and artifact rules at early revenue and beyond.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Synthesis of src-042–046: schools split on Slack-forward vs evergreen-first and on handbook depth; agreement on chat≠memory and minimum written norms when team complexity rises. No source agrees on a single headcount threshold — use stage bands and triggers (remote, cross-team, hires outside founding context).

## Why

Deep channel taxonomy duplicates Basecamp/GitLab/Vohra; this framework’s value is **when** to decide **what**, and which school fits your context. Granular atoms atom-192–217 support audit trail; this super-atom is the Phase 2 feedstock for a thin wiki.

## Decisions by stage (embedded)

**Foundation:** chat tool + urgent vs non-urgent — oral OK.

**First hires (decide):** channel list; public vs DM; what leaves chat; response time; owners/SLAs for CS paths. Schools: Slack-forward vs evergreen-first; one-page vs coach-only. Middle: one page if remote or ≥3 non-founders; three non-negotiables (chat≠memory, codify decisions, public default for ops).

**Early revenue (decide):** written guidelines; cross-team escalation; incident lane; light artifact map; email for external/formal. Organic school breaks here without written paths.

**Growth (decide):** playbook, SLA tiers, escalation matrix, review cadence. Prefer living doc + tickets over handbook sprawl.

**Scaled:** full architecture — reference external playbooks, don’t replicate in wiki.
