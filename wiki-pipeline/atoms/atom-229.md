## Claim

You should run exit interviews with a consistent question set, conducted by a neutral party (founder, ops lead, or external advisor) — not by the departing employee's direct manager alone.

## Source quote or paraphrase

OpenOrg separates exit interview (data gathering by People Ops or neutral third party) from manager closure conversation (appreciation). Tinyteam agrees: skip-level or HR gets more candid answers.

## Why

At first hires the manager often *is* the reason someone leaves. A neutral interviewer surfaces patterns across departures that inform how you hire and manage — the early-revenue evolution of this process.
