## Claim

In the Netherlands, you are legally required to continue paying a sick employee for up to two years — budget for this from the moment you make your first hire.

## Source quote or paraphrase

Dutch law (Wet verbetering poortwachter / WVP) entitles employees to continued pay during illness for up to two years: typically 70% in year 2, though many employers pay 100% in year 1. The employer bears this cost directly unless covered by insurance (AOV/WGA-ERD).

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

Unlike most countries where sick leave is government-funded after a short period, Dutch employers are the first and primary payer for two full years. For a 5-person company, one long-term sick employee can represent 20% of payroll cost with no revenue contribution — a material financial risk that is widely underestimated at the first-hires stage.
