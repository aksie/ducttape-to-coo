## Claim

In the Netherlands, a RI&E (risico-inventarisatie en -evaluatie) is legally required from the moment you hire your first employee — including for a small office of 2–10 people. At ≤25 employees, you can do a simplified version yourself without a certified safety expert.

## Source quote or paraphrase

KVK (Dutch Chamber of Commerce): "Een RI&E is verplicht voor alle werkgevers die één of meer personen in dienst hebben." (A RI&E is required for all employers with one or more employees.) Companies with ≤25 employees and ≤40 total employee hours/week, or those using a recognised branch-specific RI&E instrument, are exempt from requiring professional review of the assessment.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

The Arbowet (Dutch Working Conditions Act) holds employers responsible for identifying and mitigating workplace risks from day one. The Dutch Labour Inspectorate (Nederlandse Arbeidsinspectie) actively enforces this; failing to have a RI&E can result in a fine and reduced insurance coverage. For a small office, the risks are typically low (ergonomics, emergency exit, first aid kit) but the legal obligation exists regardless of size or sector. The RI&E is also the anchor for the verplichte preventiemedewerker (mandatory safety officer), which the founder can fulfill themselves at ≤25 employees.
