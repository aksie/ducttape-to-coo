## Claim

Using a freelance platform (Fiverr, Upwork, etc.) does not automatically give you IP ownership over the work produced. If a contractor touches code, content, or creative output, you need a separately signed agreement with an explicit IP assignment clause.

## Source quote or paraphrase

"They're on a platform" (Fiverr, Upwork, etc.) is not a substitute — the platform's standard terms may not give you full IP ownership. Every freelancer/contractor who touches code, content, or creative output needs a signed agreement with explicit IP assignment language.

## Why

Platform terms are written to protect the platform, not the commissioning company. IP ownership defaults vary by platform and by jurisdiction. A buyer conducting IP due diligence will ask for evidence of assignment from every contributor to core IP. If that evidence doesn't exist, the IP ownership is uncertain — which is a material risk for any technology or content business.
