## Claim

Signing a traditional multi-year office lease when you are at 2–10 people is one of the most common and expensive early-stage mistakes. Lease for your current headcount, not your projected headcount.

## Source quote or paraphrase

gable.to (vendor, flagged): "A founder raises $10M, projects 50 hires in 18 months, and signs a five-year lease for 8,000 square feet. Twelve months later, they've hired 25 people, not 50. They're paying for 4,000 square feet of empty space." 75% of traditional commercial leases lock in for 3–5 years. Corroborated by startuprise.co.uk (real estate advisory, also vendor): companies consistently model space against best-case hiring plans and skip the scenario where that plan runs 6–9 months late.

## Why (inferred from source if not explicit)

At First Hires stage (2–10 people), the trajectory of headcount and revenue is fundamentally uncertain. A 3–5 year lease converts a variable cost into a fixed one at exactly the stage where optionality has the most value. Every square foot of empty space is cash that cannot go to product, hiring, or runway extension. The mistake is compounded by the difficulty of subleasing or exiting a lease: in a soft real estate market, the stuck tenant pays for space they cannot use and cannot exit. The fix is to stay in coworking or short-term (6–12 month) subleases until the hiring trajectory is demonstrated, not projected.
