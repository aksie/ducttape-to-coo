## Claim

If your team uses shared admin logins or shared passwords that are not rotated when someone leaves, you are exposed — even if you disabled their personal email account.

## Source quote or paraphrase

HN thread: shared passwords must rotate when anyone with access leaves; generic admin credentials called out in NCS case as epic security failure; most companies skip rotation because people seem trustworthy.

## Why

Personal account disable does not touch credentials the person already knew. Shared admin accounts mean one departure can compromise every system that login touches.
