# Internal Communication about Strategy — Growth (~50–150 people)

> Information cascades up to leadership long before it reliably cascades down. At growth stage, repeating the strategy story is operational work — not a side project after planning.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Strategy, priorities, and context cascade down on the same rhythm as planning** — after they flow up through reporting. Upward articulation in the management team is necessary but not sufficient; teams need a downward transmission on the planning calendar.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **Founders and executives expect to repeat the same strategy story far more often than feels natural.** In a ~fifty-person company, each person may hear it once a year while the leader feels they repeat it weekly — that gap is under-communication for the organisation, not over-communication by the leader.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
- **Leadership articulates strategy precisely upward, but teams below execute on a stale or partial picture.** Management-team clarity does not substitute for team-level transmission — if the org works from a plan six months old while the investor deck is current, cascade down is broken.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
- **Strategy lives in the founder's head and the investor deck while the organisation runs on an outdated plan.** Deck updates without operational cascade produce silent misalignment across functions.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

### Process entry

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
- **Nobody owns downward transmission on the planning rhythm — so the planning job is not finished.** Treating internal comms as separate from strategy (instead of a direct derivative of it) leaves a gap that shows up as teams executing on context the leadership assumed was shared.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->
