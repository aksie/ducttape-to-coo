# Internal Communication about Strategy — First Hires (2–10 people)

> At first hires the strategy story still travels by repetition in a small room — not by cascade machinery. The failure mode is assuming that scales without naming the shift.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Founders still tell the strategy story often enough that the early team shares one mental model.** Repeated narrative at this scale replaces formal cascade machinery — osmosis works when everyone is still in the same conversation often enough.

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **Multi-founder teams explain direction slightly differently to their functions** — creating unspoken frustration and uncertainty before anyone names the misalignment. Inconsistent strategy stories compound as functions grow.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

### Process entry

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
- **Internal comms is treated as separate from strategy and nobody owns transmission.** A practitioner view at this stage: internal comms is a derivative of strategy, not a parallel workstream — if nobody owns telling the story, cascade will fail when headcount grows.
