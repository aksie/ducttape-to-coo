# Shareholder & Investor Reporting — First Hires (2–10 people)

> Written reporting is for **you** first — external investors are an audience layer on a habit that keeps you honest. Start monthly before you have shareholders; widen distribution when angels arrive.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **A monthly written update runs on calendar cadence** — even pre-funding, even if recipients are only you and the leadership team. Same structure you will use for investors later.
  <!-- sources: src-058 (practitioner contribution, human:stefanverkerk) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **Each update scores last period's goals in writing** — tied to your [quarterly goals](../../../templates/docs/quarterly-goals.md) doc or the goals you stated in the prior update — with honest notes on what went differently than expected.
  <!-- sources: src-058, src-059 (template synthesis) -->

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
1. **Start now — send the first edition to yourself or co-founders.** Use the [investor update template](../../../templates/rendered/investor-update.html): highlights, **misses/help up front**, checkbox lookback, checkbox lookahead (product / growth / finance). Quarterly runway and scenario sections can wait.
   <!-- sources: src-058, src-059, templates/docs/investor-update.md -->

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
2. **On lookback, explain the delta — not just checkboxes.** For each goal: what you did, what outcome differed from plan, what you learned. Performing confidence is not the point; documenting reality is.
   <!-- sources: src-058 (human:stefanverkerk) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
3. **When angels or F&F investors are on the cap table, add them to the same email** — do not invent a new format under fundraising pressure. Keep monthly short form; add quarterly depth (runway, burn, scenarios) once per quarter.
   <!-- sources: src-058, src-059, processes.json 1.2b stageFocus -->

<!-- claim-id: c-006 -->
4. **Name explicit asks when you need help** — warm intro, customer lead, advisor — with a pasteable blurb investors can forward. Updates that only report good news train investors to ignore you when you actually need something.
   <!-- sources: src-059 (template synthesis) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Founder / key-person time

<!-- claim-id: c-007 -->
- **Investor alignment happens only in coffees and calls** — you have said "ships in six weeks" three times without a written record of the slip. Verbal optimism without lookback lets you kid yourself.
  <!-- sources: src-058 (human:stefanverkerk) -->

### Process entry

<!-- claim-id: c-008 -->
- **You cannot point to last month's stated goals and how you scored against them.** Highlights-only emails are a newsletter, not a reporting process.
  <!-- sources: atom-257, src-059 -->

## How this evolves next

<!-- claim-id: c-009 -->
- **At early revenue**, reporting adds board prep (deck, pre-read), cap table hygiene, and coordination with legal on shareholder matters — the monthly email habit continues; governance layers on top.
  <!-- sources: processes.json 1.2b stageFocus early-revenue, atom-258 -->

## Context variants

### If you are bootstrapped (no external investors yet)

<!-- claim-id: c-010 -->
- **Audience is internal only — that is correct.** Leadership team or founder-only distribution still counts. The discipline is writing the lookback; investors are not the reason to start.
  <!-- sources: src-058 (human:stefanverkerk) -->

### If you have angel or friends & family investors

<!-- claim-id: c-011 -->
- **Same monthly template, wider BCC.** Key metrics, progress against quarterly goals, what you need help with — keep it simple per `processes.json` default stageFocus.
  <!-- sources: processes.json 1.2b stageFocus, src-058 -->

### If you are venture-backed (seed / Series A)

<!-- claim-id: c-012 -->
- **Monthly investor email plus quarterly board meeting** — 5–10 slide deck, pre-read 48h ahead, written board minutes. Cap table in Carta, Pulley, or Ledgy from day one of the round. Information rights in the term sheet usually require this cadence.
  <!-- sources: processes.json 1.2b variants venture-early, atom-258 -->
