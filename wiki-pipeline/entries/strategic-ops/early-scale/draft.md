# Company-Level Planning & Goals — Early Scale (~25 people)

> At early scale, a single company goal list is not enough. Goals need owners, layers, and a cascade that connects each level to the one above.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
<!-- atoms: atom-078-gitlab -->
- **Each organizational layer should have objectives that relate to the key results above it.** Once functions and managers appear, people need to see who owns which part of the outcome and how their layer contributes. A goal cascade connects the company's priorities to team-level work.

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
<!-- atoms: atom-079-gitlab -->
- **Prefix key results with an owner so each part of the company goal has a visible directly responsible person.** Ownership makes goals operational — if nobody is named on a key result, it is probably not going to happen.

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
<!-- atoms: atom-079-gitlab -->
- **Accept that lower-level key results do not have to match higher-level goals perfectly.** Imperfect matching keeps the cascade practical instead of turning planning into taxonomy work. If the cascade must be perfect, people spend more time aligning than executing.

## Warning signs you're behind

> Warning signs for this cell should be triangulated with another operator source. At present, only single-company OKR handbook evidence is available.

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
<!-- atoms: atom-078-gitlab (inferred) -->
- **Teams cannot explain how their work connects to company goals, or they claim everything is important.** Without a visible cascade, each team optimizes for its own priorities, not the company's.

### Founder / key-person time

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
<!-- atoms: atom-079-gitlab (inferred) -->
- **The founder or a single ops person is manually connecting every team goal back to company strategy in spreadsheets or documents.** If the cascade is not explicit, someone has to do the integration work — and it scales poorly.

## How this evolves next

<!-- placeholder: no evolution atoms in this batch for early-scale -->
At Growth (~50–150 people), planning becomes a cadence system across time horizons — week, month, quarter, year, strategy, vision, and mission. OKRs are necessary but not enough once recurring company rituals multiply.
