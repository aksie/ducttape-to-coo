# Company-Level Planning & Goals — Growth (~50–150 people)

> At growth stage, planning becomes an operating rhythm across multiple time horizons — not just a quarterly goal list.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
<!-- atoms: atom-080-gitlab -->
- **Planning operates as a cadence system across time horizons: week, month, quarter, year, strategy, vision, and mission.** Growth adds recurring planning events that cannot be coordinated from a single quarterly goal list. Cadence turns planning into an operating rhythm.

### Process entry

- **The company only does planning once per quarter and everything else is ad hoc.** Without a cadence, important planning events (strategy review, vision check, annual planning) get squeezed out by the urgency of the quarter.

## How this evolves next

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
<!-- atoms: atom-081-gitlab -->
- **OKRs need to sit inside a broader company cadence with board meetings, sales targets, metrics reviews, yearly planning, strategy, vision, and mission.** OKRs coordinate goals, but they do not coordinate every recurring management process. A growing company needs both the goal system and the operating calendar to fit together.
