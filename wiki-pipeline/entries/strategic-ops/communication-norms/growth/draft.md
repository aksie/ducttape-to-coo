# Operational Internal Communication Norms — Growth (~50–150 people)

> At growth stage, informal access to the CEO feels helpful and fast — until two people in the same meeting have different amounts of the information. Formal routes are how you protect line-manager authority without killing speed.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Information routes aim for roughly 80% formal and 20% informal** — a practitioner target at this scale, not a measured benchmark. Formal routes distribute context evenly; unbounded informal routing creates unequal information in one room.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **Informal routes (walk-ins, skip-level) are allowed but bounded** — informal access preserves speed and trust when it does not become the default bypass around line managers.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
- **Streams that would otherwise hit the founder ad hoc have a named owner** — legal and commercial "quick yes/no" questions are the classic case, with a thin layer between small daily asks and CEO intuition.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
- **Two people in the same C-level meeting hold 100% versus 40% of the relevant information** — a decision gets pressed before everyone is ready, and resentment follows. Unequal information in decision forums produces outcomes that feel coerced.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

### Process entry

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
- **In long-tenure organic companies, informal routes can reach ~40%** — people go to the CEO when the line manager is slow or says no. The CEO feels helpful and decisive; line manager authority is undercut without anyone meaning to.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-006 -->
- **Commercial or legal staff bypass managers for speed** — "CEO said yes in the corridor" trains the org to route around line authority. Speed today erodes decision-rights structure tomorrow.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->
