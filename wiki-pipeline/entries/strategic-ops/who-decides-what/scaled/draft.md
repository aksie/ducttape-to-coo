# Who Decides What — Scaled (150+ people)

> At scaled stage, founder intuition still drives the company — suppressing it loses the role. Channel it with process steps instead of smoothing it away.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Process steps wrap founder intuition rather than replacing it** — a shower insight becomes a staged test, not a company-wide pivot by Monday. Founder energy drives the company; the operator's job is to channel it, not remove it.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **When conflict rises, use mediator-style neutral questions to surface what is unsaid** — practitioner-trained mediation helps; it does not replace willingness to reflect.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Founder / key-person time

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
- **An operator tries to smooth all founder erratic energy away** — and loses founder trust or the role. The energy that built the company needs somewhere to go.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner) -->

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
- **Task-driven, low-empathy founders who believe the best facts win and will not move without full conviction stall a growing org** — fine for product decisions, brutal for organisational disagreement. Mediation helps but does not replace willingness to reflect.
