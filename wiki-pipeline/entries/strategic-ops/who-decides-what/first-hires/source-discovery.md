# Source discovery — 1.4 Who Decides What @ First Hires

**Wiki stub:** [1.4--first-hires](https://aksie.github.io/ducttape-to-coo/wiki.html#1.4--first-hires)  
**`stageFocus` (processes.json):** Write down what needs founder approval (hiring, major spending, pivots). Everything else, team decides. Practice letting go.

**Stage-fit lens:** At 2–10 people this is **not** RACI/SPADE bureaucracy — it is a one-page “founder approval list” plus practising delegation before early-revenue makes re-litigation painful. Deprioritise growth/scaled frameworks unless they explicitly address sub-25 headcount.

**Second pass (jun 2026):** User asked for the **soft side** — afstemmen van verantwoordelijkheden tussen founders op **talent, voorkeur en een echt gesprek**, vóór of naast harde approval-lijsten. Die bronnen zitten in **Track B** hieronder; veel is foundation-adjacent maar blijft relevant zolang het besluitvormende kernteam uit founders bestaat.

**Praktische synthese Track B (voor Phase 2):**

1. **AOR-oefening** (Lin): product, eng, sales, ops, finance, hiring — score skill + passie → wie “default owner” is per cluster.
2. **Founder sync** (Buechler): wekelijks, agenda de hele week vullen; level-2/3 gesprekken (relatie + strategie).
3. **Eén pagina output:** “Founder A owns X by default / Founder B owns Y” + **gezamenlijke** lijst: hiring, grote spend, pivot → beide of CEO.
4. **Overlap norm** (HN 43818415): geen rigide silo’s — founders blijven in elkaars domein leesbaar.
5. **Herziening:** rollen veranderen na PMF en bij eerste niet-founder hires (Lin vraag; Lockett cases).

### Track B-fin — Persoonlijke runway & levenscontext (bepaalt initieel financieel pad)

**Praktische synthese B-fin (voor Phase 2):**

1. **Per founder uitspreken:** maanden persoonlijke runway (spaargeld ÷ vaste lasten); partnerinkomen ja/nee; minimum salaris; full-time nu of trigger (omzet €X / funding €Y).
2. **Kortste runway wint voor bedrijfsstrategie** — niet als zwakte, als constraint: hiring-tempo, founder-salaris, sales-druk, bootstrap vs raise.
3. **Asymmetrie benoemen** (Wasserman): wie kan langer ramen-level; wie drijft “we moeten nu monetizen/raisen” — anders conflict op kleine beslissingen.
4. **Koppeling aan rollen:** founder met korte runway + sales-passie ≠ automatisch “jij own sales” als dat burn versnelt; founder met lange runway kan interim CFO/ops dragen zonder salaris.
5. **Cross-ref:** `wiki/processes/legal/4.1--foundation.md` (money in, co-founder scenarios); **2.1** (company cash) — beide in founder sync, niet verwarren.

**Ook relevant maar lagere prioriteit:** [Confidante 9 hot-button conversations](https://confidante.substack.com/p/partnership-agreement-cofounders) (working style Venn, shared decision Venn); [Under30CEO complementary skills](https://www.under30ceo.com/how-to-choose-a-co-founder-with-complementary-skills/) (awkward early conversation > later conflict).

## Recommended synthesis direction (for Phase 2)

**Two layers @ first hires (recommended wiki structure):**

| Layer | What | Sources |
|-------|------|---------|
| **1. Founder circle (soft)** | Drie assen: (a) skill + passie (AOR), (b) **persoonlijke runway & levenscontext**, (c) founder sync — dan pas domein-ownership | Track B + B-fin (B1–B4, B8–B9, B13–B17) |
| **2. Team-wide (harder)** | Approval-lijst: hiring, spend >€X, pivot → founder OK; rest naar hire met mandaat | Track A + **3.2** |

**Home message @ first hires:** founders stemmen eerst **onderling** af wie wat wil en kan dragen; daarna één zichtbare lijst voor wat **altijd** founder-approval nodig heeft. Niet omgekeerd.

**Schools to preserve (like 1.3):**

| School | First hires position |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Talent + passion mapping** | AOR exercise — ownership by energy, not job title |
| **Personal runway first** | Per-founder maanden zonder salaris + vaste lasten → bootstrap vs raise vs ramen; kortste runway = bedrijfsconstraint |
| **Financial asymmetry** | Spaargeld/partnerinkomen vs krap — expliciet of kleine uitgaven worden subsidie-conflict |
| **Weekly founder sync** | Relationship + strategy alignment before team process |
| **Overlap > silos** | Founders stay multi-disciplinary; strict split is a red flag |
| **Founder still decides most** | Defensible at 5 people if documented |
| **Delegate projects early** | Basecamp / Shape Up — boundaries + team ownership |
| **Single decider per decision** | Light DRI naming in meetings, not a matrix |
| **Middle ground (wiki default)** | Founder conversation → written approval list → practise letting go |

**Warning signs to extract:** founder bottleneck on trivial decisions; team waiting for OK; decisions re-opened because founder wasn't in the room; **founders never had the roles conversation** (assumed from titles); passion/skill mismatch (high skill, hates sales, still “owns” sales); **personal runway never discussed** (één founder stopt/freelancet na 4 maanden); ongelijke financiële druk → premature fundraise of ruzie over uitgaven/salaris.
