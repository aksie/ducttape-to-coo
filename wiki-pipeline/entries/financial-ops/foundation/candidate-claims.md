## Candidate claims from GitLab Era 1

### c-gitlab-001: Early expense policy can start as a principle
- Section: what_good_looks_like
- Supporting atoms: atom-067-gitlab, atom-071-gitlab
- Candidate claim: At the foundation stage, your expense policy can be principle-based: spend company money carefully, but do not require pre-approval for every useful work expense.
- Synthesis notes: Strong GitLab-specific evidence for a small, trust-based team. Needs triangulation before becoming general guidance.

### c-gitlab-002: Add a lightweight escalation norm
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-068-gitlab
- Candidate claim: Tell people what to do when they are unsure: inform a manager before the purchase or as soon as possible afterward.
- Synthesis notes: Useful because it preserves autonomy while creating a backstop for ambiguous expenses.

### c-gitlab-003: Keep minimal financial hygiene even in duct-tape mode
- Section: warning_signs
- Supporting atoms: atom-069-gitlab
- Candidate claim: Trust-based spending still needs deadlines for expense reports and fast correction of accidental personal spend on company cards.
- Synthesis notes: This is the control edge of the early GitLab policy.

### c-gitlab-004: Reimburse the work setup before optimizing procurement
- Section: tools_resources
- Supporting atoms: atom-070-gitlab
- Candidate claim: For an early remote team, reimburse the basic equipment and services people need to work effectively before introducing procurement machinery.
- Synthesis notes: Especially relevant for remote-first companies; may need qualification for office-first teams.
