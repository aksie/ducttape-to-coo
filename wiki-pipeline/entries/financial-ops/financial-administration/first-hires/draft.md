# Financial Administration — First Hires (2–10 people)

> With employees come payroll tax obligations and more invoices. Your external bookkeeper should run the close; your job is to feed them clean, timely data.

## What good looks like at this stage

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Month-end close runs through your bookkeeper on a agreed rhythm, and you never fall more than a month behind on inputs.** Receipts, outgoing invoices, and payroll records reach finance on schedule. The books reflect reality without the founder doing reconciliation.
  <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
1. **File payroll tax (loonheffing) returns and correspondence in `/finance/tax/loonheffing/`.** From first hire this is a recurring obligation. Store each declaration and any Belastingdienst letters — buyers and auditors ask for them.
   <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
2. **Agree a monthly rhythm with your bookkeeper: what you send, by when, and what you get back.** Lock a simple checklist (receipts in, invoices out, payroll export) and run it every month without exception.
   <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
- **Payroll records and tax filings live only in your bookkeeper's portal with no copy in your company filing structure.** If the relationship ends or access is lost, you cannot respond to a due diligence request in days — only in weeks.
  <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) | flags: unverified -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Early Revenue, annual accounts filing and the management-to-statutory bridge become the priority.** Tax folders expand (VPB, BTW, WBSO). Formal financial administration starts to look like a transaction-ready data room, not just bookkeeping hygiene.
  <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->
