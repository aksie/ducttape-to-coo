# Financial Administration — Early Revenue (~10–30 people)

> At early revenue, formal financial administration means your statutory accounts, tax filings, and management reporting can be explained — by you, quickly, without reconstructing years of decisions under deal pressure.

## What good looks like at this stage

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **You maintain a one-page bridge between management accounts and statutory annual accounts.** It explains revenue recognition differences, EBITDA adjustments, and non-recurring items. Update it when you prepare annual accounts — not when a buyer asks.
  <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **Signed annual accounts for the last five years live in a known folder, filed with KvK on time.** Auditors sign off without surprises. Tax filings (VPB, BTW, loonheffing) and correspondence are complete and retrievable.
  <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
1. **File signed annual accounts as PDFs within two weeks of AGM sign-off.** Path: `/finance/annual-accounts/[year]-annual-accounts.pdf`. Keep at least five years accessible.
   <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
2. **[NL] File WBSO applications and decisions in `/compliance/grants-and-subsidies/wbso/`.** These support both tax due diligence and your R&D narrative. Missing WBSO files are a common gap that is slow to fix later.
   <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
3. **Document financial systems (one paragraph per tool) and internal controls.** Who does what in finance, four-eyes approval above a payment threshold, authorised signatory list, bank accounts with access documented securely.
   <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-006 -->
- **The management-to-statutory bridge does not exist and would take weeks to reconstruct.** This is consistently the most time-consuming due diligence item — and the easiest to maintain if you write it once a year when accounts are fresh.
  <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

## How this evolves next

<!-- claim-id: c-007 -->
- **At Growth, archive monthly management reports (18+ months) and treat the bridge as non-optional.** Statutory and management views diverge more as complexity grows; buyers expect a time series, not only the current year.
  <!-- sources: src-039 (practitioner contribution) -->

## Tools & resources

- Exact, Zenvoices, Human Wave / AFAS / Nmbrs — document what you use in one paragraph each
- Belastingdienst portal: ensure more than one person has access, not only the bookkeeper
- Compliance calendar in Google Sheets: VPB, BTW, KvK, WBSO windows, audit dates
