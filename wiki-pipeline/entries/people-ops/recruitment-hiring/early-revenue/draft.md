# Recruitment & Hiring — Early Revenue

> Hiring at scale needs an approval chain, not just interview scorecards. Budget → approved role → post → offer → contract → payroll. Signing authority and templates live under 4.1 Governance & Corporate and 3.1 HR Administration — this page owns the hiring steps between them.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Every open role has a documented approval before it goes public: budget check, management agreement on title and level, and an indicative salary range** (junior, senior, manager, VP, executive). Interview process and scorecards run on top of that — not instead of it.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **Offer letters and labour contracts only leave the building after finance sign-off and from authorised senders.** CFO or COO confirms each offer amount fits the approved band. Head of HR or CFO/COO sends the contract from the lawyer-reviewed template.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
- **HR receives the signed contract before the start date.** Payroll and insurance are active on day one — "hired" means finance has the paperwork, not that a manager shook hands.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
1. **Before posting a job opening, confirm budget and get management agreement on the role.** Record title, level, and indicative salary range on a headcount sheet, hiring tracker, or approval email. No LinkedIn or job-board post without that row filled in.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
2. **Run your interview process and scorecards as today** — but treat an approved role as a gate: if the profile changes materially, go back for a new approval before continuing.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) | flags: unverified -->

<!-- claim-id: c-006 -->
3. **Before sending an offer letter, get CFO or COO OK on the specific salary** within the band agreed at step 1. The offer is not a manager-side formality — it is the finance checkpoint.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-007 -->
4. **Issue the labour contract only from the standard template, sent by head of HR or CFO/COO** — not a hiring manager's edited Word file. The signer must have documented authority per 4.1 Governance & Corporate.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-008 -->
5. **Hand the signed contract to HR before the start date** so payroll and insurance are set up. File in `/06-hr/employment-contracts/[name]/` per 3.1 HR Administration.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry

<!-- claim-id: c-009 -->
- **Job openings are live but nobody can name who approved the budget or salary band for that role.** You are recruiting on manager urgency, not on a headcount plan.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-010 -->
- **Someone is "hired" on a self-edited contract with a salary that was never approved, signed by a person without signing authority.** Payroll, insurance, and diligence will all flag it — often weeks later.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

### Founder / key-person time

<!-- claim-id: c-011 -->
- **CFO or CEO discovers a new hire is working but not on payroll or insured** because the contract never reached HR. Retroactive fixes burn finance and leadership time under pressure.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

## How this evolves next

<!-- claim-id: c-012 -->
- **At Growth, formalise the chain in an ATS or hiring tracker** — approval fields, offer band, contract template version, and HR handoff status visible to finance and leadership without chasing email threads.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) | flags: unverified -->

## Tools & resources

<!-- claim-id: c-013 -->
- **Hiring tracker columns (minimum):** role, level, approved salary band, budget OK (Y/N + approver), offer amount, offer approver (CFO/COO), contract sent by, contract filed in HR (date).
