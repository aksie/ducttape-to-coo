# Recruitment & Hiring — First Hires

> Your first structured hires still need lightweight gates: agree the role is in budget before you post, and do not let managers send their own contracts. Full approval chain detail at Early Revenue; signing authority under 4.1 Governance & Corporate.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Basic job posts with 2–3 interview rounds, scorecards, and reference checks** — plus an emailed OK from CFO that the role fits budget and a rough salary band before the post goes live.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **Offers and contracts do not leave the founder, CFO, or HR filing owner without explicit OK** — no self-edited contracts, no salaries that were never agreed.
  <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
1. **Before posting on AngelList, LinkedIn, or your network, agree as founders (or with CFO) that this role is in budget** and note a rough salary band by level (junior, senior, manager). One line in a shared doc is enough at this stage.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
2. **Run 2–3 interview rounds with different people; use a simple scorecard; always check references.**
   <!-- sources: processes.json stageFocus | flags: unverified -->

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
3. **Before any offer goes out, founder or CFO confirms the amount fits what you agreed when you opened the role.**
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-006 -->
4. **Send the labour contract from your lawyer-reviewed template** — founder, CFO, or designated HR owner — not an edited attachment from the hiring manager.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain), src-048 (Contract signing authority) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-007 -->
5. **Get the signed contract to HR before day one** so payroll and insurance are live. A handshake hire without paperwork is how people end up working uninsured.
   <!-- sources: src-060 (Practitioner — Hiring approval chain) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry
