# Recruitment & Hiring — Growth (~50–150 people)

> At growth stage you start hiring senior leaders and C-level roles. One person's "not a fit" stops working — split the interviews, remove solo vetoes, and let three yeses decide.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Senior hires go through three separate conversations — and no single interviewer has a veto.** The hiring manager checks fit and chemistry. Someone one level up checks potential only (live case, whiteboard — not a final hire/no-hire). A long-tenure culture carrier checks values over coffee. Each gives a signal; none can kill the hire alone.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner), atom-388, atom-389 -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **The hire happens when all three conversations spark yes — not when one person says "not a fit."** A binary culture veto from one interviewer is too subjective once you are hiring heads and executives at scale.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner), atom-389 -->

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
1. **Run the three conversations in order — with different jobs for each round.** (1) **Hiring manager** — role fit, hard criteria, chemistry: did this meeting give you energy? (2) **One level up** — **potential only**: unprepared problem, whiteboard, live case. Output is a potential estimate, not hire/no-hire — **no veto**. (3) **Long-tenure culture carrier** — life, work, values over coffee. Also **no solo veto**. The three sparks together decide.
   <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner), atom-388 -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Process entry

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
- **A culture or values interview ends in a binary "not a fit" with veto power.** One subjective no blocks senior hires that the hiring manager and executive team would otherwise make — and the process stops scaling with you.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner), atom-391 -->

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
- **You expect a specialist hire (e.g. CRO, CFO, Head of CS) to be friends with every other department head.** The bar is manager chemistry and values alignment — not universal friendship across the leadership team.
  <!-- sources: src-064 (anonymous-practitioner), atom-392 -->
