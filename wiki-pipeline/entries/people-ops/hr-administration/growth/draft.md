# HR Administration — Growth (~30–100 people)

> At growth, HR due diligence in a transaction is a major workload. Maintained filing throughout is a competitive advantage; scrambling during a live process is not.

## What good looks like at this stage

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **HR filing has a single source of truth — HRIS or folders, not both in parallel without rules.** Employment contracts, freelance agreements, dispute records, and works council documentation (if applicable) are complete and retrievable.
  <!-- sources: src-040 (practitioner contribution) -->

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
1. **When adopting an HRIS, migrate fully and designate it as the source of truth.** Do not maintain parallel folder and system copies without explicit rules — inconsistencies surface immediately in due diligence.
   <!-- sources: src-040 (practitioner contribution) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Founder / key-person time

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
- **You cannot produce an org chart with compensation, full contract set, dispute history, and CAO status within days of a buyer request.** HR due diligence at this stage is significant; preparing from scratch during a transaction is extremely costly and signals weak operations.
  <!-- sources: src-040 (practitioner contribution) -->

## How this evolves next

- **At Scaled, HR administration becomes a dedicated function with automated workflows for contracts, onboarding, offboarding, and compliance audits.**
  <!-- sources: src-040 (practitioner contribution) -->
