## Candidate claims from startup legal interview (src-041)

*Promoted to `draft.md` as c-007–c-018 (pending review in approval tool). This file is the audit record of the src-041 synthesis batch.*

### c-src041-f-001: Light co-founder paper before traction
- Section: what_good_looks_like
- Supporting atoms: atom-152, atom-153
- Candidate claim: **Co-founders have talked through the basics on paper — roles, capital, exit triggers — without heavyweight legal docs.** You are building and testing demand, not playing company with notaries and lawyers before a business exists. Zero paper among co-builders is as foolish as an 80-page shareholder agreement pre-PMF.
- Synthesis notes: Core interview theme. Complements c-001 (filing) with pre-incorporation behaviour. NL/EU context.

### c-src041-f-002: Agree when you will formalise
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-154
- Candidate claim: **Block an evening to agree co-founder scenarios and commit to a formalisation trigger.** Roles, money in, what happens if it fails — then explicitly: once we see traction, we incorporate, transfer IP, and document founder terms.
- Synthesis notes: "Beer napkin" step from interview. Actionable and stage-appropriate.

### c-src041-f-003: Incorporate on traction, not on the idea
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-159, atom-191
- Candidate claim: **Incorporate when you have real momentum — users, revenue, or serious investor or partner interest — not on day one of the idea.** Escalate legal work in step with real risk (traction, people, money, data), not founder anxiety.
- Synthesis notes: atom-191 (why) folded into explanation. May need edit vs existing c-004 timing ("as soon as possible" on IP) — reconcile at review: IP transfer at incorporation, not necessarily at idea stage.

### c-src041-f-004: All IP in the entity at setup
- Section: what_good_looks_like
- Supporting atoms: atom-160, atom-161
- Candidate claim: **A clean entity exists and all IP — code, brand, product, content — sits in the company, including work from the pre-BV period.** Execute an IP assignment from founders as part of incorporation; not "mostly," not "except the first commits."
- Synthesis notes: Reinforces published c-004 with interview emphasis on pre-BV commits. Strong practitioner signal.

### c-src041-f-005: Keep the structure boring
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-163, atom-167
- Candidate claim: **Keep your corporate structure simple — standard BV setup, no exotic holdings from networking advice.** Complexity is where expensive fixes live; advisors regularly see founders locked into arrangements they do not understand.
- Synthesis notes: Pairs with c-src041-f-006 (exotic structures warning).

### c-src041-f-006: Quarterly legal/ops check-in
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-181, atom-182
- Candidate claim: **Once a quarter, block an hour to walk employment papers, customer/supplier contracts, privacy, tech/data architecture, and tax/compliance against how the business actually operates.** A fixed rhythm replaces oscillating between "we have nothing" panic and "we don't need this" denial.
- Synthesis notes: Cross-stage habit; starts at foundation. Carries forward to early-revenue.

### c-src041-f-007: Over-legalising before PMF
- Section: warning_signs
- Warning category: founder_time
- Supporting atoms: atom-155, atom-156, atom-186
- Candidate claim: **You commissioned extensive founder legal work or spend disproportionate time with notaries and lawyers before anyone will pay for the product.** Legal structure is eating energy that should go to product and customers — you are timmering everything shut before learning if the idea works.
- Synthesis notes: Distinct from filing-structure warnings (c-005). Interview-specific anti-pattern.

### c-src041-f-008: Co-building with zero written understanding
- Section: warning_signs
- Warning category: process_entry
- Supporting atoms: atom-158
- Candidate claim: **Two co-founders are building together with no written understanding of roles, capital contribution, or exit triggers.** Verbal-only deals become expensive once money, IP, or investors appear.
- Synthesis notes: Pre-incorporation gap not covered by src-038.

### c-src041-f-009: IP still with founders after incorporation
- Section: warning_signs
- Warning category: process_entry
- Supporting atoms: atom-165
- Candidate claim: **After incorporation, meaningful IP, code, or brand assets still sit with individual founders.** Do not assume "IP never killed a deal" — this drives unclean cap tables and due-diligence pain.
- Synthesis notes: Evolves published c-004 from action into active warning sign.

### c-src041-f-010: Exotic cap or holding structures
- Section: warning_signs
- Warning category: process_entry
- Supporting atoms: atom-167
- Candidate claim: **Your structure includes exotic arrangements one founder adopted from informal advice without understanding them.** These become expensive to explain, fix, or defend in diligence.
- Synthesis notes: Interview anecdote pattern (borrel advice). geographically_nl optional flag at review.

### c-src041-f-011: Blind panic or denial on legal
- Section: warning_signs
- Warning category: founder_time
- Supporting atoms: atom-188
- Candidate claim: **You swing between blind panic about legal gaps and blanket denial that documentation is needed — with no structured check-in.** Unstructured emotional response leads to wasted spend or DD surprises.
- Synthesis notes: Diagnostic-framework connection from interview. Meta warning for product/tech founders.

### c-src041-f-012: Standard founder agreement templates
- Section: tools_resources
- Supporting atoms: atom-164
- Candidate claim: **Use a reputable standard founder or shareholder agreement template as the foundation for early founder terms** — adapt to your principles rather than starting from zero or over-lawyering before friends-and-family money.
- Synthesis notes: Genericised from interview (no vendor name). Pairs with early-revenue c-src041-er-002.
