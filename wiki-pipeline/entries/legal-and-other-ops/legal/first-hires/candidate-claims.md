## Candidate claims from startup legal interview (src-041)

*Promoted to `draft.md` as c-011–c-019 (pending review in approval tool). Audit record of the src-041 synthesis batch.*

### c-src041-fh-001: Written contract for everyone on day one
- Section: what_good_looks_like
- Supporting atoms: atom-172
- Candidate claim: **Everyone who joins — employee or contractor — receives something in writing immediately.** Not "we are all friends, it will never go wrong." For digital companies, contracts include IP, confidentiality, and NL employment basics from day one.
- Synthesis notes: Aligns with published c-006 but emphasises interview's "friends" failure mode. Consider merge vs carry as evolved wording.

### c-src041-fh-002: Employment template ready before first hire
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-173
- Candidate claim: **Have a standard employment contract template ready before your first hire** — from a startup-savvy lawyer or reputable template — and reuse one consistent document stack for every hire.
- Synthesis notes: Overlaps c-006; candidate adds "before first hire" timing and single-stack discipline.

### c-src041-fh-003: Freelancer IP assignment in writing
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-174
- Candidate claim: **Every freelancer who touches product needs a written agreement with explicit IP assignment — platform terms alone are not enough.** Missing contractor IP assignment is among the most common legal DD gaps.
- Synthesis notes: Extends c-006 freelance mention with explicit platform warning from interview.

### c-src041-fh-004: GDPR from minute one for digital products
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-175, atom-179
- Candidate claim: **If you collect personal data, design privacy and GDPR compliance from minute one** — what you collect, why, how it is stored, processor agreements where needed. Do not collect user data for a year and only then write a privacy policy.
- Synthesis notes: Not in published first-hires page. Digital-product qualifier important; flag `conditional` at review for non-data businesses.

### c-src041-fh-005: One generalist legal advisor early on
- Section: what_to_do
- Supporting atoms: atom-176, atom-184
- Candidate claim: **Prefer one startup-savvy advisor or small firm covering employment, commercial, and privacy over many one-off specialists.** Tighten the relationship at concrete triggers: first hire imminent, first term sheet, first large customer contract, or move into regulated/data-sensitive territory.
- Synthesis notes: Advisor selection + when to engage. Complements tools entry.

### c-src041-fh-006: Hire without a contract
- Section: warning_signs
- Warning category: process_entry
- Supporting atoms: atom-178
- Candidate claim: **You hired someone with no employment or contractor contract because you trust the relationship.** When relationships sour or diligence arrives, missing contracts create liability and IP uncertainty that are expensive to fix retroactively.
- Synthesis notes: Interview pull-quote theme. Distinct from hour-registration warnings.

### c-src041-fh-007: Privacy and data collected without lawful basis
- Section: warning_signs
- Warning category: process_entry
- Supporting atoms: atom-179
- Candidate claim: **You have been collecting user data for months without a coherent privacy policy, lawful basis, or technical controls.** Late privacy fixes often require product and architecture changes under time pressure.
- Synthesis notes: Digital-product specific. Consider `geographically_biased` (GDPR/NL-EU).

### c-src041-fh-008: Competitor privacy terms as starting point
- Section: tools_resources
- Supporting atoms: atom-177
- Candidate claim: **Review comparable Dutch or EU competitors' public privacy policies and adapt honestly to what your product actually does** — a reasonable starting point while you refine with counsel.
- Synthesis notes: Pragmatic interview advice. Not a substitute for legal review.

### c-src041-fh-009: Platforms for episodic legal help
- Section: tools_resources
- Supporting atoms: atom-183
- Candidate claim: **For episodic early-stage legal needs, use platforms that match companies with vetted freelance legal specialists** — suitable for one-hour template fixes as well as longer engagements. Retainer not required on day one.
- Synthesis notes: Genericised (no platform names). tool_resource only; no vendor endorsement.
