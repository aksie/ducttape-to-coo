- Reviewed by: ""
- Reviewed date: 2026-05-28
- Edited claim text: **Write down your work location policy before the first hire joins.** Fully remote, office-first, or hybrid with specific expectations — one or two sentences is enough. Share it in the offer letter or onboarding doc. The policy shapes who you can hire and what cultural habits form in the first 90 days. Changing it retroactively after people have joined on different expectations is expensive in trust and sometimes in attrition.

### c-004
- Status: approved_with_edit
- Flags: [conditional]
- Reviewer notes: ""
- Reviewed by: ""
- Reviewed date: 2026-05-28
- Edited claim text: **Assign one person as responsible for the office.** Supplies restocked, lease terms known, single point of contact for the building and any vendors. At 2–10 people this is a few hours a month as a side responsibility, not a job. The cost of not assigning it is the "nobody's job" failure mode: small frictions accumulate invisibly until you try to insert an owner at 20+ people and find the process has no foundation. Offload to someone running the co-working / entire office building and keep employees feeling responsible for things themselves by documenting how to do things and letting them to it themselves, to create the right culture and make sure things are left better than they are found.
