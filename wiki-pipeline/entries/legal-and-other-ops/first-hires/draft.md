# Office & Facilities — First Hires (2–10 people)

> At First Hires, Office & Facilities barely exists as a distinct process. The only things that matter are making an explicit decision about where people work — and making sure one person owns the physical space if you have one.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **You have made a stated decision about where people work.** Remote, in-office, or hybrid — it's written down, shared before the first hire joins, and everyone who came on board did so knowing it. There is no "we'll figure it out later" ambiguity about whether showing up in an office is expected.
  <!-- sources: src-027 (HN: remote vs in-person) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **If you have office space, one named person owns it.** They know when the lease renews, who supplies are ordered from, what the basic safety requirements are, and who the landlord calls. They don't need to manage everything personally — they just make sure nothing falls through a gap.
  <!-- sources: src-027, src-029 (alexrodba.com) -->

## What you actually need to do

<!-- claim-id: c-003 -->
1. **Write down your work location policy before the first hire joins.** Fully remote, office-first, or hybrid with specific expectations — one or two sentences is enough. Share it in the offer letter or onboarding doc. The policy shapes who you can hire and what cultural habits form in the first 90 days. Changing it retroactively after people have joined on different expectations is expensive in trust and sometimes in attrition.
   <!-- sources: src-027 (HN: remote vs in-person), src-028 (HN: colocation data) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-004 -->
2. **Assign one person as responsible for the office.** Supplies restocked, lease terms known, single point of contact for the building and any vendors. At 2–10 people this is a few hours a month as a side responsibility, not a job. The cost of not assigning it is the "nobody's job" failure mode: small frictions accumulate invisibly until you try to insert an owner at 20+ people and find the process has no foundation.
   <!-- sources: src-029 (alexrodba.com) -->

<!-- claim-id: c-005 -->
3. **[NL] From your first employee, complete a RI&E** (risico-inventarisatie en -evaluatie) under the Arbowet. For a small office with ≤25 employees, you can do a simplified version yourself using a recognised branch instrument — no certified safety expert required. A basic office RI&E takes a few hours: document the risks (ergonomics, emergency exit, fire extinguisher, first aid kit), write a brief Plan van Aanpak, and keep it on file. The Dutch Labour Inspectorate can fine you for not having one, and insurers can reduce coverage.
   <!-- sources: src-030 (KVK) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-006 -->
- **You signed a traditional multi-year office lease at 2–10 people.** At this stage, your headcount trajectory is fundamentally uncertain. A 3–5 year lease converts a variable cost into a fixed commitment at exactly the point where optionality matters most. Lease for your current headcount, not your projected one. If you're already in one, model the sublease or exit options now rather than when you next need the runway.
  <!-- sources: src-031 (gable.to, vendor — flagged; warning corroborated across multiple commercial sources with no incentive to fabricate the problem) -->

### Process entry

<!-- claim-id: c-007 -->
- **Nobody on your team can immediately name who to contact when the printer breaks, supplies run out, or a visitor needs building access.** This is the signal that office ownership was never assigned. The fix is one line in your onboarding doc: "[Name] is the person responsible for office logistics."
  <!-- sources: src-029 (alexrodba.com) -->

## How this evolves next

<!-- claim-id: c-008 -->
At Early Revenue (~10–20 people), formalise your remote/hybrid policy — not just as a line in an onboarding doc but as a real decision communicated consistently in hiring. If you have office space, the owner role becomes more than a side responsibility. At Growth (~20–50 people), facilities becomes a real function: lease management, maintenance contracts, visitor and contractor access management, and formal health & safety compliance all need dedicated attention. Build that process then, not now — the First Hires version is just ownership plus the legal minimum.
<!-- sources: src-029 (alexrodba.com), src-031 (gable.to), src-030 (KVK) -->

## Tools & resources

<!-- claim-id: c-009 -->
- **[NL] RI&E for small offices:** Use a recognised branch instrument (branche-RI&E) from your sector association, or the generic kantooromgevingen (office environments) checklist. The KVK website lists recognised instruments. A basic office RI&E covers: ergonomics, emergency exit, fire extinguisher, first aid kit, and a brief Plan van Aanpak.
  <!-- sources: src-030 (KVK) -->

- **Work location policy template:** A one-pager covering location policy (remote/hybrid/in-office), core hours expectations, equipment provided or reimbursed, and how to request exceptions. Keep it in your employee handbook or onboarding Notion doc. The Promise Legal startup handbook template (Section 11) has a customisable version with three policy variants.
