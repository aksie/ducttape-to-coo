# Governance & Corporate — Early Revenue

> The first governance discipline that bites is the annual accounts: adopt them properly and file them on time, because this is where director liability starts.

## What good looks like at this phase

<!-- claim-id: c-001 -->
- **Annual accounts are properly adopted and filed on time.** Adoption is by a shareholders' resolution signed by all shareholders, the accounts are signed by the board, and at minimum the CFO knows the filing status. Doing this correctly and on time is what keeps the filings defensible later.
  <!-- sources: src-061 (practitioner, Trailhead legal) -->

## Warning signs you're behind

### Output quality

<!-- claim-id: c-002 -->
- **Adoption is treated as "filing Thursday, assume approval unless I hear otherwise."** That email is not valid adoption — late or incorrect filing of annual accounts can trigger director liability. A trustee in an insolvency looks back at exactly these filings.
