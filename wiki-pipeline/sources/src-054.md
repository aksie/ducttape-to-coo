## Summary

HN thread on Auth0 layoffs that branches into offboarding practice. Practitioners share first-person stories: developer defaced site after revoke using password saved in personal cloud; marketing employee nearly exfiltrated customer email list when allowed to linger after termination meeting. Also argues hidden tacit knowledge in people's heads is the highest-value offboarding transfer target.

## Notable claims

- Revoke access at termination, but personal copies of credentials outside company systems can still enable harm
- Allowing terminated employees to stay on premises or systems after the meeting creates exfiltration risk
- Most important offboarding work is transferring tacit knowledge (system logins, backup schedules, pressing issues) before it vanishes
- Immediate lockout vs transition window is a risk tradeoff — involuntary departures warrant faster revocation
- Firing via email without conversation is harmful; access removal can still happen at the same time as the conversation
