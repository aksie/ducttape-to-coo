## Summary

Operator essay (ex-Superhuman) documenting 15+ communication principles for startups: effort conservation, channel norms, public-by-default, acknowledgment, and notification sovereignty. Explicit guidance on when to use which channel and what to document. Essay is sponsored by a dictation tool — principles are practitioner-framed, not vendor-prescribed tooling.

## Notable claims

- Mandating 100% Slack communication often drowns individuals despite sounding intentional
- Channel norms must be written in a company wiki/handbook and be required reading for new hires
- Starting responsiveness norms: Slack within ~3 working hours, email within ~1 working day
- Non-sensitive valuable information belongs in public channels with minimal @-tagging — others may ignore
- Sensitive topics (HR, comp, fundraise, credentials) are the minority and stay private
- Valuable DMs should be moved to public channels when non-sensitive
- Recipients should acknowledge receipt quickly (emoji reaction is enough)
- Individuals proactively check channels on a schedule aligned to norms — not purely via notifications
- One emergency channel (call / walk over) exists but is used sparingly
- Independent discovery: look up before asking; improve findability after answering
