## Summary

Practitioner contribution from Stefan Verkerk, with scenario-style planning terminology attributed to operator Nalden. Covers **2.1 Cash Flow** and **2.2 Financial Planning @ first hires through early revenue**. Core model: not parallel static scenarios — **one active budget** and a **list of if/then rules** across multiple dimensions (MRR, customer count, geography, runway). When a trigger fires, execute the pre-agreed action (which changes the budget going forward). Covid mass-cancellation budget cited as one hard row on the list, not the universal method.

EU/NL-friendly examples (fully loaded cost multiplier, etc.). Threshold numbers are illustrative — flag `needs_practitioner_check` at synthesis. Blog draft: `blog/posts/drafts/scenario-style-planning-not-a-static-budget.md`.

## Notable claims

- Annual budget as governance guardrail vs operating shape — real decisions conditional (*when unit economics*, not *launch in April*)
- IFTTT-style: budget + bandwidth + if/then; decide in calm weather, execute under stress
- Two layers: base plan (Sheet) + trigger → action list (not three parallel scenarios)
- Hire when MRR/ARR crosses line — calendar date is forecast not gate
- Trigger list spans MRR hires/cuts, customer count → support hire, geography → payment methods, runway → fundraise prep
- Monthly founder review: triggers close/fired, runway, actual vs plan
- Warning: static calendar hires, triggers only in founders' heads, moral hire debates
- Evolution: habit at 10 people before board-pack theatre at growth
