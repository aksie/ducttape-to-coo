## Summary
Six practitioners share onboarding tactics. Covers: why startups underinvest in onboarding, how to design day one, founder involvement in training, fighting factions through cross-team exposure, and onboarding into your industry not just your company. Mix of early-stage and growth-stage perspectives.

## Notable claims
- Startups spend enormous energy recruiting and then drop the ball the moment someone signs — the bait-and-switch is common and damages trust immediately
- Sloppy onboarding becomes sloppy culture — the signal sent on day one persists
- Day one should be over-scheduled, not under-scheduled — leaving new hires with nothing to do sends the wrong message
- Founders and senior leaders should personally deliver company values and origin story during onboarding, not delegate to HR
- New hires should be introduced to every function before they get buried in their day-to-day work — this prevents factions forming later
- Onboarding into your industry (not just your company) creates better judgment and greater impact
- A "Day One Document" — written culture doc — scales better than verbal transmission and is a ready reference
