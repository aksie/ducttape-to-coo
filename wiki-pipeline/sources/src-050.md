## Summary

HN comment thread on a standard offboarding practice: practitioners with root/admin access describe a mutual pact and employer expectation to revoke all access within the hour of termination — no grace period for email downloads. Emphasizes diligence over politeness and protecting both company and departed employee from later suspicion.

## Notable claims

- Access revocation should be on a standard checklist, not improvised — longer list for people with root
- Cut off completely within the hour; do not defer revocation so someone can download email or files
- Departing practitioners often remind employers what to lock them out of — reduces later blame if systems break
- Consistent lockout protects the ex-employee from suspicion if a breach occurs after they leave
