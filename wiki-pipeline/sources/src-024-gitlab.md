## Summary
In 2019, GitLab's CEO cadence page reframes planning as a system of rhythms. The page lists day, week, month, quarter, year, strategy, vision, and mission as distinct cadences with an approximate 4x timescale relationship. This becomes the bridge from goals and OKRs to an operating calendar that coordinates recurring planning and review work.

## Canonical source decision
This page is the earliest observed cadence source and is the right predecessor to the later company-wide Cadence page. Extraction is limited to the cadence model itself; linked pages and each listed event are not expanded.

## Notable claims
- Everything in a company happens in a cadence.
- Cadences span day, week, month, quarter, year, strategy, vision, and mission.
- Weekly cadence includes 1-1s and startup growth measurement.
- Monthly cadence includes metrics calls, releases, retrospectives, and most KPIs.
- Quarterly cadence includes OKRs, board meetings, sales targets, and E-group offsites.
- Yearly cadence includes the yearly plan and most of Direction.

## What changed since previous era
The 2017 OKR page focused on cascading goals through owners and layers. By 2019, GitLab added a broader temporal operating model around those goals. OKRs became one cadence inside a larger system of weekly, monthly, quarterly, yearly, strategic, vision, and mission rhythms.

## Distinctive quote
"Everything in a company happens in a certain cadence."
