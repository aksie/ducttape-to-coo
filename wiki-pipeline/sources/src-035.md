## Summary

Official guidance from the Dutch Ministry of Social Affairs on the vertrouwenspersoon obligation. At 10+ employees, Dutch employers must designate or contract a confidential advisor who can receive confidential reports of undesirable behaviour (ongewenst gedrag), including sexual harassment, intimidation, aggression, and discrimination. The role can be fulfilled by an internal appointee or an external provider.

## Notable claims

- Required at 10+ employees in the Netherlands
- Must be reachable confidentially and without consequence for the reporter
- External providers (e.g. Perspectief, GIMD) can be contracted for small companies
- Must be documented in the employee handbook or policy documents
- Applies from the moment headcount reaches 10 — not only at larger companies
