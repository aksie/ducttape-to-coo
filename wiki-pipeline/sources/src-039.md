## Summary

Practitioner contribution covering the formal financial record-keeping that companies should maintain from early revenue onwards. Strong emphasis on the management-to-statutory bridge document — described as consistently the most time-consuming item to reconstruct in DD and almost never existing when needed. Also covers internal controls documentation, financial systems inventory, and bank/signatory records.

## Notable claims

- The management-to-statutory bridge is the single most time-consuming DD item to reconstruct, and almost never exists when needed
- Writing the bridge document once per year when preparing annual accounts costs near nothing; reconstructing it under DD pressure costs weeks
- WBSO documentation is a commonly missed DD item that affects both tax DD and R&D credibility
