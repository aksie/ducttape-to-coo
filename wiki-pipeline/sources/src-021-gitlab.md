## Summary
In the 2015 monolithic handbook, company-level planning is not yet a formal public planning system. Strategy is referenced as a separate GitLab Strategy document that team members should read and contribute to, while day-to-day alignment happens through workflow norms: prioritize consciously, pick issues from the current milestone, keep promises, and avoid external commitments without internal agreement. The planning lesson is that early strategy can be a shared reference plus explicit operating heuristics rather than a full OKR process.

## Canonical source decision
This source uses the monolithic team handbook because no separate public strategy, OKR, or cadence page existed in the 2015 snapshot. Extraction is limited to the `General` and `Workflow` sections because they contain the planning-relevant operating model; benefits, security, and PTO are deliberately excluded.

## Notable claims
- Team members are expected to read and contribute to the GitLab Strategy document.
- Work should be consciously prioritized based on urgency, impact, who is waiting, and how many people are affected.
- Issues should be actionable and realistic; low-priority unowned issues should be made available to the community or closed.
- People should pick issues from the current milestone and assign issues to themselves when they start working.
- GitLab should keep promises and avoid external promises without internal agreement.
