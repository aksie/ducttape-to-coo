## Summary
The 2021-2024 company Cadence snapshots show the planning system maturing from an operating calendar into an explicit hierarchy and then into a maintained review rhythm. The 2021 version emphasizes day/week/month/quarter/year/strategy/vision/mission as a visible company calendar. The 2023 and 2024 versions make the hierarchy clearer: mission, vision, strategy, yearlies, and OKRs connect across timescales, and each layer gets reviewed at the cadence below it.

## Canonical source decision
This is a source bundle because the scaled-era lesson depends on comparing the same canonical page across three bounded snapshots. I did not extract from the linked OKR, KPI, FP&A, board, or product direction pages; the cadence page contains enough of the operating model for Company-Level Planning & Goals.

## Notable claims
- Company work is grouped by the timescale it pertains to, not merely when a page is updated.
- Long-term mission, vision, strategy, yearlies, and quarterly OKRs should connect.
- Top cross-functional initiatives typically last a year and should align to yearlies.
- KPIs measure ongoing company performance; quarterly changes to KPIs typically become OKRs.
- Each planning element is reviewed at the cadence one level below it.
- Reviews keep planning elements current and prevent them from becoming stale.

## What changed since previous era
The 2019 CEO cadence page introduced the idea that company work happens in recurring cadences. The later company Cadence page turns that into a more explicit operating architecture: short-term goals ladder up to long-term direction, and review cadences keep the system current. The scaled-era lesson is maintenance: planning artifacts need scheduled review so they do not become stale.

## Distinctive quote
"These reviews ensure that elements reflect current priorities."
