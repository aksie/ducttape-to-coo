## Summary

Official Dutch government guidance on working hours and rest time obligations under the Arbeidstijdenwet. Key rules: max 60 hours/week, max 12 hours/shift, minimum 11 consecutive hours rest between shifts, minimum 36 consecutive hours off per week. Employers must keep objective and accessible time records for all employees. Records must be retained for at least one year.

## Notable claims

- Hour registration is legally required for all Dutch employees, including remote and flexible arrangements
- A 2022 Dutch high court ruling confirmed this applies universally
- Records must be objective, reliable, and accessible to employees (per 2019 CJEU ruling)
- Non-compliance fines can reach €10,000 per employee
- Maximum 60 hours/week, 12 hours/shift, 11 hours rest between shifts
