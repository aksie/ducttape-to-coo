## Summary

As HR admin and operational commercial contract work moves from the CEO or founder to team leads (typically an Ops or HR lead at first hires), two problems appear in parallel. First, leads start signing employment or vendor contracts without the legal right to bind the company — job title is not signing authority. Second, even when motivated and competent, they may sign contracts outside what the company actually wants to commit to, because nobody agreed the boundaries.

The fix is deliberately lightweight: before the first delegated signature, agree in writing which contract types the Ops lead may sign (standard employment contracts from approved templates, freelance agreements under a value threshold, etc.) and which still require the CEO or director. Then put the legal framework in place — in the Netherlands, typically a power of attorney (volmacht) from the formal director or CEO — so delegated signatures are legally valid. File both the matrix and the delegation document in corporate/legal folders alongside other founding documents.

This becomes more important at early revenue when commercial contracts (customers, vendors, partnerships) multiply and more team leads touch external agreements. The same discipline scales: boundaries first, legal instrument second, no assumed authority from role alone.

## Notable claims

- Signing authority must not be assumed from job title when contract work delegates from founder to team leads
- A lightweight contract-type matrix plus formal delegation (power of attorney) is enough at first hires — no need for a full legal ops function
- Contracts signed without proper authority may be invalid and surface as findings in due diligence
- Boundaries matter as much as authority: agree what the Ops lead can sign, not just that someone other than the CEO will sign
