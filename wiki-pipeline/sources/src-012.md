## Summary
Paul Graham's essay arguing that growth rate is the defining metric of a startup and the best compass for decision-making at early stage. Relevant to company-level planning because it argues for using a single growth metric to simplify all prioritisation decisions.

## Notable claims
- Growth rate is the single most important number for a startup founder to know at all times
- Use a weekly growth target as the primary decision-making filter — if it helps hit the target, do it; if not, deprioritise it
- If you're getting a constant number of new customers per month, your growth rate is actually declining
- Focusing on one number reduces the complexity of startup decision-making to a tractable problem
- Startups that grow slowly tend not to grow as big — the slope of the growth curve matters more than the absolute size
