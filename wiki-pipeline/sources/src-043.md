## Summary

HN discussion on Slack misuse and company culture. Practitioners argue Slack amplifies existing norms — instant-response expectations come from the company, not the tool. Useful heuristics: Slack works when channels map to collaborating teams; cross-department or formal team-wide messages belong in email or scheduled calls. Chat-derived information often needs rewriting into durable docs.

## Notable claims

- Slack without norms amplifies the worst parts of company culture; moderated use can be productive
- Instant-response expectation is a company policy problem, not inherent to Slack
- Slack works best when channels align with teams actively working together
- Cross-department or formal whole-team communication fits email or scheduled calls better than Slack
- Slack shines for up-to-the-minute collaborative problem-solving, in moderation
- Email suits mindful, slower conversations; chat creates synchronous pressure
- Dedicated #support- channels with rotation and SLA beat ad-hoc pings in team channels
- Information born in chat usually needs to move to a document or ticket to persist
