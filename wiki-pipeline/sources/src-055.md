## Summary

HN thread on government DB wipe after firing. Includes practitioner who had just finished onboarding and used their fresh knowledge to live-test an offboarding checklist on their own accounts before executing it for a departing employee. European commenters note notice-period offboarding is common in good-faith partings; immediate credential kill for high-risk unilateral access.

## Notable claims

- Build a documented offboarding checklist of every service; live-test revocation timing on your own account before you need it
- High-risk credentials (unilateral DB admin) should terminate immediately; not every credential is high-risk
- Good-faith voluntary partings may allow weeks of handover; contentious involuntary departures need faster lockout
- If every employee credential is treated as high-risk, that signals broader security architecture failure
- Offboarding centers (structured transition spaces) are a middle ground but require long-term maintenance
