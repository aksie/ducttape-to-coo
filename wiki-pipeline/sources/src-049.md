## Summary

When CS and engineering split without agreed handoff norms, teams argue about whether CS is "@-mentioning engineers into oblivion" or engineers are "ignoring customers." Practitioner experience: both complaints reflect real tradeoffs; there is no one-size-fits-all channel map, but companies are behind when they leave the conflict as morale instead of facilitating a short joint decision on channels, tickets, mentions, and response windows.

## Notable claims

- Cross-team Slack wars at first hires often mean handoff norms were never decided — not that one function is dysfunctional
- Engineering ("we never get deep work done") and CS ("customers suffer") complaints can both be true simultaneously
- The fix is a facilitated norm decision both teams accept — specific channel/ticket/SLA choices are context-dependent and not prescriptive here
- Leaving the fight unresolved recurs every few months as headcount grows
