# F2F #58: When to hire the first operations profile

First-person practitioner blog post. Author co-founded a remote company and shares their experience hiring an office manager (their term at the time) at ~10 people. Key points:

- They hired their first operations hire ("office manager") when the team hit 10 people.
- Even though the company had no physical office, the role was still needed to handle administrative, HR, and ops tasks that had previously fallen on the founders.
- The title "office manager" was used even though the company was remote — the function is "operational glue," not specifically facilities management.
- At ~20 people they hired a Project Manager (second ops person); at ~30 people a second People Ops person.
- Lesson: the need for an ops person at ~10 people is real regardless of whether you have a physical office. The function is whoever handles "everything else."
