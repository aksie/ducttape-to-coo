# Ask HN: Should a startup start fully-remote or in-person?

HN Ask thread with substantive practitioner commentary on the remote/office decision for early-stage startups. Key practitioner views:

- At very early stage (2-5 people), in-person colocation supports higher-iteration collaboration and fast decision-making.
- The decision is not binary: "start remote, but local to each other to get together as needed" is a viable middle path.
- Communication style and skills determine how well the team functions remotely; not all teams are equally suited.
- The remote/office decision shapes the cultural DNA of the company in ways that are hard to change later.
- The decision has a direct effect on who you can hire.
- For solo founders or co-founders in different cities, remote is the only practical option regardless of preference.
