## Summary
Practitioner-oriented guide to what a startup financial model needs to contain and when to build it properly. Argues that most founders build models only for fundraising rather than for operational decision-making.

## Notable claims
- A financial model is a decision-making tool, not a fundraising document
- Rolling 13-week cash flow forecast is the operational heartbeat at early scale
- Variance analysis (actual vs forecast) matters more than the forecast itself
- You need separate models for headcount planning and cash flow — conflating them causes errors
- Department heads should own their own budget lines by Series A
