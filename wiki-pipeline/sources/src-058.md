## Summary

First-person practitioner guidance: founders defer reporting until they have shareholders, but the discipline matters more than the audience. Monthly written lookback against stated goals forces honesty that verbal investor coffees cannot — you cannot repeat "feature goes live in six weeks" for three months without confronting the gap in writing. When angels arrive, widen distribution on an existing habit; don't invent the process under fundraising pressure.

## Notable claims

- Start monthly reports before investors — send to self, co-founders, or leadership team only
- Without written goal scoring you can kid yourself; meetings stay optimistic, nothing documents what went differently than expected
- Document the delta and the lesson — everything goes differently than planned; performing confidence in updates is not the point
- Quarterly runway/scenario depth can wait; monthly lookback/lookahead against goals is minimum viable
- Pairs with quarterly goals doc — goals set targets, monthly update scores them
- When you raise, you widen distribution on an existing habit rather than building reporting from scratch
