## Summary
GitLab's detailed remote onboarding guide. Heavy on structure and process — most applicable at Growth/Scaled phase. Useful for what good looks like structurally: what should be ready before day one, what happens in week one, how to assign a buddy. gitlab_scale_bias throughout.

## Notable claims
- Everything should be ready before day one: laptop, accounts, access — waiting until the new hire arrives to set these up is a failure
- Assign an onboarding buddy who is not the new hire's manager — a peer-level guide removes the power dynamic and makes asking questions easier
- First week should be structured with explicit goals, not left to the new hire to figure out
- Document the onboarding process so it doesn't live in one person's head — the person who last ran onboarding shouldn't be the only one who can run it next time
- 30-day check-in should be a standing ritual — ask: what's going well, what's confusing, what do you wish you'd known earlier
