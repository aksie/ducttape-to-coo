## Summary

Basecamp's public employee handbook is one of the best practitioner references for small companies. Written in a direct, non-corporate tone. Covers time off, benefits, code of conduct, how the company works, and communication norms. At ~14 chapters it is significantly more concise than GitLab (2,200+ pages) while still covering the essential ground. Good model for "complete but readable."

## Notable claims

- A clear, short handbook is more likely to be read than a comprehensive one
- Basecamp explicitly documents time-off philosophy (rest is a feature, not a bug) rather than just the mechanics
- Handbook as a trust document: publishing it externally signals culture and attracts the right candidates
- Small teams benefit from a handbook that fits in one document
