## Summary
In this snapshot, expense guidance lives inside the monolithic GitLab Team Handbook under the broader "Benefits" section. The policy is short, founder-voiced, and principle-first: team members are trusted to spend in the company's interest without prior permission, with only a few lightweight reporting and reimbursement rules around the edges. The eligible expense list is concrete and practical, focused on enabling remote work rather than controlling every spend category.

## Notable claims
- Team members should spend company money as if it were their own money, with the added context that company expenses are not taxed.
- Team members do not need permission before purchases made in the interest of the company.
- When uncertain, team members should inform their manager before or after the purchase.
- Expense reports are due seven days after the end of the calendar quarter.
- Personal expenses accidentally paid with a company card must be reported quickly and refunded within 14 days.
- GitLab will pay for practical work-enabling equipment and services when needed for work and allowed by local law.
