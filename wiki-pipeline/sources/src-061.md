## Summary

Practitioner walkthrough of a structured legal scan, surfacing the target end-state for a growth-stage (Series A/B), non-regulated NL company across seven domains. The strongest, most reusable lessons concern governance discipline (board minutes, reserved matters, the internal authority/signing matrix and its KvK/derdenwerking dimension), employment-contract hygiene (lawyer-reviewed templates, employee IP assignment, addenda reconciliation), commercial-contract triggers and registers, IP posture (trademark over patent for most), and privacy/AI controls (access controls + awareness training, DPAs, AI Act high-risk triggers).

The scan is explicitly a conversation starter, not a verdict — its value comes from filling it in with the CEO/CFO and reading their risk appetite. Regulated industries are out of scope. Several governance lessons tie back to existing wiki work on signing authority (4.1) and salary addenda (3.1).

## Notable claims

- Board decisions and company direction must be documented (minutes / action list) — the bad-weather trustee test.
- An internal authority matrix (who may sign what, thresholds by area + amount, approved by resolution) spreads accountability off the statutory directors; register a volmacht with KvK for third-party effect.
- Late or incorrect filing of adopted annual accounts triggers director liability; "assume approval unless I object" is not valid adoption.
- One employment contract should be lawyer-reviewed early — key early hires are locked into the first, often flawed, contract and are hard to re-tighten later; employee IP assignment is the clause most often unsafe.
- Store side/different terms as addenda beside the employment contracts so a DD reconciles headcount cost to contracts.
- Material commercial contracts go through counsel before signing; a trigger flags non-standard contracts (threshold/strategic value); keep a central contract register (parties, expiry, change-of-control).
- For most companies trademark protection matters more than patents; an unprotected easily-copied brand is a DD red flag.
- Privacy needs internal access controls plus awareness training (human error drives big leaks; cyber insurers require it); DPAs with every processor; high-risk AI (e.g. CV auto-screening) triggers strict AI Act rules → consult an expert.
