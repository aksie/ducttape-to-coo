## Summary
Lenny Rachitsky (Airbnb) and Nels Gilbreth (Eventbrite) describe the "W Framework" for company planning: leadership provides context → teams propose plans → leadership integrates → teams confirm buy-in. Written for companies of ~25+ people. Has series_a_plus_bias but the structural insights about roles in planning apply earlier.

## Notable claims
- The root cause of bad planning is unclear roles — who is responsible for what, when
- Good planning requires top-down context first; teams cannot plan well in a vacuum
- 60% of objectives should come bottom-up from teams, not top-down from leadership
- Leadership should share what they don't know as well as what they do — this invites better plans from teams
- Focus on 3-5 strategic bets maximum; saying yes to too many things ensures none get enough resources
- When you leave planning wondering if you put too many resources behind a single bet, that's the bet that succeeds
- Teams should present their plans to leadership in person before leadership integrates — prevents premature conclusions
- The final plan should be shared company-wide with genuine excitement, not just distributed as a document
