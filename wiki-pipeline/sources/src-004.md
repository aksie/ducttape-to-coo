## Summary
Journalism piece interviewing multiple CFOs about the most common financial mistakes they see when joining or advising early-stage companies. No product to sell; relatively neutral framing.

## Notable claims
- Mixing personal and business finances persists longer than it should — still common at 20-person companies
- Expense categorisation inconsistency makes historical data useless for forecasting
- Companies often discover their unit economics are broken only when they run a proper cohort analysis for the first time
- Founders frequently don't realise cash and profit are different things until a cash crisis hits
- Deferred revenue is often mis-classified, inflating cash position perception
