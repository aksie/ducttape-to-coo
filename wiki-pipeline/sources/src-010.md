## Summary
Swipely CEO Angus Davis describes how he implemented OKRs as the company grew from 30 to 80 employees. Focuses on the practical mechanics: how to run OKR reviews, how to prevent gaming, and why goals should not be tied to performance reviews. Written from direct CEO experience at the 30-80 person stage.

## Notable claims
- OKRs are a communication tool, not a performance management tool — decoupling them from compensation prevents sandbagging
- 70% achievement is the right target — anything higher means goals weren't ambitious enough
- 60% of a company's OKRs should be defined bottom-up by employees, not dictated by leadership
- Weekly check-ins on OKRs (not just quarterly) are what make the system work
- Public goals force people to ask for help and spot where they can support each other
- All-hands at the start of every quarter should be devoted to reviewing past OKRs and setting new ones
- OKRs should include skill development objectives, not just output metrics
