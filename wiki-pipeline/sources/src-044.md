## Summary

37signals/Basecamp's public communication philosophy and day-to-day practices. Async-first: long-form writing over meetings, spatial context (discuss on the artifact), automatic check-ins, and rare synchronous calls. They use one internal tool (Basecamp) instead of Slack+email — principles transfer to any stack. Written for a ~small/medium remote product company, not enterprise scale.

## Notable claims

- Real-time sometimes, asynchronous most of the time
- Writing solidifies, chat dissolves — substantial decisions start and end in complete written thoughts
- Meetings are the last resort, not the first option
- Never expect immediate response unless true emergency — immediate-response culture is toxic
- Discuss on the thing being discussed (task comments, doc comments) for spatial context
- Policy and official announcements go to a durable, company-visible record — not email alone
- Automatic daily/weekly check-ins create predictable transparency without meetings
- Communication shouldn't require calendar synchronization
- Poor communication creates more work; miscommunication scales with headcount
