## Summary

Hiring control belongs in **Recruitment & Hiring (3.2)**, with cross-links to legal signing authority (4.1) and HR templates (3.1 / 4.5) — not a separate legal process.

The chain: confirm budget and management agreement on the role (with indicative salary range by level: junior, senior, manager, VP, executive) **before** posting; CFO or COO approval **before** sending an offer letter within that band; labour contract sent only from the agreed template by head of HR or CFO/COO; signed contract to HR before start date for payroll and insurance.

Observed failures: job posts without approved budget; candidate "hired" via self-edited contract with non-agreed salary; signed by someone without authority; contract never reached HR/finance — employee not on payroll or insured until remediated.

## Notable claims

- No job opening should go public without budget check and management agreement on role + indicative salary band
- Offer letters require CFO or COO sign-off that the amount fits the approved band
- Labour contracts must come from the standard template, issued by authorised HR or finance — not manager-edited one-offs
- Signed contracts must reach HR before start date or payroll and insurance lag creates compliance and people risk
- Rogue hires surface in due diligence as authority, compensation, and insurance gaps
