# The Startup Office Setup Guide: From Series A to Series C — Gable

Vendor blog post (Gable sells workplace management SaaS). Despite bias, contains useful data-backed observations on common mistakes. Key points:

- The trigger to move from coworking to dedicated office is typically 12–20 employees, around Series A. Below this, coworking friction is manageable.
- **Most common mistake at Series A: over-leasing.** Founders project headcount optimistically and sign leases for space they don't fill for 12+ months.
- "Skip written policies because the team is still small" is identified as a common mistake — by ~employee 15, new hires are guessing at unwritten rules.
- 75% of traditional leases lock in for 3-5 years; dangerous at a stage where headcount could double or be forced to shrink.
- Lease for current headcount +20%, not projected headcount +20%.

Bias note: Gable sells software to manage hybrid workplaces. Claims about the need for "workplace technology" should be discounted. Data on lease timing and over-leasing is not product-adjacent and is corroborated by non-vendor sources.
