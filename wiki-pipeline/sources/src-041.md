## Summary

Practitioner contribution based on an interview with an anonymous startup legal advisor (Netherlands/EU). Covers the balance between over- and under-legalising across pre-traction co-founder work, incorporation, friends-family-and-fools funding, first hire, and ongoing commercial relationships. Strong themes: beer-napkin co-founder agreements before traction; incorporate and transfer IP when momentum appears; template-grade founder docs at first external money; employment and privacy from day one for digital products; quarterly legal/ops check-ins instead of panic or denial. Valuable for legal-ops wiki cells at foundation, first-hires, and early-revenue where practitioner coverage has been thin.

## Notable claims

- Co-founders should agree basics on paper lightly before traction, but not spend thousands on full shareholder agreements pre-PMF
- Incorporate when users, revenue, or serious investor interest appear — and transfer all IP into the entity at setup
- First external investor will not accept "we have nothing on paper" — template founder terms and documented principles are enough early on
- Every hire and product-touching freelancer needs a written contract with IP assignment from day one
- Digital startups should treat GDPR and data architecture seriously from minute one, not after a year of collecting data
- Quarterly legal/ops review prevents founders oscillating between blind panic and "we're all friends"
- Biggest failures: legal work instead of building; nothing on paper when money/people show up; IP outside company; privacy/tech mess blocking investment; exotic structures from networking advice
