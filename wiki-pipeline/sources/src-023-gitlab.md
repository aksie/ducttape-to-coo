## Summary
In 2017, GitLab introduced a public OKR page with instructions and a concrete quarterly OKR tree. The operating model is hierarchical: every organizational layer has an objective similar to the key results above it, OKRs are prefaced with an owner, and lower-level matches do not need to be perfect. This is a shift from strategy-as-document to goals-as-cascade.

## Canonical source decision
This page is the canonical OKR source for the era. Extraction uses the instructions and a small number of representative examples only; the full OKR tree is deliberately not extracted.

## Notable claims
- GitLab has no more than five layers in its team structure.
- Every layer in the organization has an objective similar to the key results above it.
- OKRs are prefaced with the owner of the key result.
- The match between a lower-level key result and the higher-level key result does not have to be perfect.
- Company-level quarterly OKRs include revenue growth, predictable pipeline, product reliability, and usage goals.

## What changed since previous era
The 2016 strategy page listed company direction and functional annual goals. By 2017, GitLab added a mechanism for translating that direction into quarterly owned work across layers. Planning became less about publishing direction and more about connecting objectives to responsible owners.

## Distinctive quote
"Every layer in the organization has an objective."
