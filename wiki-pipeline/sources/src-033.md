## Summary

A curated research note covering the employee handbook content obligations that apply from the moment a Dutch startup makes its first hire. Strongest on NL-specific legal requirements: hour registration (Arbeidstijdenwet), sick leave obligations (Wet verbetering poortwachter), and the vertrouwenspersoon requirement at 10+ employees. Also covers working hours documentation, leave entitlements, office address, emergency contacts, and expense policy.

Secondary value: analysis of public handbook examples (GitLab, Basecamp, Remote.com) as structural references.

## Notable claims

- Dutch employers must track working hours for all employees including remote/flex — fines up to €10k per employee
- Dutch sick leave entitles employees to up to 2 years of continued pay; WVP milestones must be actively managed
- Vertrouwenspersoon (confidential advisor) is legally required at 10+ employees in NL
- Annual leave minimum: 20 days (most companies offer 25)
- Partner leave since 2022: 5 days fully paid + 5 weeks at 70% via UWV
- Parental leave: 9 weeks at 70% via UWV (since August 2022)
- Working hours must be explicitly documented even for remote/flexible teams
- GitLab's distinction between "time off" and "leave" is a useful structural model
- Office addresses (visiting, registered, postal) must be documented from day one
- Emergency contacts should be collected during onboarding and stored in HR records
