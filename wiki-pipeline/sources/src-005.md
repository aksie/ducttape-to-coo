## Summary
Hacker News thread with founders and operators sharing what financial processes they wished they had established earlier. Mix of bootstrapped and VC-backed perspectives. High practitioner density.

## Notable claims
- Multiple commenters: not having a month-end close ritual meant books were always 2-3 months behind
- Setting up proper expense categories from the start saves enormous pain later — retroactive recategorisation is brutal
- One commenter: "we didn't know our burn rate to within 20% until Series A — that's terrifying in retrospect"
- Several bootstrapped founders: weekly cash position review (not monthly) is the right cadence when runway is under 18 months
- Separating operating expenses from COGS early lets you see gross margin trend without a big analytical exercise later
