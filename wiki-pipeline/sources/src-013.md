## Summary
GitLab's internal OKR guidance, written for a large all-remote organisation. Most relevant at Growth/Scaled phase due to gitlab_scale_bias, but the mechanics of OKR structure, scoring, and cadence are applicable earlier. Strong on the "why" behind OKR design choices.

## Notable claims
- OKRs should be set quarterly with a clear scoring method — 0.0 to 1.0 where 0.7 is a "good" score
- Key results must be measurable and binary-gradable, not subjective
- OKRs are not a laundry list — if everything is a priority, nothing is
- Objectives should be inspirational; key results should be operational
- The CEO sets company OKRs first; all other OKRs cascade from and align to those
- Regular check-ins (monthly or more frequent) prevent OKRs from becoming a once-a-quarter exercise
