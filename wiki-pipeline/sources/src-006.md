## Summary
Reddit thread from someone who ran finance at a 15-25 person startup and shared lessons learned. First-person practitioner account. Bootstrapped context primarily.

## Notable claims
- Monthly P&L review with the founding team changed decision-making quality more than any other single change
- The first time you do a proper budget vs. actuals review, you will find at least one significant line item that has been wrong for months
- AP backlog is invisible until it causes a payment crisis — a simple tracker prevents it
- Getting a bookkeeper earlier than you think you need one pays for itself within 3 months in founder time saved
- Payroll timing errors are common and completely avoidable with a simple calendar reminder system
