## Summary

Practitioner contribution from an operator with M&A experience on both the buy side and sell side. Covers the recommended filing structure for legal and corporate documents (corporate docs, contracts, IP, insurance, compliance), the compliance calendar concept, and the GDPR/privacy filing. Includes a first-person anecdote about discovering filing gaps during a fundraising round even after the contributor believed they were well-prepared.

Key insight: the cost of maintaining this structure year-round is minimal; the cost of not having it at deal time is significant — both in preparation time and in negotiating position.

## Notable claims

- Bad first impressions in a data room have disproportionate impact on how buyers view everything else
- Self-assessment of DD readiness is unreliable — external review before opening the data room catches gaps the founder misses
- Disorganisation signals risk even when the underlying issues are minor, leading to lower price or more reps & warranties
- The filing structure should be established from day one, not assembled when a deal is imminent
- Compliance calendar prevents documents from lapsing (trademarks, domains, insurance renewals)
