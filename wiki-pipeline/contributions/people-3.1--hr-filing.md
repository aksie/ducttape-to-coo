## What does good look like?

When HR filing is working well, every employee has a complete contract on file — signed, in a known location, accessible within two minutes. The org chart is current. Freelancers and contractors have signed IP assignment agreements. There are no employment disputes you've forgotten about.

Good also looks like: standard templates for offer letters and freelance agreements that have been reviewed by a lawyer at least once, updated when the law changes, and used consistently.

## What should you actually do?

**Employment contracts:**
- Every employee: signed contract filed in `/hr/employment-contracts/[name]/`
- Start with key employees — if you have 20 people and only 10 contracts are on file, start with the highest-paid and longest-tenured
- Standard appendices: non-compete clause, non-solicitation clause, confidentiality agreement, IP assignment — these should be in the standard contract, not optional add-ons
- Keep a signed copy and note any amendments (salary changes, role changes) in the same folder

**Freelance / contractor agreements:**
- Every freelancer/contractor who touches code, content, or creative output needs a signed agreement with explicit IP assignment language
- "They're on a platform" (Fiverr, Upwork, etc.) is not a substitute — the platform's standard terms may not give you full IP ownership
- File in `/hr/freelance-contracts/[name]/`
- Keep a standard template that's been reviewed by a lawyer

**Templates:**
- Standard offer letter template (arbeidsovereenkomst template)
- Standard freelance agreement template
- Store in `/hr/templates/` — versioned, with review date noted

**HR metrics (light version to maintain):**
- Headcount by role/department — update whenever someone joins or leaves
- Attrition: keep a simple log of who left and why (voluntary/involuntary)
- Sick leave % — available from payroll system
- Disputes and settlement agreements: list with one-liner summary and link to signed PDFs

**Works council / confidential advisory:**
- If you have 50+ employees: works council (ondernemingsraad) obligations apply — document consultation steps and decisions
- From first hire: confidential counsellor (vertrouwenspersoon) — either internal or external — is a legal obligation under the Working Conditions Act (Arbowet) for companies with employees. Document who this is and that it's been communicated.
- Overzicht vertrouwenscommissie: who the external confidential counsellor is, cadence of check-ins, confirmation of no current or recent situations

## How does this change as you grow?

At first hires, one person manages everything and the main risk is contracts ending up in email rather than a central location. Designate one owner.

At early revenue (~15-25 people), the volume makes informal management impossible. You need a system — even a simple shared drive structure with a naming convention is far better than ad hoc.

At growth, an HR system (AFAS, Personio, or similar) typically takes over contract management. The transition from folder-based to system-based filing needs to be clean — don't maintain both in parallel without a clear "source of truth" designation.

At this stage, HR due diligence in a transaction is a significant workload. Buyers want to see: org chart with compensation, key person risk assessment, all employment contracts, all dispute history, collective agreement status, works council documentation.

## Tools and resources

- Personio or AFAS (NL common HR systems for 20-100 people)
- Human Wave / Nmbrs (payroll — often also stores contracts)
- A shared drive folder structure as described above (Google Drive or SharePoint)
- Arbeidsrechtadvocaat / NVP (for reviewing standard templates)
- Arboportaal.nl (for Arbowet compliance requirements including vertrouwenspersoon)

## Anything else?

Geographic note: Dutch employment law context throughout. Key NL-specific items:
- Vertrouwenspersoon obligation under Arbowet (from first employee)
- Ondernemingsraad (works council) from 50 employees
- CAO applicability — if a sector collective agreement applies, document it and note any deviations in individual contracts
- Non-compete clauses have specific enforceability requirements in NL that differ from other jurisdictions
