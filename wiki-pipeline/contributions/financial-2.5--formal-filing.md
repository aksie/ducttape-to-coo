## What does good look like?

When financial administration is working well, the formal financial record exactly matches what you can explain — and you can explain the differences between what's in the statutory accounts and what's in your management reporting. Auditors sign off without surprises. Tax filings are complete and on time. Every year produces a clean, filed annual account that lives in a known place.

Good also looks like: a one-page document that bridges your management financials to your statutory financials — explaining revenue recognition differences, EBITDA adjustments, and any non-recurring items. This document saves enormous time in a due diligence process.

## What should you actually do?

**Annual accounts:**
- Statutory annual accounts (jaarrekening) signed off by the AGM and filed with KvK within the legal deadline
- Audited accounts where required (typically from ~€6M revenue or earlier if investors require it)
- Store as signed PDFs: `/finance/annual-accounts/[year]-annual-accounts.pdf`
- Keep last 5 years accessible

**Tax filings:**
- Corporate income tax (VPB): filed return + any correspondence with Belastingdienst
- VAT (BTW): quarterly or monthly returns
- Payroll tax (loonheffing): monthly declarations
- R&D tax credit (WBSO): applications and decisions — these are valuable in a DD process as they demonstrate R&D activity
- Store in `/finance/tax/vpb/`, `/finance/tax/btw/`, `/finance/tax/loonheffing/`, `/compliance/grants-and-subsidies/wbso/`

**Management reporting bridge:**
- Maintain a one-page (or one-tab) document that explains:
  - Revenue recognition differences (e.g., deferred revenue in statutory vs. recognized in management accounts)
  - EBITDA reconciliation: what adjustments are made and why
  - Any non-recurring items and how they're treated
  - Why specific line items differ between the two views
- This document is almost always requested in DD and almost never exists — having it saves 2-4 weeks of scrambling

**Financial systems documentation:**
- One paragraph per tool describing what it does: ERP (Exact), billing (Zenvoices or similar), payroll (Human Wave or similar), VAT compliance for cross-border (MOSS/OSS if applicable)
- Document internal controls: who does what in finance, four-eyes principle (dual authorization for payments above threshold), authorized signatory list
- Keep bank account list with authorized signatories and access credentials stored securely

## How does this change as you grow?

At early revenue, the main task is ensuring annual accounts are filed and the basic tax compliance calendar is maintained. Management reporting exists but may not be formally bridged to statutory.

At growth, the bridge document becomes critical — management reporting is more sophisticated, statutory accounts are more complex, and the gap between the two is larger and harder to explain without preparation. An audit trail of management reporting (all monthly reports, archived) becomes expected by investors and buyers.

At scaled, a controller or CFO owns this entire area. The key transition is from founder-managed to professionally managed financial administration with formal internal controls sign-off.

## Tools and resources

- Exact (NL standard ERP for SME)
- Zenvoices (invoice processing automation)
- Human Wave / AFAS / Nmbrs (payroll)
- MOSS/OSS portal (for VAT on digital services sold cross-border in EU)
- Belastingdienst portal — ensure multiple people have access, not just the bookkeeper
- A simple compliance calendar in Google Sheets: filing deadlines, renewal dates, audit schedule

## Anything else?

Geographic note: Dutch BV context throughout. Key deadlines differ from other jurisdictions. The WBSO scheme is NL-specific but equivalent R&D credit schemes exist in most EU countries.

The "management reporting bridge" is consistently the most time-consuming item to reconstruct in a DD process. It's also the easiest to maintain year-round if you simply write it down each time you prepare the annual accounts.
