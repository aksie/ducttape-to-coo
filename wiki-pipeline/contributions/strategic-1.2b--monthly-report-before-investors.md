## What breaks or goes wrong?

Founders defer any written reporting until they have external investors. In the meantime they stay "aligned" through informal channels — coffee with angels, Slack with co-founders, verbal standups. That works until it doesn't: you can tell the same investor "feature goes live in six weeks" across three coffees spanning twelve weeks without ever confronting the slip in writing.

Without a monthly written score against goals, you can kid yourself. Meetings feel productive; narrative stays optimistic; nothing forces you to name what went differently than expected or what you actually learned.

## What should you actually do?

Start a **monthly report before you have investors** — send it to yourself, co-founders, or the leadership team only. Use the same structure you will use for shareholders later (highlights, misses, lookback checkboxes, lookahead). The audience is secondary; the discipline is primary.

When you do raise or add angels, you are not inventing a new process under pressure — you are widening distribution on a habit that already keeps you honest.

## Anything else?

- Everything goes differently than expected — that is normal. The value is **documenting** the delta and the lesson, not performing confidence.
- Quarterly depth (runway, scenarios) can wait; monthly lookback/lookahead against stated goals is the minimum viable habit.
- Pairs naturally with the [quarterly goals template](../../../templates/docs/quarterly-goals.md) — goals set the targets; the monthly update scores them.
