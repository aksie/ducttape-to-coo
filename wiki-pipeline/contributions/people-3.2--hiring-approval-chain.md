## Process placement

**Home: 3.2 Recruitment & Hiring** — budget → approved role → job post → interviews → offer approval → contract issuance.

**Cross-links (not separate legal process):**

- **4.1 Governance & Corporate** — who has signing authority (power of attorney, contract-type matrix)
- **3.1 HR Administration** — lawyer-reviewed templates, payroll filing, insurance activation
- **4.5 Internal & Labour** — employment contract template discipline

## What does good look like?

Before any job opening goes live, management agrees the role is in budget and records an indicative salary range by level (junior, senior, manager, VP, executive). No public post without that sign-off.

Before an offer letter goes out, CFO or COO confirms the specific amount fits the approved band. The labour contract is sent only from the agreed template — by head of HR or CFO/COO — not edited ad hoc by a hiring manager. The signed contract reaches HR before the start date so payroll and insurance are live on day one.

## What should you actually do?

**Approval chain:**

1. **Budget check** — role is in the headcount / cost plan (coordinate with finance).
2. **Management agreement** — leadership agrees this is the job opening you will spend budget on; capture title, level, and indicative salary range.
3. **Post the job** — only after steps 1–2 are documented (email, headcount sheet, or hiring tracker row).
4. **Offer letter** — CFO or COO OK on the amount within the approved band before it is sent.
5. **Labour contract** — head of HR or CFO/COO issues the standard template; no self-edited versions; signer must have documented authority per 4.1.
6. **HR handoff** — signed contract to HR/CFO/COO before start date → payroll and insurance.

## What went wrong (practitioner lesson)

- Job openings were posted **without approved budget** — spend and headcount drifted.
- A candidate was treated as "hired" after receiving a **self-edited contract** with a **salary that was never agreed** in the approval chain.
- The contract was signed by someone **without signing authority**.
- The signed contract was **never submitted to HR, CFO, or COO** — the person was **not on payroll or insured** until the gap was discovered and fixed retroactively.

## Anything else?

- This is hiring ops, not a fourth legal process — legal enters at **signing authority** (4.1) and **template discipline** (3.1 / 4.5).
- Tighten the chain at **early revenue** when multiple managers hire in parallel; at **first hires**, a lightweight founder/CFO OK before post and offer is enough.
