# 📋 Due diligence checklist — [Transaction / round]

Master checklist of what buyers and investors typically request, with expected format and where to store each item in your data room. Adapt sections to your business model and jurisdiction.

## 1. Financial due diligence

### Financial statements & reporting

Prior years revenue, costs & P&L — summarised in a presentation with supporting documentation organised as follows:

- **Formal documentation**
  - Audited financial statements (last 3 years) — all PDFs (annual accounts signed off by the General Meeting of Shareholders) → [data room path]()
  - Tax filings (last 5 years), including R&D tax credits and incentives, tax contingencies and disputes, sales tax / VAT compliance across jurisdictions — all PDFs → [data room path]()

- **Bridge from formal to management financials**
  - Explanation of revenue recognition policies (especially for subscription, advertising, licensing) and EBITDA reconciliation and adjustments — one-pager (differences between statutory reporting and operational practice: how the numbers are built up and why figures differ) → [link]()
  - Bridge from statutory financials to management financials — one-pager or Excel with management financials → [link]()

- **Management reporting** (current year / last 18 months, links to earlier periods where available)
  - In whatever format is available — Excel or Google Sheets (all monthly management reports) → [link]()

- **Current status**
  - P&L: unaudited interim financials (current year) — Excel or Google Sheets → [link]()
  - Balance sheet:
    - Working capital and receivables / payables — ability to take a spot check of each; link to bank login; links to contracts for receivables / payables where applicable → [link]()
    - Debt & liabilities — list with 5-line summary and links to documentation → [link]()
  - Forecast, sensitivities & key risks — one-pager maximum → [link]()

---

### Revenue breakdown

- Revenue breakdown by stream (subscriptions, advertising, content licensing, syndication) — management Excel / Google Sheets (high-level chart, 3 years back) → [link]()
- Churn rates and customer lifetime value (CLV), cohort analysis by customer acquisition period (churn, CAC and LTV), geographic revenue segmentation ([region A] vs. [region B]) → [link]()
- Revenue growth trends and seasonality — same format, 3 years back → [link]()
- Pricing models and changes over time — timeline in Excel / Google Sheets, 3 years back → [link]()
- Business model risks — one-pager
  - Subscriber acquisition cost trends
  - Churn rate acceleration
  - Other key risks → [link]()

---

### Material contracts

- Material contracts — start with the top 10 customers and suppliers; further detail depending on size per customer / supplier and percentage of costs / revenue

### Litigation & disputes
